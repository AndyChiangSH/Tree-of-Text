The San Antonio Spurs defeated the visiting Sacramento Kings, 98 - 85, at AT & T Center on Monday evening. The Kings got out to a good start on the road, as they jumped ahead 60 and went on to outscore the Spurs, 25 - 17, in the first quarter. The Spurs picked up their game in the second quarter but the Kings kept their advantage all the way to halftime, where they led 50 - 43. The Kings stayed on top to start the third and even held a double - digit lead for most of the quarter. However, the Spurs closed the quarter on a 12 - 6 run that cut the deficit to six, as they trailed, 66 - 60, heading into the fourth. The Spurs kept up the momentum going in the fourth and took their first lead of the half. The Kings fought back, but the Spurs shut it down over the final five minutes, where they outscored the Kings 16 - 6, to seal the home win and clinch a playoff spot for the 21st consecutive season. Rudy Gay led the Spurs with 18 points on 5 - of - 9 shooting, six rebounds, one assist, one steal and one block, in 21 minutes off the bench. Manu Ginobili followed up with 17 points on 5 - of - 11 shooting, six rebounds, five assists and one steal, in 27 minutes off the bench. LaMarcus Aldridge tallied 15 points, 14 rebounds, two assists and two blocks, in 32 minutes. The Spurs shot 42 percent from the field, including 6 - of - 27 from long range. Meanwhile, Willie Cauley-Stein led the Kings with 25 points, 10 rebounds, two assists and one block, in 37 minutes. De'Aaron Fox followed up with 21 points, seven assists and four rebounds, in 37 minutes. Buddy Hield added 17 points, in 32 minutes off the bench, while Nigel Hayes added seven points, nine rebounds, one assists and one steal. The Kings shot 41 percent from the field, including 8 - of - 18 from long range. The Kings (26 - 55) go on to play host to the Rocket on Wednesday. The Spurs (47 - 34) take on the Pelicans in New Orleans on Wednesday.
