The Atlanta Hawks defeated the host Chicago Bulls, 123 - 118, at United Center on Sunday afternoon. The contest picked up where it left off in the Bulls' and Hawks' last meeting, which was four - overtime shootout. The first quarter was a high - scoring one, with the Hawks taking a 35 - 32 lead into the second. They managed to hold the edge through the second and pushed their lead to seven by halftime. The Hawks kept up the momentum in the third, as they pushed their lead to 15, just minutes into the quarter. However, the Bulls kept fighting and managed to cut the deficit to five by the start of the fourth. They played hard through the fourth as well, but the Hawks stayed in control and held on for the road win. Alex Len led the Hawks with 28 points, nine rebounds, two assists and two blocks, in 26 minutes. Trae Young followed up with 18 points, five assists and three rebounds, while Dewayne Dedmon added 13 points, 12 rebounds, two assists and one steal. The Hawks shot 47 percent from the field, including 21 - of - 43 from long range. Meanwhile, Lauri Markkanen led the Bulls with 19 points, nine rebounds, three assists and two steals, in 28 minutes. Robin Lopez followed up with 16 points, six rebounds, four assists and a block, while Kris Dunn chipped in 14 points, six assists, four rebounds and two steals. The Bulls shot 51 percent from the field, including 8 - of - 27 from long range. The Bulls (18 - 46) will look to bounce back as they take on the Pacers in Indiana on Tuesday. The Hawks (22 - 42) take on the Heat in Miami on Monday.
