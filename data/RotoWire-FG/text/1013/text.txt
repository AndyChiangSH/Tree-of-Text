The Los Angeles Lakers defeated the Indiana Pacers, 104 - 96, at Staples Center on Thursday evening. The Lakers (12 - 9) came out of the gates seemingly intent on eradicating a two - game skid in short order, as they exploded for 38 first - quarter points while limiting the Pacers (13 - 9) to 15. However, Indiana almost turned the tables completely on Los Angeles in the second quarter with their own 35 - point haul, and they entered halftime with a manageable 56 - 50 deficit. It was a much tighter game in the second half, but LeBron James helped ensure the win by scoring the Lakers' final six points. It was a particularly impressive defensive performance for the home squad, which limited Indiana to a 37 percent figure from the field and forced them into 17 turnovers. James led the way for the Lakers with 38 points, a total he supplemented with nine rebounds, seven assists and two steals. Brandon Ingram followed with 14 points, three assists, one rebound, three blocks and one steal. Josh Hart contributed a bench - leading 13 points. Kyle Kuzma produced a near double - double with 11 points and nine rebounds while also adding two assists. Kentavious Caldwell-Pope matched Kuzma's scoring total over 18 minutes off the bench. Domantas Sabonis led the Pacers with a 20 - point, 15 - rebound double - double that also included three assists, one steal and one block. Bojan Bogdanovic poured in 14 points while adding three rebounds and two assists. Thaddeus Young supplied 11 points, five boards, one assist and five steals. Doug McDermott and Cory Joseph had matching 10 - point tallies off the bench. The Pacers remain on the road and tangle with the Sacramento Kings on Saturday night, while the Lakers remain home and face off with the Dallas Mavericks on Friday evening.
