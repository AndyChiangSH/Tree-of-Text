The Cleveland Cavaliers defeated the host Oklahoma City Thunder, 120 - 112, at Chesapeake Energy Arena on Tuesday evening. Things were tight to start, as the Cavs and Thunder traded baskets and the lead through the first quarter. The second quarter began with more of the same, however after the Thunder took a 38 - 36 lead, the Cavs went on a 20 - 8 run to take a 10 - point lead with 3:50 left in the half and held on to take a 62 - 57 lead into halftime. The Thunder came out strong in the third and used an 80 run to cut the deficit to as fe as two, as they trailed 78 - 76, with five minutes left in the quarter. The Thunder continued the pressure and managed to pull ahead, 82 - 81, however the Cavs responded and reclaimed a 91 - 87 lead heading into the final frame. Cleveland slowly pulled ahead to a 10 - point lead halfway through the fourth quarter, and despite a final push by the Thunder, held on for the road win. This marks the fourth straight win for the Cavaliers. LeBron James led the Cavs with 37 points, eight rebounds, eight assists and one block, in 39 minutes. J.R. Smith followed up with 18 points, six rebounds, three assists and one steal, in 30 minutes, while Rodney Hood and Jordan Clarkson chipped in 14 points each off the bench. The Cavs shot 51 percent from the field, including 16 - of - 36 from long range. Meanwhile, Paul George was the high - point man for OKC, as he tallied 25 points on 8 - of - 19 shooting, in 39 minutes. Steven Adams had a big night with 22 points, 17 rebounds, two steals, one assist and one block, in 42 minutes. Carmelo Anthony finished with 24 points, and seven rebounds, while Russell Westbrook tallied 21 points, 12 assists, seven rebounds and two steals. The Thunder shot 44 percent from the field, including 11 - of - 35 from long range. The Thunder (32 - 26) will be right back in action as they take on the Grizzlies in Memphis on Wednesday. The Cavs (34 - 22) play host to the Wizards on Thursday.
