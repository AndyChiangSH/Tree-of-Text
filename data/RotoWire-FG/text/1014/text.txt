The Toronto Raptors defeated the Cleveland Cavaliers, 106 - 95, at Quicken Loans Arena on Saturday evening. The Raptors (20 - 4) received news earlier in the day that star point guard Kyle Lowry (back) wouldn't suit up, which meant that the rest of the starting five would need to pick up the scoring slack. Toronto scuffled some without Lowry in the first half, but they still forged a solid 47 - 40 halftime lead. However, the Cavaliers (4 - 18) hung around, getting as close as 82 - 75 with 9:9 remaining. The visitors managed to stay one step ahead though, leading to the double - digit road win. Despite being short - handed, the Raptors shot a solid 49 percent while limiting Cleveland to a 39 percent success rate. Kawhi Leonard took the Raptors on his shoulders in Lowry's absence, leading the way with 34 points and adding nine rebounds, two assists, two blocks and one steal. Danny Green supplied 15 points, seven rebounds and two blocks. Fred VanVleet drew the start for Lowry and also posted 15 points, adding four assists, three rebounds and one steal. Pascal Siakam matched VanVleet's scoring total and also offered five rebounds and five assists. Serge Ibaka contributed 10 points and six boards. Tristan Thompson and Jordan Clarkson co - led the Cavaliers with 18 points apiece. Thompson added 19 rebounds, three assists and one steal. Clarkson hauled in two rebounds, dished out two assists and recorded one steal. Rodney Hood and Alec Burks followed with matching 13 - point tallies. Cedi Osman totaled 12 points, six rebounds, three assists and one steal. Collin Sexton registered 10 points, two rebounds and two assists. The Raptors head back home for a Monday night showdown with the Denver Nuggets, while the Cavaliers hit the road and face the Brooklyn Nets on Monday evening as well.
