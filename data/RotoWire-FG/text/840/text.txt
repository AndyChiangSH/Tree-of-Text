The Detroit Pistons defeated the Indiana Pacers, 107 - 83, at Little Ceasar's Arena on Tuesday. A 40 - 19 first quarter really set the tone in this game, as Indiana could never recover from that hole. In fact, this was a wire - to - wire win for Detroit, as they led by double - digits for all but three minutes. The Pistons ended up shooting 50 percent from the field, while holding the Pacers to 44 percent shooting. The rebounding disparity was vast, with Detroit winning that battle, 46 - 26. The Pacers (19 - 15) didn't have a single player score more than 13 points, as Victor Oladipo led them with 13 points, three assists, two steals and two blocks. Myles Turner totaled 10 points, three rebounds and three assists. Darren Collison and Thaddeus Young combined for 10 points, three rebounds, no assists and no steals. The Pistons (19 - 14) were led by Tobias Harris, as he tallied 30 points and six rebounds on 10 - of - 11 shooting. Andre Drummond amassed 21 points, 18 rebounds and three assists. Reggie Jackson accrued eight points, three rebounds and 13 assists before departing with an ankle injury in the third quarter. Ish Smith led the bench with 12 points, two rebounds and three assists, Indiana will have a quick turnaround, as they host the Dallas Mavericks on Wednesday for their next fixture. As for Detroit, they travel to play the Orlando Magic on Thursday.,
