The Charlotte Hornets defeated the visiting New York Knicks, 101 - 92, at Spectrum Center on Monday. While Charlotte came into this game as a sizable favorite, it took five ties and 10 lead changes to determine a winner. The Hornets took control in the fourth quarter though, outscoring the Knicks, 30 - 21. Offensive rebounding was huge for Charlotte, as they outrebounded New York, 14 - 7. Ball movement was also decisive, with the Hornets out - assisting the Knicks, 25 - 19. Charlotte's bench was probably the difference though, as they outscored New York's bench, 53 - 37. The Knicks (10 - 39) were led by Kevin Knox, as he amassed 19 points and three rebounds. Tim Hardaway Jr. finished with 17 points, 10 rebounds and four assists. Noah Vonleh accrued five points, 12 rebounds and two assists. No other player on the roster reached double figures in scoring, rebounding or assists. The Hornets (24 - 25) were led by Jeremy Lamb, as he collected 15 points, nine rebounds and three assists. Kemba Walker amassed 14 points, five rebounds, five assists and two blocks. Four players reached double figures in scoring off the bench, with Tony Parker leading the way with 15 points and four assists. Wily Hernangomez tallied 11 points and 11 rebounds off the bench as well. Both teams return to action on Wednesday, with Charlotte traveling to face the surging Boston Celtics and New York playing host to the Dallas Mavericks.
