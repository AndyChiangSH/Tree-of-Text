The Philadelphia 76ers narrowly edged out the Denver Nuggets, 107 - 102, at Pepsi Center on Saturday. Philadelphia (16 - 19) will be happy to have the win, as it had gone just 1 - 5 over its last six games. Moreover, star center Joel Embiid (rest) was not active for Saturday's contest. Still, the team earned the win, thanks in large part to a strong defensive effort. The Sixers held the Nuggets to just 40 percent shooting overall and 7 - of - 32 shooting from beyond the three - point line. On the offensive end, six Philadelphia players scored in double digits, in what was a balanced effort for the team. Dario Saric led the Sixers with 20 points on 6 - of - 15 shooting. It was a rare off night for rookie star Ben Simmons despite the win. Simmons had just six points and committed five turnovers on the night. For Denver (19 - 17), the loss is the second straight in tough, last - minute fashion. The team struggled to find its shot throughout the night, despite a team - leading performance from sophomore guard Jamal Murray. Murray shot 11 - of - 23 en route to a team - high 31 points. The 20 - year - old is now averaging 19 points per game in the month of December. Star center Nikola Jokic also had a big night, scoring 19 points to go with 13 rebounds. The duo combined to shoot 16 - of - 18 at the free - throw line, reaching the charity stripe more times than the entire 76ers team. Philadelphia will travel to Phoenix on New Year's Eve. Denver, meanwhile, will host Phoenix next Wednesday.
