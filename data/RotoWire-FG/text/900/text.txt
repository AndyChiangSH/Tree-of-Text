The Oklahoma City Thunder defeated the visiting Memphis Grizzlies, 110 - 92, at Chesapeake Energy Arena on Sunday evening. The Grizzlies started off with a 60 lead, but the Thunder quickly took control and did not look back. The Thunder picked up the pace and amassed a 35 - 18 lead by the end of the first quarter. The second quarter was more highly contested, as the Grizzlies managed to pour in 36 points, but the Thunder kept pace with 39 points of their own, to take a 74 - 54 lead by halftime. The teams traded baskets through the third quarter, with the Grizzlies managing to gain a little bit of ground, as they outscored the Thunder by five, to cut the deficit to 15, heading into the final frame. Nonetheless, the Thunder were able to cruise to the finish, as they maintained a double digit lead through the fourth quarter. Oklahoma City was without Russell Westbrook (ankle) and Carmelo Anthony (ankle), but Paul George was able to step up and take the lead by pouring in 33 points on 10 - of - 24 shooting, in 35 minutes. Alex Abrines followed up with 16 points, while Patrick Patterson, Raymond Felton and Jerami Grant chipped in 14 points each. The Thunder shot 45 percent from the field, including 16 - of - 37 from long range. Meanwhile, Marc Gasol was the high - point man for Memphis, with 18 points on 6 - of - 14 shooting, in 33 minutes. JaMychal Green followed up with 14 points, 12 rebounds, two assists, one steal and one block, while Tyreke Evans chipped in 12 points, five rebounds and three assists. The Grizzlies shot 39 percent from the field, including 6 - of - 28 from long range. The Grizzlies (18 - 37) will look to bounce back as they take on the Thunder again, this time in Memphis on Wednesday. The Thunder (32 - 25) play host to the Cavaliers on Tuesday.
