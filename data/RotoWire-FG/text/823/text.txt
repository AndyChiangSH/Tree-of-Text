The Charlotte Hornets defeated the Oklahoma City Thunder, 116 - 103, at Chesapeake Energy Arena on Monday. While this was a competitive game through the first three quarters, a huge run at the end of the third quarter changed the game. In fact, Charlotte turned a 66 - 66 tie into an 88 - 68 lead with a 20 - 2 run. Oklahoma City couldn't recover from there, as the Thunder never got within single - digits from there. Shooting was key, with Charlotte hitting 53 percent of their shots, while holding the Thunder to just 41 percent from the field. They also played well on defense, with the Hornets recording eight steals and four blocks. The Hornets (10 - 16) were led by Dwight Howard, as he tallied 23 points, seven rebounds and two blocks. Kemba Walker amassed 19 points, three rebounds and nine assists. Marvin Williams totaled 18 points, seven rebounds and two assists. Michael Kidd-Gilchrist amassed 17 points, four rebounds and four assists. The Thunder's (12 - 14) best player was Russell Westbrook. as he collected 30 points, four rebounds, seven assists and three steals. Paul George accrued 20 points and five rebounds. Steven Adams recorded an 11 - point, 10 - rebound double - double. Both teams have road games on Wednesday for their next fixture, with Oklahoma City playing the Indiana Pacers and Charlotte facing the Houston Rockets.
