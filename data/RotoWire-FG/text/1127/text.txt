The Minnesota Timberwolves defeated the host Dallas Mavericks, 110 - 108, at American Airlines Center on Wednesday evening. The Timberwolves got out to a good start on the road, as they took a 32 - 27 lead after one quarter. The Mavs picked it up in the second and tied it up at 36, three minutes into the quarter. After a few lead changes, the Mavs and Timberwolves ended up tied at 58 by halftime. The third quarter started out close, but the Timberwolves took over toward the end of the quarter, to build a 10 - point lead entering the fourth. The Mavs did not back down and got out to a good start in the fourth. They kept fighting and managed to tie it at 99, with four minutes left. The last minutes was filled with ties and lead changes, but the Timberwolves were able to stick it out to finish on top, and end a two - game losing streak. Karl-Anthony Towns led the Timberwolves, with 28 points, 13 rebounds, three assists and three blocks, in 36 minutes. Andrew Wiggins followed up with 19 points, seven assists, three rebounds and two steals, while Tyus Jones added 15 points, six assists and a rebound. The Timberwolves shot 49 percent from the field, including 10 - of - 27 from deep. Meanwhile, Luka Doncic led the Mavs with 27 points, 12 rebounds, six assists and a block, in 31 minutes. Dwight Powell followed up with 25 points, eight rebounds, two assists, two steals and a block, while Trey Burke chipped in 12 points, four assists and two rebounds. The Mavs shot 46 percent from the field, including 7 - of - 39 from long range. The Mavericks (31 - 47) will look to bounce back as they play host to the Grizzlies on Friday. The Timberwolves (35 - 43) play host to the Heat on Friday.
