The Chicago Bulls defeated the host Orlando Magic, 105 - 83, at Amway Center on Friday. The recent forms of these teams would indicate an easy win for Orlando, but Chicago was in charge for the majority of this matchup. The second half is where the Bulls really separated themselves, outscoring the Magic, 61 - 38. Rebounding was a huge factor as well, with Chicago winning the rebounding battle, 48 - 38. The Magic really struggled shooting, hitting just 41 percent from the field, 29 percent from three - point range and 60 percent from the free - throw line. The Bulls (2 - 5) were led by Justin Holiday, who tallied 19 points, two rebounds and two steals. David Nwaba amassed 16 points, five rebounds and two steals. Lauri Markkanen totaled 13 points, nine rebounds and three assists. Robin Lopez amassed 11 points and 10 rebounds en route to a double - double. Kris Dunn, Denzel Valentine and Paul Zipser all reached double figures off the bench. The Magic (6 - 3) got 21 points, five rebounds and three assists out of Even Fournier. Nikola Vucevic accrued 14 points, three rebounds and two assists. Aaron Gordon collected 11 points, seven rebounds and two assists. No other player on the roster scored more than eight points. Chicago will have a quick turnaround, as they host the New Orleans Pelicans on Saturday for their next game. Orlando will host the Boston Celtics on Sunday for their next fixture.
