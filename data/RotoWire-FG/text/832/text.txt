The Cleveland Cavaliers defeated the Los Angeles Lakers, 121 - 112, at Quicken Loans Arena on Thursday night. The Cavaliers (21 - 8) and the Lakers (10 - 17) engaged in quite a battle in this game, as the Lakers proved to be a harder - than - expected opponent for a Cavs team that has been on a roll of late. The Cavaliers built a three - point advantage at the break and they never looked back, but that doesn't mean the game was easy. Both teams missed chances to either cut or extend the lead, and in the end the more experienced team came out on top. But the Lakers certainly proved they can compete with any team in the league if they engage to do so. LeBron James was the undisputed star for the Cavaliers, as the small forward finished with a strong triple - double of 25 points, 12 rebounds, and 12 assists but he wasn't the only player who made a difference for Cleveland. Kevin Love contributed with 28 points and 11 rebounds, and Jose Calderon contributed with 17 points, six assists, three rebounds and two steals. Dwyane Wade had 10 points with six assists. Brandon Ingram led the way for the Lakers with 26 points, six rebounds and six assists. Lonzo Ball came close to a triple - double, as the rookie point guard with 13 points, 11 assists, and eight boards. Two players off the bench delivered strong performances as well, as Kyle Kuzma finished with 20 points and seven rebounds, and Julius Randle added 13 points with four boards and three assists.
