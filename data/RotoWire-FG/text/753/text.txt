The host Golden State Warriors defeated the Portland Trail Blazers, 110 - 81, in Game 2 of the Western Conference first - round playoffs at Oracle Arena on Wednesday. Prior to the game, it was announced that Golden State star Kevin Durant would sit the game out due to a strained calf. Warriors coach Steve Kerr defended the decision, saying he would "feel like an idiot" if Durant were to re - aggravate the injury. Durant is officially listed as day - to - day and may rest again in Game 3 given the Warriors' comfortable cushion. Golden State (20) showed off its top - 3 ranked defense throughout the game, holding Portland to just 33 percent shooting on the night. With Durant sitting out and star players Steph Curry and Klay Thompson combining for just 35 points on 12 - of - 35 shooting, it was defense that proved key to earning the blowout win. The team finished with eight steals and a whopping 11 blocks. Four of those blocks came from bench big man JaVale McGee, who was superb in limited playing time. McGee shot a perfect 7 - of - 7 from the field for 15 points. Portland (0 - 2) will head back to its home court in a fairly deep hole after dropping each of the first two games of the series by double figures. After what was a miraculous first half for the Blazers' backcourt in Game 1, Damian Lillard and C.J. McCollum went ice cold in Game 2, combining to shoot 9 - of - 34 for just 23 points. The team as a whole saw horrendous shooting splits, hitting just 33 percent of its shots from the field and 20 percent from beyond the arc. Game 3 will take place in Portland on Saturday.
