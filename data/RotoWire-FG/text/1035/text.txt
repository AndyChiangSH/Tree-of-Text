The Portland Trail Blazers defeated the visiting Dallas Mavericks, 121 - 118, in overtime at Moda Center on Sunday evening. The Mavs got out to a good start on the road, with an early 10 - 4 lead, before taking a 27 - 25 lead into the second quarter. However, the home squad turned it on in the second, where they outscored the Mavs by 12, to take a 62 - 52 lead by halftime. The Trail Blazers maintained a double - digit lead through most of the third quarter but the Mavs pushed to make it an eight - point game heading into the fourth. The Mavericks kept fighting and managed to trim the deficit to just two points with four minutes left. They traded baskets down the stretch, culminated by a super - clutch three - pointer by Luka Doncic to send the game to overtime, tied at 107. Nonetheless, the Trail Blazers took control with an early lead in OT and stayed a step ahead the rest of the way. Damian Lillard led the Trail Blazers with 33 points, seven assists, two rebounds and one block, in 39 minutes. C.J. McCollum added 22 points, five rebounds, five assists and one steal, while Meyers Leonard chipped in 13 points and six rebounds, in 23 minutes off the bench. The Blazers shot 47 percent from the field, including 11 - of - 38 from long range. Meanwhile, Harrison Barnes led the Mavs with 27 points on 11 - of - 20 shooting, and one assist, in 35 minutes. Luka Doncic followed up with 23 points, 11 rebounds and six assists, in 35 minutes. DeAndre Jordan finished with 14 points, 10 rebounds and one assist. The Mavs (15 - 17) will look to bounce back as they play host to the Pelicans on Wednesday. The Trail Blazers (19 - 14) take on the Jazz in Utah on Christmas Day.
