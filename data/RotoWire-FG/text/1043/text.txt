The Boston Celtics defeated the visiting Minnesota Timberwolves, 115 - 102, at TD Garden on Wednesday. The final scoreline makes this game look closer than it actually was, as Boston won in wire - to - wire fashion. In fact, a 35 - 21 second quarter really set the tone, with the Celtics leading by at least five points for the final 30 minutes. Their offense was unstoppable, as they connected on 59 percent from the field and 40 percent from three - point range while recording 34 assists. Rim protection was also critical, with the Celtics out - blocking the T'Wolves, 8 - 2. Transition offense was also big for Boston, as they outscored Minnesota in fastbreak points, 23 - 10. Bench production can't be overlooked either, with the Celtics outscoring the Timberwolves bench, 62 - 18. The Timberwolves (17 - 21) got another spectacular performance from Karl-Anthony Towns, as he collected 28 points, 12 rebounds and seven assists. Andrew Wiggins was just as good, as he scored a season - high 31 points to go along with two rebounds and three assists. Tyus Jones got another start and tallied 14 points, nine assists and four steals. No other player reached 10 points, five rebounds or three assists. The Celtics (22 - 15) played without Kyrie Irving (eye), as Gordon Hayward had his best game of the season. In fact, Hayward went 14 - of - 18 from the field to lead the bench with 35 points and five assists. Terry Rozier got a rare start and totaled 16 points, three rebounds, five assists and five steals. Al Horford accrued 15 points, five rebounds, four assists and two blocks. On deck for Minnesota is a home matchup versus the Orlando Magic on Friday. Boston also returns to action on Friday, as they welcome the Dallas Mavericks to Beantown.
