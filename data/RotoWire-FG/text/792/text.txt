The New Orleans Pelicans defeated the visiting Atlanta Hawks, 106 - 105, at Smoothie King Center on Monday evening. The Hawks (2 - 12) started off strong, with a 32 - 23 lead after one quarter. The Pelicans (8 - 6) tightened things up in the second quarter, but still trailed by five at halftime. The Hawks held strong and managed to hold a two - point lead heading into the final frame. The game came down to the final couple possessions, where the Pelicans nabbed the one - point lead with a few clutch shots. The Hawks had a chance to win it with a final possession, but ended up giving up a turnover with three seconds left in the game. E'Twaun Moore tied his career - high with 24 points on 11 - of - 18 shooting, in 33 minutes. DeMarcus Cousins followed up with 22 points, 16 rebounds, seven assists and three steals, while Anthony Davis added 13 points, 10 rebounds, seven assists and two steals. Darius Miller gave the Pelicans a much - needed boost, with 21 points on 8 - of - 11 shooting, in 21 minutes off the bench. The Pelicans shot 54 percent from the field and assisted on 31 of their 41 field goals. Meanwhile, seven players scored in double digits for the Hawks, led by Kent Bazemore, who finished with 22 points, seven assists, four rebounds and three steals, in 29 minutes. Marco Belinelli chipped in 14 points, while Tyler Cavanaugh added 16 points and six rebounds, in 18 minutes off the bench. Luke Babbitt and John Collins totaled 12 points each. The Hawks shot 45 percent from the field, including 47 percent from long range. The Hawks will look to end a four - game losing streak, as they play host to the Kings on Wednesday. The Pelicans play host to the Raptors on Wednesday.
