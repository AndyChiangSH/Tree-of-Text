The host Oklahoma City Thunder defeated the Dallas Mavericks, 112 - 99, at Chesapeake Energy Arena on Sunday evening. For the second straight game, All-Star forward Paul George led the Thunder (6 - 7) to the victory. George, who scored a team - high 42 points in the Thunder's recent win over Los Angeles, scored another 37 points to lead the team on Sunday. The veteran All-Star hit seven three - pointers and shot 12 - of - 22 overall on the night in what was arguably his most effective night in a Thunder uniform. George's strong play helped to make up for the absence of star forward Carmelo Anthony, who missed the game with a back injury. Reigning MVP Russell Westbrook also delivered big numbers, scoring 27 points. However, the point guard continued to struggle from the field, shooting only 6 - of - 18 on the night. Dallas (2 - 12) owns the unfortunate title of the league's worst record. Despite a tough battle against the reigning Eastern Conference champion Cavaliers on Saturday, the Mavericks have dropped two straight games and sit at the bottom of the Western Conference standings. While the Mavericks have seen few bright spots in the 2017 - 18 campaign, rookie point guard Dennis Smith, Jr. has impressed with his strong play. On Sunday, Smith scored 15 points to go along with five rebounds. Smith was among Dallas's leading scorers in the game. The team's leader in the points column, though, was veteran forward Harrison Barnes, who scored 22 to go along with 13 rebounds. Dallas will host the Spurs on Tuesday, while OKC will host the Bulls on Wednesday.
