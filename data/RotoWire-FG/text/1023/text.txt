The Sacramento Kings defeated the host Chicago Bulls, 108 - 89, at United Center on Monday evening. The Kings jumped out to an early, 10 - 4, lead, but the Bulls battled back to take a, 33 - 26, lead by the end of the first. The home team kept the momentum through the second, as they stayed a couple steps ahead the whole way, and built a, 56 - 45, lead by halftime. The Bulls held their advantage to start the third, but the Kings mounted a 24 - 8 run, to close the quarter and take a 81 - 74 lead into the final frame. The Kings kept the pressure on in the fourth, as they quickly pushed their lead to 10. They continued to add to the lead until they led by 20 with two minutes left, allowing them to finish off their four - game road trip with a positive, 3 - 1, record. De'Aaron Fox led the Kings with 25 points, six assists, four rebounds, four steals and one block, in 33 minutes. Bogdan Bogdanovic and Marvin Bagley III tallied 16 points each, while Willie Cauley-Stein added 11 points, 16 rebounds, three assists and three steals. The Kings shot 46 percent from the field, including 16 - of - 37 from long range. Meanwhile, Zach LaVine led the Bulls with 19 points, three steals, two rebounds and two assists, in 32 minutes. Lauri Markkanen tallied 13 points, six rebounds, one assist and one steal, while Kris Dunn, Bobby Portis and Justin Holiday chipped in nine points each. The Bulls shot 45 percent from the field, including 8 - of - 28 from long range. The Bulls (6 - 22) will look to bounce back as they take on the Magic in Orlando on Thursday. The Kings (14 - 12) host the Timberwolves on Wednesday.
