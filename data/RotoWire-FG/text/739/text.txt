Despite a close game down the stretch, the visiting Golden State Warriors managed to beat the Phoenix Suns on Wednesday, 120 - 111. Golden State (65 - 14) clinched the league's best record for the third straight season with the win. The team also extended its league - best win streak to 13 games. It was almost inevitable that the Warriors would walk away with the win after they started the game with a 40 - 18 first quarter advantage. Despite that, however, Phoenix was able to bring the game back to a nail - biter by the late fourth quarter. Still, it was tough for the Suns to do anything to stop reigning MVP Steph Curry. Curry scored a team - high 42 points while dishing out 11 assists. The other "Splash Brother," Klay Thompson, added 22 points. On the whole, the Dubs shot 50 percent on the night and made 13 three - pointers. Phoenix (22 - 57) dropped its 13th consecutive game and continues to sit at the bottom of the Western Conference. The Suns shot only 41 percent on the night on Wednesday, but kept things close with Golden State thanks to strong rebounding. Phoenix held a 55 - 40 advantage on the boards and pulled down a whopping 20 offensive rebounds. At the heart of the rebounding effort was young big man Alan Williams, who had a 16 - point, 17 - rebound double - double in just 25 minutes of play. On Friday, Phoenix will host OKC, while Golden State will host the Pelicans on Saturday.
