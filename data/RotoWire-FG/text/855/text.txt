The Charlotte Hornets defeated the Los Angeles Lakers, 108 - 94, on their home court on Friday night. The Hornets (15 - 23) had clear control of the game by the end of the first half, as they held a 15 - point lead. While shots weren't always falling for Charlotte, the Hornets benefited from an awful offensive showing from the home team. The Hornets were able to hold the Lakers to just 40 percent shooting from the field, and allowed only 12 free - throw attempts on the night. On offense, Charlotte was carried by a few strong performances. Kemba Walker finished the night with 19 points, a team high. Off the bench, Jeremy Lamb scored an efficient 17 points on 7 - of - 13 shooting. The team recorded 26 team assists as well, as ball movement was a focus throughout. Los Angeles (11 - 27) had yet another pitiful showing on both ends, leading to their ninth straight loss. Rookie point guard Lonzo Ball returned to the starting five following an extended absence due to a shoulder sprain. Ball played fairly well, scoring 11 points and dishing five assists in 27 minutes. Unfortunately, the same could not be said of his fellow rookies. Kyle Kuzma shot just 2 - of - 14 for four points on the night. He also committed three turnovers. Josh Hart, meanwhile, shot just 1 - of - 6. The team as a whole shot only 40 percent from the field and went just 9 - of - 12 at the free - throw line. Los Angeles will host the Atlanta Hawks on Sunday, while the Hornets will host the Dallas Mavericks on Tuesday.
