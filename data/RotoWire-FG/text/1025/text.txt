The Denver Nuggets defeated the Memphis Grizzlies, 105 - 99, at Pepsi Center on Monday. Injuries have continued to plague the Nuggets (18 - 9) this season, but they fought through the absence of two starters Monday to earn a key victory over the Grizzlies after dropping back - to - back games to the Hornets and Hawks. They were led by a stellar outing from Nikola Jokic, who contnues to show how he's one of the best all - around players in the NBA. Jokic posted a game - high 27 points on 9 - of - 14 shooting to go along with 12 rebounds and six assists in 38 minutes. The most important performance from a Nuggets players Monday night, however, was from reserve point guard Monte Morris. Morris came off the bench to score 20 points on 8 - of - 12 shooting on top of five rebounds and six assists in 28 minutes. As for the Grizzlies (15 - 11), they have now dropped two games in a row, and Monday's loss can be chalked up to a lack of production from the starting unit. Mike Conley shot just 5 - of - 14 from the field, but he was the only Memphis starter to reach double figures, scoring a team - high 19 points in 32 minutes. Marc Gasol was just 2 - of - 6 from the field and scored just seven points, while rookie sensation Justin Jackson Jr. played just 20 minutes and managed to post only seven points in the process. The Grizzlies' bench, however, did everything it could to keep the team in the game. Led by Shelvin Mack's 14 points, the five Memphis reserves scored 51 of the team's 99 points, outscoring the five starters.
