The Houston Rockets defeated the Dallas Mavericks, 104 - 97, at Toyota Center on Sunday. This was a near wire - to - wire win for Houston, as Dallas' only lead came in the first three minutes. The Rockets never trailed from there, as they led by at least five points for the final 20 minutes of action. Free-throw shooting was the difference, with Houston hitting 23 - of - 33 and Dallas going just 11 - of - 16 from the charity stripe. The Rockets were also buoyed by the turnover differential, forcing nine turnovers from the Mavericks while committing just nine on their end. The Mavericks dominated the rest of the stat sheet, as they won the rebounding and assisting differentials by double - digit margins. The Mavericks (18 - 39) best player was Dennis Smith Jr., as he accumulated 16 points, five rebounds and 11 assists. Yogi Ferrell led the team with 20 points, five rebounds and two steals. Dwight Powell totaled 18 points and 12 rebounds while Jalen Jones led the bench with eight points and 10 rebounds, The Rockets (42 - 13) were led by James Harden, as he collected 27 points, six rebounds, five assists and two steals. Chris Paul finished with 25 points, eight rebounds, nine assists and three steals. Clint Capela amassed 16 points, 11 rebounds and three blocks. Both teams return to action on Tuesday, with Houston traveling to play the Minnesota Timberwolves and Dallas playing host to the Sacramento Kings.
