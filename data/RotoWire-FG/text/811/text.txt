The Golden State Warriors defeated the hosting Miami Heat, 123 - 95, at American Airlines Center on Sunday evening. The Warriors (18 - 6) were coming off a huge win against the Orlando Magic, and continued their road trip with another excellent performance on the offensive end of the court. Despite the fact the Heat (11 - 12) led after the first quarter and were down by two after half time, they simply couldn't find a way to contain Golden State in the second half. The reigning NBA champions won the third quarter 37 - 17 and 24 - 18 in the fourth, looking dominant in the process and improving their road record to 10 - 3. Meanwhile, Miami fell to 5 - 6 at home. Stephen Curry led the Warriors offensively with 30 points, as the two - time MVP winner shot 11 - of - 16 from the field and 5 - of - 9 from three - point range. Kevin Durant added 24 points with seven assists, and Klay Thompson chipped in with 19 points, five rebounds, and four assists. Omri Casspi contributed with 10 points and six boards off the bench. At the other end, Goran Dragic led the way for the Heat with 20 points and Josh Richardson added 12 points, but they would have liked a better performance from Dion Waiters. The shooting guard only made one of his 10 field - goal attempts, and finished the game with four points in 24 minutes. Tyler Johnson (13 points) and Kelly Olynyk (14 points, seven boards and five assists) did a good job off the bench as well.
