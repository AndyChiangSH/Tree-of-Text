The host Portland Trail Blazers took down the Houston Rockets on Thursday, 117 - 107. Portland (37 - 38) has been one of the league's hottest teams, and has now won eight of its last nine games. Since acquiring big man Jusuf Nurkic, the team has seen immense success, due in large part to the play of the young center. On Thursday, Nurkic scored 19 points to go along with 11 rebounds and blocked three shots. The star of the show, however, was point guard Damian Lillard. Lillard scored a team - high 31 points and recorded a double - double, dishing out 11 assists. Portland shot over 46 percent on the night and finished with 13 three - pointers made. Houston (51 - 24) also came into the game scorching hot, but dropped the game by double figures by the end. MVP contender James Harden was superb once again, scoring 30 points and pulling down four rebounds. It was a season low in the assists column for the league leader in dimes, though, as he dished out only four assists on the night. Five players scored in double figures in what was still a relatively strong offensive night for the Rockets. The team shot over 43 percent on the night and scored 107 points. The Rockets will visit Golden State on Friday, while Portland will host Phoenix on Saturday.
