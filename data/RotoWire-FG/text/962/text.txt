The New Orleans Pelicans defeated the host Portland Trail Blazers, 97 - 95, in Game 1 of the Western Conference first - round playoffs at Mod Center on Saturday. There were three ties and 10 lead changes in this game, as it marked the Pelicans first playoff win in nearly a decade. Defense was key for New Orleans, as they recorded 12 blocks and forced Portland to shoot just 38 percent from the field and 31 percent from three - point range. The Pelicans ended up hitting 48 percent from the field and 33 percent from long range. Ball movement was a huge part of that, with the Pelicans out - assisting the Trail Blazers, 26 - 17. Points off turnovers were also big for New Orleans, as they won that battle, 23 - 9. The Pelicans (10) got yet another gem out of Anthony Davis, as he collected 35 points, 14 rebounds, two steals and four blocks. Jrue Holiday accrued 21 points, seven rebounds, two assists and two blocks. Nikola Mirotic accumulated 16 points, 11 rebounds, three assists, two steals and four blocks. Rajon Rondo provided six points, eight rebounds and 17 assists. The Trail Blazers (0 - 1) were led by Damian Lillard, as he tallied 18 points, seven rebounds, seven assists and two steals, but went just 6 - of - 23 from the field. C.J. McCollum amassed 19 points, two rebounds, two assists and three steals. Jusuf Nurkic totaled 11 points, 11 rebounds and two blocks. Ed Davis led the team in rebounding, grabbing 13 boards off of the bench. These teams will be off until Tuesday when they play Game 2 back in Portland.
