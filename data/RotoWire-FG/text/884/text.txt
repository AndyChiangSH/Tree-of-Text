The Brooklyn Nets defeated the Philadelphia 76ers 116 - 108 on Wednesday at Barclays Center. Wednesday night was the complete, efficient team effort that the Nets (19 - 33) needed to end their four - game winning streak. Point guard D'Angelo Russell embodied that efficiency, as despite still being limited off the bench in his slow return from a knee injury, Russell shot 8 - of - 15 from the field en route to scoring 22 points in just 17 minutes as a reserve. He was only outdone by starting point guard Spencer Dinwiddie, who scored a team - high 27 points in 34 minutes thanks to his ability to get to the free throw line, shooting 13 - of - 15 from the charity stripe. In all, five Brooklyn players scored in double figures while the team shot 41 percent from three and 51 percent from the field overall, as they managed to torch a stout Philadelphia defense. That poor defensive effort from the 76ers (24 - 24) ended up being the difference in Wednesday's game, as the team still managed to have a pretty solid overall effort on the offensive end, shooting 39 percent from three and 47 percent from the field overall. Joel Embiid, Ben Simmons and J.J. Redick accounted for majority of Philly's offensive production, with the three starters combining to score 73 of the team's 108 points Wednesday. Embiid led the way with a monster double - double of 29 points and 14 rebounds in 38 minutes. Up next, the Nets will remain home Friday and play the Lakers, while the 76ers will return home Friday to take on the Heat.
