The New Orleans Pelicans defeated the host Boston Celtics, 116 - 113, in overtime at TD Garden on Tuesday evening. The Pelicans got out to a good start on the road, as they managed to take the early advantage through the first quarter and hold a 32 - 25 lead heading into the second. They continued to play with the advantage through the second quarter and pushed their lead to 10, up 58 - 48, heading into halftime. The Celtics did a better job in the second half and chipped away until they took a three - point lead with 4:40 left in the game. The teams traded buckets during the last minutes of regulation, until Kyrie Irving nailed a game - tying layup with 11 seconds left, that would send it to overtime. The Pelicans held strong in OT and edged the Celtics, en route to their third straight win. Anthony Davis led the way for New Orleans with 45 points, 16 rebounds, two assists, two blocks and one steal, in 44 minutes of action. DeMarcus Cousins added 19 points, 15 rebounds, five assists and three steals, while Jrue Holiday finished with 23 points, seven assists, four rebounds, three steals and one block. The Pelicans shot 45 percent from the field, including 7 - of - 27 from long range. Meanwhile, Kyrie Irving was the high - point man for the Celtics, with 27 points on 8 - of - 24 shooting, in 40 minutes. Jaylen Brown followed up with 16 points, five assists, four rebounds and one steal, while Al Horford finished with 14 points, nine rebounds, six assists, one steal and one block. The Celtics shot 40 percent from the field, including 19 - of - 50 from long range. The Celtics (34 - 11) play host to the 76ers on Thursday. The Pelicans (23 - 20) will be right back in action as they take on the Hawks in Atlanta on Wednesday.
