The New Orleans Pelicans defeated the Phoenix Suns, 125 - 116, at Smoothie King Center on Monday. The Pelicans (34 - 26) and the Suns (18 - 44) engaged in quite a battle that ended with New Orleans winning its sixth straight game, while Phoenix dropped its 10th straight contest. New Orleans had to come from behind in this one, however, as Phoenix quickly jumped to a 36 - 26 point lead in the first quarter and reached half time up by seven. The Pelicans answered back with a huge fourth quarter, however, and a 38 - 24 third - period performance put them up by seven heading into the fourth. Phoenix tried to comeback, but New Orleans sealed the deal and ended winning a tough game that could end up being decisive for the team's playoff chances. Anthony Davis led the way for the Pelicans with a monster stat line: 53 points, 17 rebounds, five blocks, and three assists in only 38 minutes. Jrue Holiday contributed 20 points and seven assists, while Emeka Okafor had 14 points and four rebounds in just 19 minutes. Rajon Rondo finished with six points and five rebounds but dished out 12 assists. Nikola Mirotic added 10 points and eight boards off the bench. Devin Booker had quite a game as well for the Suns, as the talented shooting guard finished with 40 points, 10 rebounds and seven assists. T.J. Warren had 23 points and six rebounds, while Elfrid Payton had 16 points, seven boards, five assists and two steals. Josh Jackson chipped in with a 20 - point performance coming off the bench.
