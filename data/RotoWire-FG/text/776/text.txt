The Los Angeles Clippers defeated the visiting Dallas Mavericks, 119 - 98, at Staples Center on Wednesday night. The Clippers (5 - 2) cruised their way to a relatively easy win in this one, as they pulled away in the first half and never looked back. Los Angeles built a 66 - 48 lead in the first half, highlighted by an impressive 34 - 19 run during the second quarter. They simply kept the Mavs away on the scoreboard during the remaining two quarters. Dallas tried to react from time to time, but the Clips always shut the door en route to a comfortable win. Blake Griffin's solid all - around effort certainly sparked the Clippers, as the star power forward finished the game with 20 points, six rebounds and six assists. Austin Rivers had another decent outing with 19 points starting at shooting guard. DeAndre Jordan added 13 points with eight boards. Lou Williams provided a spark off the bench thanks to 17 points, four rebounds and three assists off the bench. On the Mavericks' side, all five of their starters scored in double digits but that wasn't enough to take down the Clippers. Rookie point guard Dennis Smith Jr. led the way with 18 points, but he also committed six turnovers. Harrison Barnes had 14 points and Dirk Nowitzki ended with 13 points, eight boards and two assists. One player to highlight was Maxi Kleber, as the German rookie finished with 11 points (on 4 - for - 4 from the field) off the bench.
