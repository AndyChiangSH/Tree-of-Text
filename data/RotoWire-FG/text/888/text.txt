The Golden State Warriors took down the host Sacramento Kings, 119 - 104, at Golden One Center on Friday. Though the final score would not indicate it, Friday's California clash was a relatively close game throughout. The Kings (16 - 35) remained within single digits of the defending champions for the majority of the game, and trailed by just one point with seven minutes left in regulation. From there, though, it was all about Kevin Durant. Durant had 10 consecutive points for the Warriors (41 - 11), including two big three - pointers to help the team reclaim a double - digit lead. Golden State scored 20 points in total in the final seven minutes of the game, helping to open up a commanding lead on the Kings' home court. Durant finished the game with a team - high 33 points, which came on 12 - of - 17 shooting. Steph Curry had 23 points, while Klay Thompson added another 20. The trio's strong play helped make up for the team's relatively high number of turnovers. Golden State committed 25 on the night, which was just one away from tying its season high. For the Kings, all five starters scored in double digits in what was a relatively strong showing for the first 42 minutes. Veteran Zach Randolph led the team with 18 points and seven rebounds. Rookie forward Justin Jackson added another 17, shooting 7 - of - 13 on the night. Both teams face the second half of a back - to - back on Saturday, as the Warriors travel to Denver while Sacramento hosts the Dallas Mavericks.
