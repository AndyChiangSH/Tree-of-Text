The Charlotte Hornets defeated the Indiana Pacers, 119 - 93, at Bankers Life Fieldhouse on Tuesday. The Hornets (36 - 46) were already out of the postseason and the Pacers (48 - 34) had locked up their seed in the Eastern Conference, so this game had nothing but pride at stake for both franchises. Charlotte started this game with a chip on their shoulder, however, and quickly jumped to a 37 - 25 lead in the first quarter. Indiana pulled back in the second quarter (26 - 30) and made things close entering the second half, but the Hornets pulled away and won the final two quarters, 24 - 16 and 32 - 22, to end the regular season on a strong note. Meanwhile, the Pacers will now enter the playoffs with lots of doubts. Frank Kaminsky was the best player for the Hornets in this game, as he finished with 24 points and seven rebounds while coming off the bench. Malik Monk also thrived as a substitute, as he contributed 17 points on 25 minutes. Kemba Walker and Marvin Williams each finished with 15 points, while Dwight Howard and Nicolas Batum had 14 points. Howard also added 17 rebounds. The Pacers didn't have a good offensive performance, as they only had five players reaching the 10 - point mark but neither scored over 15 points. Darren Collison, Glenn Robinson III and T.J. Leaf all had to lead the way on a Pacers squad that didn't have Victor Oladipo due to rest. Al Jefferson registered 12 points, while Domantas Sabonis came off the bench and finished with 10 points and 10 boards in 16 minutes.
