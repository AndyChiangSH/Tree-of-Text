The Milwaukee Bucks defeated the host Toronto Raptors, 104 - 99, at Scotiabank Arena on Sunday. In a battle between two of the Eastern Conference elite, this matchup wasn't decided until the final shot. In fact, it took eight ties and 14 lead changes to determine a winner, as neither club led by more than 11 points. The statistics were incredibly even, as these clubs tied in field goal percentage, rebounds and steals while coming within two of one another in assists and turnovers. Three-point shooting was the only noticeable difference for Milwaukee, as they connected on 15 - of - 39 while holding Toronto to 15 - of - 44. In addition, the Bucks won the points in the paint battle, 38 - 32, and the fastbreak scoring, 12 - 7. The Bucks (16 - 8) were led by Giannis Antetokounmpo, as he collected 19 points, 19 rebounds and six assists. Brook Lopez matched those 19 points, adding two assists and two steals to his tally. Malcolm Brogdon tallied 18 points, two rebounds and two assists. Khris Middleton finished with 10 points, nine rebounds and five assists. The Raptors (21 - 6) were led by Serge Ibaka, as he accrued 22 points, six rebounds, four assists, two steals and two blocks. Kawhi Leonard amassed 20 points, eight rebounds, four assists, two steals and two blocks. Pascal Siakam totaled 17 points, seven rebounds, two assists and three blocks. Fred VanVleet led the bench with 19 points, two rebounds and two assists. On deck for Milwaukee is a home matchup versus the Cleveland Cavaliers on Monday. As for Toronto, they return to action on Tuesday when they face the Los Angeles Clippers on the road.
