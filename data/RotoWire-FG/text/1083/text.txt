The Miami Heat defeated the Portland Trail Blazers, 118 - 108, at Moda Center on Tuesday evening. Tuesday's contest was shaping up to be a close one until the third quarter, when Dwyane Wade turned back the clock to help Miami (25 - 27) pull away. The future Hall of Famer scored eight points in the last four minutes of the quarter, extending the Heat's lead to as much as 15. The Trail Blazers (32 - 21) were unable to come back despite a valiant effort early in the fourth from Jake Layman and the second unit. Wade finished the win with 22 points and nine rebounds. While Wade anchored the second unit, the starters relied heavily on Hassan Whiteside. The star big man finished with a team - high 28 points on 11 - of - 12 shooting and also grabbed four steals and two blocks. As a whole, the Heat shot a whopping 54 percent from the floor, despite hitting only seven three - pointers. Portland, meanwhile, saw a game - high 33 points from C.J. McCollum. McCollum's strong night helped to partially offset a poor shooting performance from Damian Lillard, who went just 5 - of - 15 from the field in the loss. Off the bench, Jake Layman pitched in another 25 points and eight rebounds. Portland will suit up again on Thursday as the Trail Blazers play host to the San Antonio Spurs. Miami, meanwhile, will continue its West Coast road trip with a visit to Sacramento on Friday.
