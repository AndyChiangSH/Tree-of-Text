The Golden State Warriors defeated the Denver Nuggets, 116 - 102, at Oracle Arena on Tuesday. Tuesday's tilt had big stakes, as the Nuggets (51 - 26) entered the contest just one game behind the Warriors (53 - 24) for first place in the West. Golden State made quick work of the competition, though, hopping out to an early double - digit lead and never looking back. Denver trailed by double - digits for the entirety of the second half, shooting only 38 percent from the floor en route to the blowout loss. With their win, the Warriors now have a two game lead for first in the West with just five games remaining. The Warriors were led by 28 points and 12 rebounds from All-Star center DeMarcus Cousins. Kevin Durant tacked on another 21 points as the Warriors shot a blistering 54 percent from the floor in the win. Draymond Green finished with nine rebounds and nine assists, and five Warriors in total scored in double figures. Jamal Murray scored 17 points for Denver, making him the lone Nuggets player to score more than 11 points. Superstar big man Nikola Jokic was limited to 4 - of - 10 shooting and six turnovers. The team as a whole hit only seven three - pointers and committed 15 turnovers. Denver will host San Antonio on Wednesday, while the Warriors take on the Lakers in LA on Thursday.
