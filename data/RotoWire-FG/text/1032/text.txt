The Houston Rockets defeated the visiting San Antonio Spurs, 108 - 101, at Toyota Center on Saturday evening. The contest got out to a competitive start, with the home team taking a two - point advantage into the second quarter. The Spurs were able to grind their way back and claim a two - point lead by halftime, after a low - scoring second quarter. However, the Rockets went off for 43 points in the third to amass a 15 - point lead heading into the final frame. Despite a strong push from the visitors in the fourth, the Rockets were able to hold on for the much - needed win, while the Spurs saw their three - game winning streak come to an end. James Harden led the Rockets with 39 points, 10 assists, four rebounds and two steals, in 41 minutes. Clint Capela had a big night with 21 points, 23 rebounds, two assists and a block, while P.J. Tucker tallied six points, 16 rebounds, three assists and one steal. Eric Gordon added 18 points, six assists, two rebounds and a block. Meanwhile, DeMar DeRozan led the Spurs with 28 points, eight assists, seven rebounds, two blocks and one steal, in 37 minutes. LaMarcus Aldridge added 18 points, six rebounds and one block, while Rudy Gay tallied 13 points, eight rebounds and five assists, in 34 minutes.
