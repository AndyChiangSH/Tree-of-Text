The Houston Rockets defeated the host Utah Jazz, 112 - 101, at Vivint Smart Home Arena on Thursday evening. Despite the Jazz's strong home record, the Rockets came into Utah and took control from the start. The Rockets led 25 - 20 after one quarter and carried the five - point lead to halftime, where they led 51 - 46. Houston came out firing on all cylinders in the third, where they outscored the Jazz 37 - 20, to take an 88 - 66 lead into the final frame. The Rockets extended their lead to 24 points, before the Jazz took to a 90 run to make things interesting, however the Rockets pulled away for the win. This marks the eighth straight win for the Rockets, coinciding with Chris Paul's return to the lineup. Paul was stellar in this one, with 18 points, 13 assists, nine rebounds, three steals and one block, in 36 minutes. James Harden finished with 29 points on 8 - of - 19 shooting, five rebounds, three assists and one steal, in 35 minutes. Ryan Anderson added 23 points, six rebounds, two assists and one steal, in 35 minutes. The Rockets shot 47 percent from the field, including 18 - of - 41 from long range. Meanwhile, Donovan Mitchell led the Jazz with 26 points on 9 - of - 19 shooting, four rebounds, four assists and one steal, in 35 minutes. Alec Burks followed up with 17 points, while Thabo Sefolosha chipped in 14 points off the bench. The Jazz shot 44 percent from the field, including 11 - of - 30 from long range. The Jazz (13 - 13) go on to take on the Bucks in Milwaukee on Saturday. The Rockets (19 - 4) visit Portland to take on the Trail Blazers on Saturday.
