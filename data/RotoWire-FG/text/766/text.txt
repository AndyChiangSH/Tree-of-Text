The Cleveland Cavaliers defeated the host Milwaukee Bucks, 116 - 97, at BMO Harris Bradley Center on Friday evening. The Cavs (20) used a great team effort to overcome a strong attack from the Bucks (1 - 1) in Milwaukee. Six players scored in double digits for Cleveland, including four of the five starters. The Cavs shot 54 percent from the field, including 42 percent from long range, and went 17 - of - 17 from the foul line. On the other hand, the Bucks hit 46 percent from the field, and 31 percent from long range. LeBron James led the way in the win with 24 points on 10 - of - 16 shooting, eight assists, five rebounds, a block and a steal, in 37 minutes. Kyle Korver chipped in 17 points on 6 - of - 8 shooting, including 5 - of - 6 from long range, in 19 minutes off the bench. Kevin Love totaled 17 points, to go along with 12 rebounds, six assists and two blocks. Meanwhile, Giannis Antetokounmpo did all he could to keep his team in the game, finishing with 34 points, eight assists, eight rebounds, three steals and one block, in 39 minutes. The only other player to score in double digits for Milwaukee was Malcolm Brogdon, who finished with 16 points on 6 - of - 12 shooting. The Bucks will be right back in action, as they play host to the Trail Blazers on Saturday. The Cavaliers will also be in action on Saturday, when they play host to the Magic.
