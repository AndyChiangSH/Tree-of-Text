The New York Knicks defeated the host Miami Heat, 98 - 94, at American Airlines Arena on Friday evening. Despite playing without Carmelo Anthony (lower back), Derrick Rose (knee), or Lance Thomas (hip), the Knicks (29 - 47) managed to steal one from one of the hottest teams in the league. The Heat (37 - 39) started out with a 33 - 29 first quarter and stayed with it the whole way, holding the Heat off in the final minutes, as they made a strong push. The Heat had a chance to tie it up with just 12 seconds remaining, however they were called for offensive basket interference on the play, costing them the game. Kristaps Porzingis led the way for the Knicks, with 22 points on 10 - of - 18 shooting, seven rebounds, three assists and a block, in 36 minutes. Courtney Lee followed up with 20 points, while Justin Holiday added 12 off the bench. Willy Hernangomez finished with 10 points 12 rebounds. Ron Baker also chipped in 10 points, and dished six assists, with only one turnover, as he got the start at the point. The Knicks have been eliminated from playoff contention, but this win is a good confidence booster for some of the young guys. They go on play host to the Celtics on Sunday. Meanwhile, the Heat shot only 41 percent from the field, including 24 percent from long range, as they could not find their rhythm against the feisty Knicks. Goran Dragic led the Heat with 22 points and five assists. Hassan Whiteside followed up with 17 points and 16 rebounds, while Tyler Johnson added 15 points off the bench. James Johnson shot only 3 - of - 10 from the field, on his way to 12 points, in 27 minutes off the bench. The Heat will have to look to their next contest on Sunday, when they play host to the Nuggets, to solidify their spot in the top eight.
