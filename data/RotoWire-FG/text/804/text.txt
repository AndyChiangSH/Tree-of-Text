The Houston Rockets defeated the visiting New York Knicks, 117 - 102, at Toyota Center on Saturday evening. The Rockets (15 - 4) came from behind in this one, and delivered a great performance in the second half to come out on top against a Knicks (10 - 9) that just couldn't keep up with Houston's intensity on both ends of the court. The Knicks stormed out of nowhere to build a 39 - 29 lead in the first half and maintained a 61 - 58 at the break. But the Rockets flipped the script in the third quarter, and put themselves on top with a 37 - 13 win in the subsequent 12 minutes. Houston never looked back, and ended cruising their way to a huge win at home. James Harden delivered another impressive performance, and led Houston's offense with 37 points and 10 assists across 37 minutes. Clint Capela added a double - double of 12 points with 15 rebounds, and Trevor Ariza contributed with 16 points. Chris Paul only made two of his 12 field - goal attempts on route to a six - point performance, but he faciliated the ball as evidenced by his 13 assists and three steals. Ryan Anderson added 17 points and seven boards off the bench. New York didn't have Kristaps Porzingis in this game due to injury, so two unfamiliar names led the Knicks' offense in this one. Michael Beasley had one of his best games in a long time on route to a 30 - point, eight - rebound performance, and Kyle O'Quinn added 20 points, 15 boards, and four assists. Tim Hardaway Jr. didn't have his best game either, and he only finished with 11 points while going 4 - for - 12 from the field.
