The Charlotte Hornets defeated the visiting Cleveland Cavaliers, 110 - 99, at Spectrum Center on Wednesday. This was a tale of two halves, as Cleveland held the lead for the entirety of the first half while Charlotte held the lead for the majority of the second half. In fact, the Cavaliers rode a 25 - 16 first quarter to that lead but the Hornets responded with a 41 - 29 third quarter to take control. Rebounding was the difference in the game, with Charlotte outrebounding Cleveland, 51 - 35. The Hornets ended up shooting 51 percent from the field while holding Cleveland to 48 percent shooting. The Hornets did a ton of damage down low, outscoring the Cavaliers in the paint, 60 - 42. The Cavaliers (8 - 24) leading scorer came off the bench, as Jordan Clarkson led the team with 20 points, three rebounds and two assists. Cedi Osman led the starters with 13 points, two rebounds and two assists. Larry Nance Jr. was their best player, collecting 10 points, 15 rebounds, seven assists and two steals. The Hornets (15 - 15) were led by Kemba Walker, as he tallied 30 points, three rebounds, six assists and two steals. Marvin Williams amassed 18 points, 10 rebounds and two assists. Jeremy Lamb accrued 18 points, 12 rebounds and five assists. Michael Kidd-Gilchrist was a nice spark off the bench, providing 12 points, eight rebounds, two assists, two steals and two blocks. On deck for Charlotte is a home matchup versus the Detroit Pistons on Friday. Cleveland also returns to action on Friday, as they travel to play the Toronto Raptors.
