The Memphis Grizzlies defeated the Dallas Mavericks, 99 - 90, at FedEx Forum on Friday. Memphis were expected to win this game easily, but Dallas made a late run to make a respectable scoreline. It was a near wire - to - wire win for the Grizzlies, as Dallas' only lead came in the first quarter, with it being a one - point lead for less than a minute. The second quarter was the difference, with Memphis winning that quarter, 34 - 15. Rebounding was a big factor for the Grizzlies, as they won that battle, 47 - 38. Dallas couldn't get anything going on offense either, shooting 38 percent from the field and 35 percent from three - point range. The Mavericks (31 - 44) playoff hopes have been steadily slipping away and this loss may be the nail in the coffin. Despite shooting just 3 - of - 17 from the field, Dirk Nowitzki led Dallas with 13 points and 12 rebounds. Wesley Matthews matched those 13 points, adding three rebounds and seven assist to his tally. J.J. Barea led the bench, as he provided 13 points and five assists. The Grizzlies (42 - 34) have now followed up two wins after four - straight losses, as they appear to be out of that slump. Mike Conley was the star, as he collected 28 points, five rebounds and six assists. No other starter had more than nine points. Zach Randolph finished second on the team, providing 22 points, 12 rebounds and three assists off the bench. Troy Daniels also played well off the bench, dropping 21 points. Vince Carter went just 1 - of - 7 from the field, but still accrued three points, eight rebounds and five assists.
