The New Orleans Pelicans defeated the Sacramento Kings, 117 - 89, at Smoothie King Center on Friday. This was a revenge game for many players, but the Pelicans made minced meat of the Kings. It was as dominating as it could be, with New Orleans holding a double - digit lead for the final three quarters. Defense was key, as the Kings were held to 39 percent shooting, while committing 22 turnovers. New Orleans ended up shooting 48 percent, while committing just 12 turnovers. The 30 combined steals and blocks for the Pelicans marked a season - high as well. as they also dominated the points in the paint (60 - 38) and fastbreak differential (27 - 10). The Kings (29 - 47) had a tough task here playing against their former franchise player and they simply didn't have enough to compete in this matchup. Ben McLemore led the team in scoring off the bench, as he provided 15 points, eight rebounds and four steals. Willie Cauley-Stein recorded a double - double, amassing 11 points and 14 rebounds. Skal Labissiere accrued 13 points and eight rebounds. Buddy Hield produced 13 points, eight rebounds and three assists. On deck for Sacramento is a road matchup versus the Minnesota Timberwolves on Saturday. The Pelicans (33 - 43) wanted to show up for their big trade deadline acquisition against his former team and they did just that in this dominating win. DeMarcus Cousins abused his former team, totaling 37 points, 13 rebounds, four assists, three steals and two blocks. Anthony Dais finished with 19 points, 12 rebounds, three assists, two steals and five blocks. Jrue Holiday collected four points, five rebounds, eight assists, three steals and two blocks. Jordan Crawford, Dante Cunningham and E'Twaun Moore all scored between 12 and 13 points off the bench. New Orleans will look to keep rolling on Sunday in a home matchup against the Chicago Bulls.
