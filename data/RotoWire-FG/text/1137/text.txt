The Toronto Raptors defeated the visiting Philadelphia 76ers, 108 - 95, in Game 1 of the Eastern Conference semi finals, at Scotiabank Arena in Saturday evening. The Raptors got out to a great start at home, with a 39 - 31 lead after one quarter. The Sixers picked up the fight int the second but were still outscored by one in the quarter, as the Raptors took a nine - point lead into halftime. The 76ers jumped out to a good start in the third and cut the deficit to four, however, the Raptors dug in and pulled back ahead, taking a 92 - 81 lead into the fourth. The Raptors controlled the pace in the fourth, as they pushed their lead to 20 and cruised through the rest of the quarter to the Game 1 win. Kawhi Leonard led the Raptors with 45 points, 11 rebounds, two assists, two steals and a block, in 38 minutes. Pascal Siakam followed up with 29 points, seven rebounds and one assist, while Kyle Lowry finished with nine points, eight assists, three rebounds, a steal and a block. The Raptors shot 52 percent from the field, including 9 - of - 27 from deep. Meanwhile, Tobias Harris tallied 14 points, 15 rebounds, six assists and a steal, in 38 minutes for the 76ers. Joel Embiid was held to 16 points, eight rebounds and two assists, while J.J. Redick added 17 points, two rebounds, two assists and a block. The Sixers shot 39 percent from the field, including 11 - of - 32 from long range. Game 2 will take place in Toronto on Monday.
