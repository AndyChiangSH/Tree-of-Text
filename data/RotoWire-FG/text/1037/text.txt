The Indiana Pacers defeated the Detroit Pistons, 125 - 88, at Bankers Life Fieldhouse on Friday evening. The Pacers (24 - 12) set the tone for what would be a dominant win early, forging a 32 - 21 first - quarter lead. The Pistons (16 - 17) couldn't make any serious inroads into their deficit after that point, and Indiana's pinpoint shooting had a lot to do with it. The home squad shot an eye - popping 60 percent overall, with seven players hitting double digits in the scoring column. They were nearly as impressive on the defensive end, as they limited Detroit to 37 percent shooting - - including 25 percent from three - point range - - and forced them into 22 turnovers. Blake Griffin led the way for the Pistons with 18 points, a total he supplemented with seven rebounds and five assists. Luke Kennard followed with a bench - leading 14 points and added two rebounds and one steal. Reggie Bullock poured in 13 points and added three boards, one steal and one block. Andre Drummond generated a 12 - point, 12 - rebound double - double that also included three blocks and one steal. Reggie Jackson mustered eight points, six assists and four rebounds. Darren Collison co - led the Pacers with 19 points and also provided seven assists, three rebounds and two steals. Domantas Sabonis matched Collison's scoring total and also pulled down 12 rebounds, dished out three assists and recorded one block. Myles Turner and Thaddeus Young had matching 17 - point tallies. Bojan Bogdanovic supplied 12 points, five rebounds and two assists. Doug McDermott had 12 of his own and also posted three boards. The Pistons remain on the road and tangle with the Orlando Magic on Sunday afternoon, while the Pacers remain home and face the Atlanta Hawks on Sunday afternoon as well.
