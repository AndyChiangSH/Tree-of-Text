The Milwaukee Bucks defeated the visiting Indiana Pacers, 117 - 98, at Fiserv Forum on Thursday. While Indiana kept this game close in the first half, Milwaukee ran away in the second half. In fact, the Bucks rode a 130 run near the end of the third quarter to take a game - high 16 - point lead, then scored 13 unanswered points early in the fourth quarter to capture a new game - high 22 - point lead. Those runs were simply too much to overcome for the Pacers, as the Bucks ended up outscoring them, 63 - 48, in the second half. Milwaukee ended up leading for all but two minutes, as Indiana's largest lead was a single point. Rebounding was key, with the Bucks outrebounding the Pacers, 51 - 39. The shooting disparity was also decisive, with Milwaukee shooting 50 percent from the field while holding the Pacers to just 39 percent shooting. The Pacers (42 - 24) were led by Myles Turner, as he tallied 22 points, 17 rebounds, three assists and three blocks. Bojan Bogdanovic amassed 17 points, five rebounds and two steals. Thaddeus Young accrued 10 points, two rebounds, four assists, two steals and two blocks while Darren Collison totaled nine points and nine assists. The Bucks (49 - 16) were led by Giannis Antetokounmpo, as he collected 29 points, 12 rebounds and five assists. Khris Middleton accumulated 27 points, two rebounds, four assists and three steals. Brook Lopez finished with 13 points, six rebounds and four blocks while Eric Bledsoe accrued 12 points, six rebounds and four assists. On deck for Milwaukee is a home matchup versus the Charlotte Hornets on Saturday. As for Charlotte, they're off until Monday when they travel to face the Houston Rockets.
