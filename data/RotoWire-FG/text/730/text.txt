The Milwaukee Bucks defeated the Detroit Pistons in overtime, 108 - 105, at BMO Harris Bradley Center on Friday evening. The Bucks (40 - 36) allowed the Pistons (35 - 42) to climb back into the game after they'd ambushed by a 35 - 22 margin in the first quarter. Detroit came back to outscore their hosts, 51 - 36, over the second and third periods, and then led by as much as 91 - 81 after a Kentavious Caldwell-Pope three - pointer with 5:9 remaining. However, an 80 run by Milwaukee over the subsequent 2:11 closed the gap to 91 - 89 and a Thon Maker basket with 51 seconds left tied the game at 95, eventually leading to overtime. A see - saw extra period saw the game knotted the game at 102 with as little as 1:22 remaining, but Khris Middleton's 25 - foot three - point jumper at the 40 - second mark began a game - clinching 60 run for the Bucks. Detroit was led by Tobias Harris, who posted 23 points, seven rebounds, three assists and one steal off the bench. Caldwell-Pope followed with 17 points, eight boards, two assists and two steals. Ish Smith and Beno Udrih contributed matching 16 - point efforts from the starting five and second unit, respectively, with the former adding five assists and four rebounds and the latter providing eight assists, one rebound and two steals. Maurice Morris supplied 13 points, four boards and three assists. Jon Leuer posted nine points and 10 rebounds, while Andre Drummond compiled seven points, 13 boards, two assists, two steals and two blocks. Giannis Antetokunumpo spearheaded the Bucks attack with 28 points, 14 rebounds, nine assists, two blocks and one steal. Middleton was right behind him with a 25 - point, 10 - rebound double - double that also included one assist and one steal. Maker exploded for a career - high 23 points, which he supplemented with two rebounds and one steal. Matthew Dellavedova drew the start for Malcolm Brogdon (back) and totaled six points, seven assists and three rebounds. The Pistons are now off until next Wednesday evening, when they take on the Toronto Raptors at home. The Bucks remain home for a Sunday afternoon showdown with the Dallas Mavericks.
