The Detroit Pistons defeated the Memphis Grizzlies, 100 - 93, at Little Caesars Arena on Tuesday night. The Pistons (40 - 41) came into the contest with the utmost urgency, knowing they'd need to win their last two games to control their own destiny and hang on to the eighth and final playoff spot in the Eastern Conference. It was therefore rather surprising to see Detroit get off to a poor start, as they were behind by a 32 - 20 margin after one quarter and 57 - 38 at halftime. They finally seemed to awaken to the reality of their circumstances coming out of intermission by cutting four points in the Grizzlies' (32 - 49) advantage during the third quarter, but it was a monumental 21 - 4 run over the first 5:58 of the final period that finally gave them a lead for the first time since 11:44 mark of the first quarter. Although it held Detroit to just 40 percent shooting, Memphis hurt its cause by committing 21 turnovers. Delon Wright and Bruno Caboclo co - led the Grizzlies with 15 points, with Wright notably pulling down nine rebounds. Tyler Zeller totaled 13 points, six rebounds, three assists and two blocks. Tyler Dorsey generated 12 points, four rebounds and three assists. Ivan Rabb, Chandler Parsons and Jevon Carter had matching nine - point tallies off the bench. Ish Smith led the Pistons with 22 points, adding four assists and three rebounds. Andre Drummond posted a 20 - point, 17 - rebound double - double that also included one assist, five steals and three blocks. Luke Kennard contributed 15 points, two rebounds and one assist. Reggie Jackson supplied 12 points, five assists, one rebound and one steal. Blake Griffin, whose workload is being managed due to knee soreness, posted just five points, two rebounds, one assist, one steal and one block while logging 18 minutes. The Grizzlies wrap up the season by hosting the Golden State Warriors on Wednesday night, while the Pistons visit the New York Knicks on Wednesday evening in another must - win game.
