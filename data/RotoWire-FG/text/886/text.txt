The Phoenix Suns took down the Dallas Mavericks, 102 - 88, at Talking Stick Resort Arena on Wednesday. While Phoenix (18 - 34) earned a much - needed quality win, the story of the game was centered around a gruesome lower leg injury to one of the Suns' players. Play stopped in the second quarter when Phoenix's Isaiah Canaan hit the floor hard and appeared to break his left ankle. Canaan was eventually helped off the floor and exited the game after only five minutes of play. Though the injury rattled players on both teams, the Suns were still able to come away with a win. They jumped out to a double - digit lead in the first 10 minutes of the game and managed to remain ahead for the remainder of regulation. Rookie forward Josh Jackson continued his strong stretch of recent play, leading the team with 21 points on 9 - of - 14 shooting. Dallas (16 - 36) is in a tough spot following the loss. The team has now gone just 1 - 8 in its last nine contests. Wednesday's loss was in part due to poor shooting from the Mavericks. As a whole, the team shot just over 36 percent from the field and went only 14 - of - 26 from the free - throw line. The Mavericks were also led by a rookie, as Dennis Smith, Jr. scored a team - high 17 points in the loss. Phoenix will host the Utah Jazz on Friday, while Dallas will travel to take on The Sacramento Kings on Saturday.
