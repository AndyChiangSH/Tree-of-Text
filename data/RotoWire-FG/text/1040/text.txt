The Utah Jazz defeated the visiting New York Knicks, 129 - 97, at Vivint Smart Home Arena on Saturday. Results like this can usually be deceiving by the lopsided scoreline but that wasn't the case here with this dominating effort by the Jazz. In fact, Utah set the tone with a 39 - 17 first quarter, as they led by at least 20 points for the final three quarters in their wire - to - wire win. Rebounding was a huge factor, with Utah winning that battle, 60 - 35. The Jazz also connected on 55 percent of their shots while holding the Knicks to 37 percent shooting. Ball movement played a big factor in that, with Utah recording 30 assists and New York handing out just 17. Not to mention, the Jazz outscored the Knicks in the paint, 70 - 26. The Knicks (9 - 28) were led by Tim Hardaway Jr, as he totaled 18 points and two rebounds. Luke Kornet was their best player, as he collected 14 points, six rebounds and five assists. Noah Vonleh finished with 13 points, nine rebounds and three steals. Emmanuel Mudiay amassed 13 points and five assists. The Jazz (18 - 19) got a gem from Rudy Gobert, as he accrued 25 points and 16 rebounds on 10 - of - 12 from the field. Donovan Mitchell provided 15 points, five rebounds and three assists. Dante Exum got a rare start and matched his 13 points with a career - high 13 assists. Derrick Favors generated 12 points, 10 rebounds and three assists. Thabo Sefolosha led the bench with 10 points, 10 rebounds and three steals. Both teams are off until Tuesday when they face elite competition, with New York traveling to face the Denver Nuggets and Utah playing the Toronto Raptors on the road as well.
