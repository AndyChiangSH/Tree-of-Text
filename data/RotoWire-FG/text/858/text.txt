The Milwaukee Bucks defeated the host Washington Wizards, 110 - 103, at Verizon Center on Saturday. This was a tightly - fought battle, as there were 11 ties and 13 lead changes in total, with neither team ever leading by more than 11 points. Milwaukee had to pull off their win in comeback fashion, as they outscored Washington, 28 - 18, in the fourth quarter. In addition, the Bucks ended the game on a 14 - 4 run across the final three minutes. Despite the fact that Milwaukee is the worst rebounding team in the league, they ended up controlling the glass here, outrebounding the Wizards, 45 - 34. Milwaukee was also much better at taking care of the ball, as they recorded 11 steals, while Washington only mustered up six. The Bucks (21 - 17) got a near triple - double out of Giannis Antetokoumpo, as he collected 34 points, 12 rebounds and seven assists on 12 - of - 17 from the field. Eric Bledsoe finished with 21 points, four rebounds, four assists and three steals. Khris Middleton accrued 20 points and five rebounds. John Henson amassed 11 points and 11 rebounds en route to a double - double. The Wizards (23 - 17) were led by Bradley Beal, as he tallied 20 points, five rebounds and two blocks. John Wall accumulated 16 points, three rebounds and 16 assists, Marcin Gortat totaled 17 points, seven rebounds and three blocks. Markieff Morris provided 14 points and 10 rebounds. On deck for Milwaukee is a road matchup versus the Indiana Pacers on Monday. As for Washington, they're off until Wednesday when they play host to the Utah Jazz.
