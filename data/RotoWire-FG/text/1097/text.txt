The Chicago Bulls defeated the Boston Celtics, 126 - 116, at United Center on Saturday night. The Bulls (16 - 44) were riding high after a hard - fought one - point road victory Friday against the Magic, but they had an exponentially difficult challenge just 24 hours later in the form of the Celtics (37 - 23). Matters looked a bit worrisome for Chicago early, as Boston forged a five - point lead after 12 minutes. However, the visitors never saw what hit them in the second, as the Bulls doubled them up by a 36 - 18 margin in the second to carry a 13 - point advantage into halftime. After both teams matched each other bucket for bucket in the third, the hosts put the final nail in the proverbial coffin with a 32 - point fourth quarter, ultimately allowing the Celtics to cut three points into their lead. Both teams shot well, but it was Chicago that dominated the boards with a 49 - 32 rebounding advantage. Kyrie Irving paced the Celtics with 37 points, adding 10 assists, five rebounds, one steal and one block. Al Horford contributed 19 points, three assists, two rebounds and one block. Jaylen Brown registered 14 points, three rebounds and two steals. Jayson Tatum and Marcus Morris contributed 12 points apiece, with the latter notably hauling in six boards as well. Zach LaVine led the Bulls with 42 points and supplemented them with six rebounds and four assists. Lauri Markkanen turned in 35 points, 15 rebounds, two assists and one steal. Robin Lopez collected 17 points, six boards and four assists. Wayne Selden saw extra playing time after Otto Porter, Jr. exited early with a leg injury, totaling nine points, six rebounds, two assists and one block. The Celtics remain on the road and next face off with their biggest Eastern Conference rival in the Toronto Raptors on Tuesday night. The Bulls remain home and tangle with the Milwaukee Bucks on Monday evening.
