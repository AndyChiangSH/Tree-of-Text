The Portland Trail Blazers defeated the visiting Miami Heat, 115 - 99, at Moda Center on Monday evening. The Trail Blazers got out to a good start, as they led for most of the first quarter, before they went on an 11 - 2 to end the quarter and take a 34 - 25 lead into the second. They stayed ahead to start the second and pushed their lead to 12 mid way through the quarter, and held on to a take a 12 - point, 59 - 47 lead into halftime. Portland came out strong again in the third quarter, where they outscored the Heat29-25, to push their lead to 88 - 72 entering the fourth. The Heat battled back to make it just a three - point game mid way through the final frame, but the Blazers quickly pushed the lead back to double digits and cruised to their 10th straight win. Damian Lillard led the way for Portland with 32 points, 10 assists and five rebounds, in 37 minutes. Jusuf Nurkic followed up with 27 points, 16 rebounds, three assists and three blocks, while C.J. McCollum added 17 points, five assists, two rebounds, one steal and one block. The Trail Blazers hot 46 percent from the field, including 14 - of - 36 from long range. Meanwhile, Goran Dragic led the Heat with 23 points, two rebounds, two assists and one steal, in 30 minutes. Justise Winslow tallied 15 points, 13 rebounds four assists and two blocks, in 29 minutes off the bench. Tyler Johnson finished with 17 points, three rebounds and two assists. The Heat shot 43 percent from the field, including 10 - of - 31 from long range. The Heat (36 - 32) will look to bounce back as they take on the Kings in Sacramento on Wednesday. The Trail Blazers (41 - 26) will look to keep rolling as they play host to the Cavs on Thursday.
