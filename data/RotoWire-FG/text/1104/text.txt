The Los Angeles Clippers took down their rival Los Angeles Lakers, 113 - 105, at Staples Center on Monday evening. The Lakers (30 - 34) entered Monday's contest in desperate need of a win. The LeBron James-led team had dropped its previous two contests and was five games out of the playoff picture heading into Monday's crosstown clash. While the Lakers seemed ready to play initially, putting up 34 points in the first quarter, the team's energy quickly began to wane. The Clippers (37 - 29) were able to regain the lead by halftime before building up a comfortable cushion in the second half. LAC started the fourth quarter with a quick 80 run to push the lead to 12, and over four minutes of scoreless basketball from the Lakers allowed the Clips to maintain their advantage. The outcome lifts the Clippers to seventh in the Western Conference while moving the Lakers to 6 games out from eighth place and the coveted final playoff spot. Early on, the Clippers saw a massive spark from Patrick Beverley. The veteran embraced the mismatch with his coverage of LeBron James and finished the first half with five steals. Danilo Gallinari, meanwhile, helped to fuel the Clippers' offensive outburst in the second half. He finished with a team - high 23 points on 9 - of - 15 shooting. The Lakers, meanwhile, saw a triple - double from veteran Rajon Rondo. Rondo, who has added a three - point shot recently, knocked down four triples on the night. James also delivered a gem, scoring 27 points to go with eight rebounds and six assists. Next on the agenda for the Lakers is a tilt with the Denver Nuggets at Staples Center on Wednesday. The Clippers, meanwhile, will host the Oklahoma City Thunder on Friday.
