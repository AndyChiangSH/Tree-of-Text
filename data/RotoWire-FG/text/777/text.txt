The host Detroit Pistons defeated the Milwaukee Bucks, 105 - 96, at Little Caesars Arena on Friday. Milwaukee has now lost four of their last five games, as they ran into a hot Detroit team here. The second quarter was decisive for the Pistons, as they outscored the Bucks, 31 - 22. No other quarter had a margin of more than two points. Another key for the Pistons was their ability to get to the free - throw line, as they went 23 - of - 28, while holding Milwaukee to just 8 - of - 9. The Bucks (4 - 5) got another gem out of Giannis Antetokounmpo, as he tallied 29 points, four rebounds, three assists and four blocks. Malcolm Brogdon amassed 21 points and 10 assists. Khris Middleton totaled 17 points, seven rebounds and seven assists. The Pistons (6 - 3) were led by Andre Drummond, who amassed 24 points and 15 rebounds, while going 14 - of - 16 from the free - throw line. Avery Bradley collected 23 points, two rebounds, two assists and three steals. Reggie Jackson totaled nine points, three rebounds and eight assists. Detroit's next game will be on Saturday, as they host the Sacramento Kings. Milwaukee is off until Tuesday, when they travel to play the Cleveland Cavaliers.
