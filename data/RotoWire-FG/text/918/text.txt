The host Sacramento Kings took down the New York Knicks, 102 - 99, in a last - second thriller at Golden 1 Center on Sunday evening. The game came down to the wire after New York (24 - 40) started the fourth quarter on an impressive run. After out - scoring Sacramento 27 - 16 through the first 11 minutes of the fourth, the Knicks had the game tied at 99 - 99. Sacramento (20 - 44), though, had possession, and sophomore big man Skal Labissiere was able to make the most of it. Labissiere received the inbounds pass with five seconds remaining and stepped into a three - pointer, knocking the shot down to win the game with 2 seconds remaining. In addition to his clutch shot, Labissiere had 14 points, six rebounds, and three blocks. He shot 5 - of - 10 and added to what was a relatively efficient offensive night for the Kings. Rookie guard Bogdan Bogdanovic, though, was the driving force for the Sacramento offense. He led the team with 22 points on 7 - of - 11 shooting and had seven assists while committing no turnovers. The Knicks, meanwhile, have now lost 12 of their last 13 games. Since All-Star big man Kristaps Porzingis went down with an ACL tear, the team has been among the league's worst. On Sunday, the team struggled to hit from three - point range. New York went just 6 - of - 21 from beyond the arc. Tim Hardaway, Jr. was the team's leading scorer with 24 points. The Knicks will visit Portland on Tuesday, while Sacramento will host the New Orleans Pelicans on Wednesday.
