The San Antonio Spurs defeated the Memphis Grizzlies, 108 - 88, at AT & T Center on Saturday night. The Grizzlies (16 - 21) came out ready to match the Spurs (23 - 17) basket for basket, and they trailed just 27 - 25 after one quarter. However, the game turned in the second, as San Antonio outscored Memphis by a 37 - 19 margin. Armed with a 20 - point advantage heading into the third quarter, the home squad essentially held serve in the second half to churn out the comfortable victory. Mike Conley paced the Grizzlies with 21 points, adding three assists and one rebound. Jaren Jackson, Jr. followed with 15 points, seven rebounds, one assist and four steals. Dillon Brooks matched Jackson's scoring total. No other Memphis player managed double - digit scoring, but JaMychal Green supplied eight points, 10 rebounds and one steal off the bench. Kyle Anderson and Garrett Temple had seven points each. Derrick White paced the Spurs with 19 points and supplemented them with four rebounds, two blocks and one steal. LaMarcus Aldridge posted 18 points, seven assists, two rebounds and two steals. Patty Mills generated 15 points, four assists, two rebounds and one steal. DeMar DeRozan totaled 12 points, nine rebounds, four assists and one steal. Jakob Poeltl turned in 11 points, six rebounds, two assists and one block. The Grizzlies remain on the road and visit the New Orleans Pelicans on Monday night, while the Spurs also travel and face the Detroit Pistons on Monday evening.
