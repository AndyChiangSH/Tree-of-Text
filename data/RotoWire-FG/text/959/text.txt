The Washington Wizards defeated the Boston Celtics, 113 - 101, at Capital One Arena on Tuesday evening. The Wizards (43 - 38) had plenty of motivation to notch a victory despite their already - secured playoff spot, as they were looking to snap a four - game losing streak and potentially tunnel their way up to the seventh seed. However, the Celtics (54 - 27), already locked into the No. 2 seed, came out of the gates hot, taking a 30 - 22 lead after one quarter. That was the highlight of their night, though, as they were outscored 91 - 71 over the remaining three periods. John Wall spearheaded the home squad's efforts, hitting the 5,0 - assist milestone as part of a team - leading 29 - point, 12 - assist double - double that also included seven rebounds, three steals and three blocks. Jaylen Brown paced the Celtics with 27 points, a total he supplemented with two assists, one rebound and two steals. Jayson Tatum supplied 12 points, three rebounds, two assists and four steals. Al Horford provided 10 points, 14 boards, three assists and one steal. Greg Monroe matched Horford's scoring total and added six rebounds, two assists and two steals. Terry Rozier finished with eight points, six assists and four rebounds but struggled through a 2 - for - 12 shooting night. Beal followed Wall with 19 points, four rebounds and four assists. Markieff Morris totaled 13 points, four rebounds and two assists. Otto Porter, Jr. managed 12 points, five rebounds, two assists and one steal but did not play the second half due to a leg injury. Mike Scott matched Porter's scoring total and also contributed eight boards and three assists. Kelly Oubre, Jr. accounted for 11 points, five boards, two assists and two steals. Tomas Satoransky produced seven points, five rebounds, five assists and one steal. The Celtics finish their season with a home date against the Brooklyn Nets on Wednesday night, while the Wizards visit the Orlando Magic in their season finale on Wednesday evening.
