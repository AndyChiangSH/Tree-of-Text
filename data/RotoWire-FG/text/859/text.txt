The Sacramento Kings took down the Denver Nuggets, 106 - 98, at Golden 1 Center on Saturday. The Kings (13 - 25) had been slipping a bit recently, having gone just 1 - 3 through the first four games of their current home stand. On Saturday, however, the team was able to turn its luck around. Recently-returned rookie De'Aaron Fox was dominant in his second game back from a quad injury. Fox led the team with 18 points and seven assists. Fox was one of seven Kings players to score in double digits on Saturday. One of those players, Willie Cauley-Stein, had 17 points and was a major factor on the defensive end. Cauley-Stein came away with seven steals, a new career high and the most by any Sacramento player this season. Cauley-Stein's active defense was a big reason for Denver's turnover issues. The Nuggets turned it over 25 times on the night. Denver (21 - 18) had issues managing the ball in what was the second half of a back - to - back for the young team. Three different Nuggets starters - Nikola Jokic, Jamal Murray, and Wilson Chandler - committed five turnovers on the night. Though Denver was able to match or exceed the Kings' shooting splits across the board, lost possessions due to turnovers wound up being the difference in the game. Jokic, Denver's star center, had an interesting night, as he led the team with 10 assists but struggled to crash the glass or find his shot. He went just 3 - of - 10 from the field en route to eight points and had only seven rebounds. The Nuggets will visit Golden State on Monday, while Sacramento will host the San Antonio Spurs.
