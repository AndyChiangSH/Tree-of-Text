The Detroit Pistons defeated the Phoenix Suns, 118 - 107, at Little Caesars Arena on Sunday afternoon. The Pistons (10 - 7) have now won two straight in the midst of a five - game homestand, and they did it while playing at a stellar level during the final 12 minutes of action against a Suns (4 - 15) team that simply couldn't hold on to their slim advantage. Phoenix jumped to a three - point lead after the first quarter (30 - 27), but the Pistons were in front at half time by a two - point margin (62 - 60). The Suns restored their lead and were by up by one after three (84 - 83) and it seemed as if they were going to pull the upset. However, Detroit sealed the deal with an impressive fourth - quarter performance (35 - 23) to win comfortably by an 11 - point differential. Six Detroit players scored in double digits in this game. Andre Drummond led the way with 19 points, 16 rebounds and five steals, while Reggie Jackson also contributed 19 points while adding eight assists. Blake Griffin posted a double - double with 16 points and 11 assists, and Reggie Bullock added 13 points. Langston Galloway (15 points) and Stanley Johnson (14 points) impressed off the bench as well. Devin Booker carried Phoenix's offense with a stellar 37 - point performance that also included five assists and four rebounds, but he didn't have a lot of help from his teammates. DeAndre Ayton chipped with a strong double - double of 25 points and 13 rebounds, while Jamal Crawford did his part with 12 points and five assists coming off the bench. Trevor Ariza disappointed with only five points in 36 minutes of action.
