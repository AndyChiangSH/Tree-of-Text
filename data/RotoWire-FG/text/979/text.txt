The Chicago Bulls defeated the host Atlanta Hawks, 97 - 85, at State Farm Arena on Saturday evening. The Hawks got out to a slow start, as it took them three minutes to score their first bucket, while thankfully for them, they only trailed 50. However, the Bulls stayed strong and pushed their lead to 10 - 1. Nonetheless, the Hawks responded with 24 - 12 run, to take a 25 - 22 lead by the end of the quarter. Things tightened up from there, as the teams stayed within two or three possessions for the length of the quarter, matching each other's 19 points, so that the Hawks still led by three at halftime. It remained a one - possession game for most of the third quarter, until the Bulls pulled away with a 9 - 6 run, which allowed them to take a four - point lead into the fourth. The Bulls kept up the momentum and pushed their lead to 10, just minutes into the quarter. They guarded their lead heavily from there, and did not let the Hawks get too close, as they closed out their first road win of the season. Zach LaVine led the way for the Bulls with 27 points, 11 rebounds, three assists and one steal, in 34 minutes. Jabari Parker followed up with 18 points, eight rebounds, three assists, two blocks and one steal, in 34 minutes off the bench. Ryan Arcidiacono continued his solid play with 13 points, seven assists, two rebounds, two steals and one block. The Bulls shot 43 percent from the field, including 12 - of - 33 from deep. Meanwhile, Taurean Prince led the Hawks with 16 points, five rebounds, three assists, two steals and one block, in 34 minutes. Trae Young finished with 13 points, four assists, three rebounds and two steals, while Dewayne Dedmon tallied 13 points, 13 rebounds and five blocks, in 26 minutes off the bench. The Hawks (2 - 3) will look to bounce back as they take on the Sixers in Philly on Monday. The Bulls (2 - 4) play host to the Warriors on Monday.
