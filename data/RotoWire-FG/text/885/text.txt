The Portland Trail Blazers defeated the Chicago Bulls, 124 - 108, at Moda Center on Wednesday. Portland (29 - 22) got off to an explosive start thanks to star guard C.J. McCollum. McCollum set a new franchise record with 28 points in the first quarter alone, finishing with 50 points overall. Thanks to McCollum's historic contribution, the Blazers finished the first quarter with a massive 22 - point lead. The hot start set the tone for the game, as Portland held a double - digit lead for the remainder of regulation. McCollum seemingly could not miss on Wednesday, as he went 18 - of - 25 from the field. He was the driving force of a strong Blazers offense, which shot 56 percent from the field in the game. Damian Lillard scored 13 points, but took only nine shots as McCollum was able to take the reigns to the offense in the blowout win. Chicago (18 - 33), meanwhile, could seemingly not stop Portland's perimeter attack early in the game. On offense, though, the team saw some positive developments. Zach LaVine led the team with 23 points on 8 - of - 13 shooting. This marked the end of a major slump for LaVine, who had shot just 5 - of - 28 over his previous two games. The team was also able to hit 13 three - pointers despite the absence of elite shooter Lauri Markkanen, who sat out the game for personal reasons. The Trail Blazers will visit Toronto on Friday, while the Bulls will take on the Los Angeles Clippers on Saturday.
