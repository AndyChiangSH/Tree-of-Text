The Portland Trail Blazers took down the Dallas Mavericks, 117 - 108, at Moda Center on Sunday. Portland's success began and ended with their backcourt on Sunday, as the Trail Blazers' (25 - 21) duo of starting guards combined to score 57 points in the win. Damian Lillard continued his All-Star push, scoring 31 points on 10 - of - 15 shooting while dishing out nine assists. C.J. McCollum, meanwhile, added 26 points and hit two three - pointers. While the game was never quite a blowout, Portland managed to keep Dallas at a comfortable distance late in the game. A late basket from Lillard and several free - throws helped keep the lead at eight or more points for the final four minutes. Dallas (15 - 31) got off to a rough start, allowing Portland to take a double - digit lead in the first quarter. Despite a stronger second half, Dallas was unable to close the gap by the game's end. Like the Blazers, the Mavericks relied heavily on their starters to provide offense. Three members of Dallas's starting five scored at least 21 points. Veteran wing Wesley Matthews led the team with 23, while aging star Dirk Nowitzki turned in a vintage performance with 21. The Mavericks will play host to the Washington Wizards on Monday. Portland, meanwhile, will see the Nuggets in Denver.
