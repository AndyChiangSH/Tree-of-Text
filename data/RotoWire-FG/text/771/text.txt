The Boston Celtics defeated the host Miami Heat, 96 - 90, at American Airlines Arena on Saturday evening. This marks the Celtics' fourth straight win, after they dropped their first two of the season. The Celtics led 74 - 63 after three quarters but the Heat gave them a scare with 70 run to bring it to a two - point game, with 2:16 left in the fourth. However, Kyrie Irving showed the poise of a champion as he poured in seven straight points for the Celtics, to seal the deal. Irving was the high - point man for the Celtics (4 - 2) with 24 points, on 10 - of - 23 shooting, along with four rebounds and three assists, in 33 minutes. Jayson Tatum had another impressive game, with 20 points and five rebounds, in 31 minutes. Al Horford was solid with 12 points, nine rebounds, three assists and one steal, while Marcus Smart chipped in 16 points, four rebounds and four assists, in 26 minutes off the bench. Meanwhile, Goran Dragic led the way for Miami (2 - 3) with 22 points, four assists, three rebounds and one block, in 36 minutes. Josh Richardson and James Johnson chipped in 16 points each, while Kelly Olynyk added 14 points, nine rebounds and three blocks, in 23 minutes off the bench. Justise Winslow hauled in 12 rebounds and contributed six points, three assists, two blocks and one steal, in 23 minutes. The Heat will look to bounce back as they play host to the Timberwolves on Monday. The Celtics play host to the Spurs on Monday.
