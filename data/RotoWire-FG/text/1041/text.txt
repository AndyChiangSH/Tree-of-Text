The Denver Nuggets defeated the host Phoenix Suns, 122 - 118, at Talking Stick Resort Arena on Saturday evening. The Nuggets got out to a great start on the road as they outscored the Suns 36 - 16 through the first quarter. The Suns picked up their effort in the second and outscored the visitors by seven, however they still trailed 62 - 49 at halftime. The Suns stayed strong in the third and again managed to outscore the Nuggets in the quarter, albeit by just one point, so that they trailed 90 - 78 entering the fourth. The Suns continued to fight and pushed right down to the wire, as they outscored the Nuggets by eight point in the quarter. Despite outscoring the Nuggets by 16 over the final three quarters, it was not enough to overcome the 20 - point deficit the Suns incurred in the first. Jamal Murray led the Nuggets with 46 points, eight assists, six rebounds and one block, in 39 minutes. Nikola Jokic followed up with 23 points, nine assists, eight rebounds, three steals and one block, while Malik Beasley added 15 points, two assists, two steals and one rebound. The Nuggets shot 55 percent from the field, including 18 - of - 35 from long range. Meanwhile, Deandre Ayton led the Suns with 33 points, 14 rebounds, four steals and one assist, in 39 minutes. Devin Booker followed up with 27 points, eight assists, one rebound and one steal, while T.J. Warren added 22 points, five steals, three rebounds, three assists and one block. The Suns (9 - 28) will look to bounce back as they play host to the Warriors on Monday. The Nuggets (23 - 11) play host to the Knicks on Tuesday.
