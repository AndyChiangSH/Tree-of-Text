The Brooklyn Nets defeated the Washington Wizards, 103 - 98, at Barclay's Center on Tuesday. In a battle between two teams near the bottom of the Eastern Conference, it came down to the final possessions. In fact, there were 10 ties and 21 lead changes in total, as it was a single - digit margin for all but a few minutes. In fact, it was within a five - point margin for the final eight minutes. Both teams really struggled offensively, as they both shot just 40 percent from the field and less than 25 percent from three - point range. Ball movement was key for Brooklyn, as they recorded 26 assists, while holding Washington to just 18 assists. A 44 - 37 defensive rebounding margin was key as well for the Nets. The Wizards (14 - 13) were led by Bradley Beal, as he tallied 28 points, two rebounds, four assists and two steals. Otto Porter was the only other starter in double figures, as he amassed 11 points, five rebounds and three assists. Kelly Oubre Jr. led the bench with 12 points and nine rebounds. The Nets (11 - 15) got a double - double out of Rondae Hollis-Jefferson, as he totaled 16 points and 12 rebounds. DeMarre Carroll accrued 15 points and eight rebounds. Allen Crabbe went just 3 - of - 15 from the field, but still finished with 13 points, five rebounds and three assists. Spencer Dinwiddie collected 11 points, six rebounds, 12 assists and two steals. Caris LeVert led the bench with 16 points, three rebounds and eight assists.
