The Toronto Raptors defeated the Indiana Pacers, 120 - 115, at Air Canada Center on Friday. This marks Toronto's seventh - straight home win, as they continue to succeed north of the border. Despite the fact that it was close throughout the first half with 12 lead changes, the Raptors actually led for the entirety of the second half. Ball movement was a huge factor, with Toronto out - assisting Indiana, 29 - 21. The biggest difference in the game was scoring in the paint though, with Toronto getting 70 paint points to Indiana's 44. The Pacers (12 - 11) got another gem from Victor Oladipo, as he scored a season - high 36 points on 14 - of - 22 shooting. He also added seven rebounds, six assists, three steals and three blocks to his tally. Myles Turner amassed 17 points and six rebounds. Darren Collison accrued 16 points, five rebounds, six assists and two steals. Thaddeus Young tallied 12 points, seven rebounds and three assists. The Raptors (14 - 7) were led by DeMar DeRozan, as he collected 26 points, five rebounds, five assists and two steals. Serge Ibaka totaled 15 points, eight rebounds and two blocks. Kyle Lowry accumulated 11 points, seven rebounds and eight assists. Jakob Poeltl was huge off the bench, providing a career - high 18 points and six rebounds on a perfect 8 - of - 8 shooting. On deck for Indiana is a home matchup versus the New York Knicks on Monday. As for Toronto, they play host to the Phoenix Suns on Tuesday for their next fixture.
