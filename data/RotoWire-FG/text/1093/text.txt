The Los Angeles Clippers rolled past the Phoenix Suns, 134 - 107, at Staples Center on Wednesday. The Suns (11 - 48) entered Wednesday's contest having lost 14 in a row, and it became apparent early in the game that their streak was going to continue. The Clippers (32 - 27) grabbed a quicklead and never looked back. LAC shot a blistering 60 percent in the win, taking advantage of Phoenix's 29th - ranked defense. As always, the Clippers relied heavily on their strong second unit in the win. LA's bench scored 70 points, with the tandem of Lou Williams and Montrezl Harrell doing much of the heavy lifting. Williams and Harrell contributed 49 points and 13 assists in the blowout win. LA also saw a 20 - point night from Danilo Gallinari, who was wildly efficient in just 19 minutes on the court. New Clipper Ivica Zubac also impressed, scoring 16 points to go with seven rebounds. Phoenix, meanwhile, saw a promising night from forward Kelly Oubre, who scored 28 points to lead the team. Star guard Devin Booker went cold in the loss, though, shooting just 2 - of - 10 en route to 10 points. Both teams will head into the All-Star break after Wednesday's lopsided contest.
