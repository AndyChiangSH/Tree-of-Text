The Houston Rockets defeated the visiting Utah Jazz, 120 - 99, at Toyota Center on Monday evening. It was a hard fought game the whole way, with the Jazz looking like they could end Houston's mighty winning streak. After a tight three quarters, the Jazz pulled ahead by eight early in the fourth, leading 89 - 81 with 10:30 left in the game. It was at this point that the Rockets went ballistic. Eric Gordon poured in three straight threes and within a couple of minutes the Rockets were up by three. They continued on a rampage, outscoring the Jazz 39 - 10 from the 10:30 mark to the end of the game. This marks the 14th straight win for the Rockets. Gordon finished with a team - high 33 points on 12 - of - 18 shooting, in 35 minutes off the bench for Houston. James Harden followed up with 26 points, six rebounds, six assists, two steals and one block, while Chris Paul tallied 18 points, 10 assists, nine rebounds, two blocks and one steal. Clint Capela came up huge with 24 points and 20 rebounds, in 34 minutes. The Rockets shot 52 percent from the field, including 17 - of - 43 from long range. Meanwhile, Rodney Hood led the Jazz with 26 points on 10 - of - 18 shooting, in 31 minutes off the bench. Joe Ingles followed up with 17 points, four assists and three rebounds, while Ricky Rubio added 14 points, four assists, three rebounds and two steals. The Jazz shot 46 percent from the field, including 8 - of - 27 from downtown. The Jazz (14 - 17) head to OKC to take on the Thunder on Wednesday. The Rockets (25 - 4) will be looking for their 15th straight win as they play host to the Lakers on Wednesday.
