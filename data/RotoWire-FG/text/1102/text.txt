The Orlando Magic defeated the host Indiana Pacers, 117 - 112, at Banker's Life Fieldhouse on Saturday. There were 10 ties and 12 lead changes in this tightly - contested game, as neither club led by more than 11 points. Orlando won in comeback fashion, as they outscored Indiana, 34 - 25, in the fourth quarter. Offensive rebounding was the difference for the Magic, as they won that battle, 11 - 3. That allowed the Magic to outscore the Pacers in the paint, 54 - 48. Both offenses were on point, with Indiana connecting on 55 percent of their shots while Orlando shot 51 percent from the field. The Magic (30 - 34) were led by Nikola Vucevic, as he tallied 27 points, eight rebounds, three assists and two steals. Evan Fournier accrued 19 points, five rebounds and eight assists. Jonathan Isaac totaled 12 points and eight boards while Aaron Gordon tallied 11 points, eight rebounds and six assists. Terrence Ross led the bench with 23 points and three boards. The Pacers (41 - 23) were led by Bojan Bogdanovic, as he amassed 25 points, three rebounds and three assists. Darren Collison accumulated 23 points, three rebounds and 10 assists. Thaddeus Young finished with 13 points, six rebounds, five assists and two steals while Cory Joseph led the bench with 14 points, three rebounds and three assists. Both teams return to action on Tuesday, with Indiana playing host to the surprising Chicago Bulls and Orlando traveling to face the Philadelphia 76ers.
