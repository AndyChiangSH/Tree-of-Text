The Milwaukee Bucks defeated the Los Angeles Lakers, 98 - 90, at BMO Harris Bradley Center on Saturday. Milwaukee came into this game as a sizable favorite, as they left no doubt with this victory. It was closer than expected though, as there were 10 ties and 10 lead changes in total. The key in the game was the turnover differential, as the Bucks forced 23 of them from the Lakers, while committing just 11 of them on their end. Los Angeles killed themselves at the charity stripe, as they hit just 22 - of - 37, while Milwaukee went 22 - of - 26. The first and third quarters were decisive, with Milwaukee outscoring Los Angeles, 50 - 35. The Lakers (5 - 8) were led by Lonzo Ball, as he became the youngest player in NBA history to capture a triple - double. He finished the game with 18 points, 12 rebounds, 13 assists, three steals and three blocks. Kyle Kuzma led the team, as he totaled 21 points and 11 rebounds en route to a double - double. Julius Randle led the bench with 17 points and eight rebounds. The Bucks (6 - 6) have now won two - straight with Eric Bledsoe in the fold, as they look like a defensive powerhouse with him in the backcourt. He finished this game with 11 points, two rebounds and two assists. Giannis Antetokounmpo was the player of the game, as he accumulated 33 points, 15 rebounds and three assists. Khris Middleton amassed 13 points, four rebounds and four assists. Malcolm Brogdon was a huge spark off the bench, providing 16 points, three rebounds, three assists and three steals. Both teams return to action on Monday, with Milwaukee hosting the Memphis Grizzlies and Los Angeles traveling to face the Phoenix Suns.
