The Golden State Warriors defeated the New Orleans Pelicans, 131 - 121, at Oracle Arena on Wednesday. The Warriors (8 - 1) managed to survive a track meet in the Bay Area on Wednesday, and they earned their sixth straight victory in the process. Steph Curry was, yet again, sensational, scoring a game - high 37 points on 7 - of - 11 shooting from behind the arc and 12 - of - 20 shooting overall. He also dished out a game - high nine assists, barely edging out teammates Kevin Durant and Draymond Green, who each recorded eight. Durant finished with 24 points, while Green posted a very impressive stat line of 16 points, 14 rebounds and eight assists in 34 minutes. As a team, the Warriors consistently found ways to get open looks from three, and it paid off, as they shot 16 - of - 32 (50 percent) from long range. As for the Pelicans (4 - 3), they put up a good fight, but were unable to close the gap over the course of the second half. Jrue Holiday was the driving force behind the team's offense Wednesday night, as he scored a team - high 28 points to go along with nine assists in 42 minutes. It was a rough night, however, for superstar Anthony Davis, who didn't appear to be 100 percent healthy after missing the previous game with an elbow injury. Davis had just 17 points on 6 - of - 16 shooting and 12 rebounds in 41 minutes. Nikola Mirotic and E'Twaun Moore did an adequate job picking up the slack, scoring 26 and 21 points respectively. Up next, the Warriors will remain home to play the Timberwolves on Friday, while the Pelicans will head to Portland on Thursday to take on the Trail Blazers.
