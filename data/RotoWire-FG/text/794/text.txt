The visiting Cleveland Cavaliers defeated the Charlotte Hornets, 115 - 107, at Spectrum Center on Wednesday. LeBron James scored 31 points to lead the Cavaliers, but he was on the bench when the Cleveland (8 - 7) took the lead for good late in the game. James picked up his fourth foul with 3:31 to play in the third quarter, and went to the bench, trailing 82 - 79. Moments later, Kyle Korver hit a 3 - pointer that gave the Cavaliers an 86 - 84 lead with 1:6 left in the quarter that they would not relinquish. James returned with 8:58 to play and help extend a 95 - 88 lead to double digits in the fourth quarter. Kevin Love totaled 22 points and 10 rebounds as the Cavaliers won their third consecutive game. Jeff Green had 13 points, while Korver added 11 and J.R. Smith had 10. Michael Kidd-Gilchrist scored a season - high 22 points but couldn't prevent the Hornets (5 - 8) from losing their fifth in a row. Kemba Walker had 20 points and seven assists. Nic Batum totaled 16 points, six assists and five rebounds in his first game of the season after a tearing a ligament in his left elbow in October. Both teams are back in action Friday with the Cavaliers playing host to the Los Angeles Clippers and the Hornets at the Chicago Bulls. Cavaliers: Host the Los Angeles Clippers on Friday night.
