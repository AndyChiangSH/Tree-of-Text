The Phoenix Suns defeated the Atlanta Hawks, 104 - 103, at Talking Stick Resort Arena on Tuesday. Despite the fact that Atlanta led for the majority of this game, Phoenix led when it mattered most. In fact, the Suns led for less than five minutes, as Phoenix ended the game on a 15 - 4 run to escape with a win. The second and fourth quarters were decisive for Phoneix, as they outscored Atlanta, 61 - 44. The Suns dominated the shooting, hitting 51 percent of their shots, while holding the Hawks to just 41 percent from the field. A 47 - 40 rebounding differential was also imperative for Phoenix, as they also outassisted the Hawks, 24 - 18. Phoenix also dominated the scoring down low, outscoring Atlanta in the paint, 46 - 34. The Hawks (10 - 27) were led by Ersan Ilyasova, as he tallied 21 points and nine rebounds. Kent Bazemore accrued 20 points, three rebounds, four assists and four steals on 7 - of - 8 shooting. Dennis Schroder collected 20 points, two rebounds and five assists. Marco Belinelli led the bench with 16 points. The Suns (15 - 24) got another gem out of Devin Booker, as he led Phoenix with 34 points, five rebounds, seven assists and three steals. T.J. Warren wasn't far behind, totaling 31 points, five rebounds and two assists on 15 - of - 21 shooting. Marquese Chriss amassed 17 points, 11 rebounds, three assists and two steals.
