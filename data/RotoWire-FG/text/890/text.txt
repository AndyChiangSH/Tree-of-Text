The Dallas Mavericks took down the host Sacramento Kings, 106 - 99, at Golden One Center on Saturday. Dallas's Yogi Ferrell helped to spark a fourth quarter run that helped push the Mavericks to victory. While Ferrell finished with just 11 points, the bulk of those came in rapid succession, as he knocked down three straight three - pointers to fuel a game - winning final push for the Mavs. The team outscored Sacramento by 11 points in the fourth, helping to push them over the edge to victory. It was a balanced approach in the win for Dallas (17 - 36), as six players scored in double digits. This included all five starters, as well as Ferrell. The team's high scorer was Harrison Barnes, who scored 18 points on 7 - of - 14 shooting. Rookie point guard Dennis Smith, Jr. also had a solid game, scoring 12 points to go with five rebounds and five assists. The team as a whole shot 49 percent from the field and hit 13 three - pointers. Sacramento (16 - 36) dropped their second straight game with the loss. The team was able to shoot very effectively, hitting 53 percent of its shots from the field. However, getting to the free - throw line proved immensely difficult. The team attempted only six free - throws in the game, compared to 14 for Dallas. Rookie De'Aaron Fox led the team with 15 points and also came away with three steals. Sacramento will host the Chicago Bulls on Tuesday, while the Mavericks will visit the Los Angeles Clippers.
