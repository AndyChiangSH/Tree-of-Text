The Milwaukee Bucks defeated the San Antonio Spurs, 106 - 103, at BMO Harris Bradley Center on Sunday. The Bucks (39 - 34) finally ended the Spurs (43 - 31) winning streak in emphatic fashion, as they came out on top on a back - and - forth game where the home club managed to hold on to their early advantage. Milwaukee built a 27 - 15 in the first quarter, but San Antonio answered back and the first half finished with 47 - 47. The Bucks once again grabbed the lead in the third quarter (41 - 28) and San Antonio stormed back in the fourth (28 - 18), but they didn't have enough time to mount a second - half comeback. The game went down to the wire, but the Bucks came out on top. Giannis Antetokounmpo was the best player for Milwaukee, as he finished with 25 points, 10 rebounds and three assists. Eric Bledsoe had 23 points with five assists, and Khris Middleton chipped in with 19 points, seven rebounds, four assists and two steals. John Henson had 10 points with six rebounds and four assists, while Jabari Parker filled the stat sheet off the bench thanks to eight points, three assists, two boards and two blocks. As it has been the trend all season long, LaMarcus Aldridge was San Antonio's best player since he finished with 34 points and seven rebounds. Pau Gasol impressed coming off the bench, since he had 22 points and 13 rebounds, including seven offensive boards. Danny Green had 13 points, while Dejounte Murray contributed 10 points, nine rebounds, three assists and two steals.
