The Philadelphia 76ers defeated the Minnesota Timberwolves, 120 - 108, at Wells Fargo Center on Saturday. The 76ers (42 - 30) and the Timberwolves (42 - 32) engaged in a tight battle that saw Philly break free in the first three quarters, and even though Minnesota managed to come back in the fourth, they didn't have enough time to change their fate. Philadelphia jumped to a 32 - 24 lead in the first quarter, but the Wolves made it closer with a 28 - 27 second quarter. The biggest differential here was the third quarter, as the Sixers won that period by 20 points (39 - 19) and closed it out comfortably in the fourth despite the fact Minny won the final quarter they extended it after winning the next 37 - 22. Ben Simmons led the way for the Sixers after notching another triple - double of 15 points, 13 assists and 12 rebounds. Joel Embiid was the top scorer after finishing with 19 points, six boards and three blocks, while Dario Saric added 18 points. Robert Covington chipped in with 11 points, while Marco Belinelli contributed 17 points off the bench. Andrew Wiggins chipped in with 16 points and eight rebounds. Karl-Anthony Towns had 15 points with 11 rebounds, and Gorgui Dieng ended with the same stat line. The bench provided quite a spark for Minnesota in this game, as Jamal Crawford (13 points), Tyus Jones (11 points with six assists) and Marcus Georges-Hunt (11 points) all did a good job off the bench.
