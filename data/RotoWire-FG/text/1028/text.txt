The Phoenix Suns defeated the Dallas Mavericks, 99 - 89, at Talking Stick Resort Arena on Thursday evening. Phoenix (5 - 24) entered Thursday's nationally - televised game amidst turbulence on and off the court. The Suns had dropped 10 straight games and had declined to play Trevor Ariza in their most recent game as they attempt to find a trade destination for him. Rumors had also begun to swirl that team owner Robert Sarver was threatening to relocate the team to Seattle or Las Vegas, prompting outrage from many Suns fans. On Thursday, though, the team got good news, as Sarver publicly promised to keep the team in Phoenix and the Suns earned their first win in over three weeks. The Suns saw a strong shooting night from beyond the arc, knocking down 10 three - pointers. Dallas (15 - 13), meanwhile, shot a dismal 5 - of - 33 from beyond the arc in one of its coldest nights of the season. T.J. Warren led Phoenix with 30 points, shooting 11 - of - 17 from the field in the win. Trevor Ariza also saw the court again, where he scored 13 points and grabbed eight rebounds. Josh Jackson added 14 points but shot just 5 - of - 17 from the field in doing so. Dallas, meanwhile, scored only 89 points, as none of its players scored more than 15 in the loss. Rookie wing Luka Doncic scored 13 points to go with five rebounds and six assists. Veteran big man DeAndre Jordan also had a nice night, scoring 14 points and pulling down 16 rebounds despite the loss. Phoenix will host the Minnesota Timberwolves on Saturday, while Dallas will see the Sacramento Kings on Sunday.
