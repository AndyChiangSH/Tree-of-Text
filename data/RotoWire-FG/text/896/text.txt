The Toronto Raptors defeated the New York Knicks, 113 - 88, at Air Canada Centre on Wednesday evening. The Raptors (38 - 16) came into this game riding a three - game winning streak and had no problems to extend it four, since the Knicks (23 - 33) barely posed a threat offensively. The absence of Kristaps Porzingis was something New York couldn't mask, and Toronto controlled the game right from the start. In fact, the Raptors jumped to a 23 - 18 lead after the first quarter and they never looked back, winning each quarter by at least five points on route to a comfortable 25 - point win. The loss extended New York's losing streak to five contests. DeMar DeRozan (eight points, 2 - 11 FG) and Kyle Lowry (seven points, 2 - 10 FG) didn't have good performances, but the bench picked up the slack for the Raptors in this one since five second - unit players scored at least 10 points. The star of the game for Toronto was Jonas Valanciunas, who filled the stat sheet with 18 points, 10 rebounds, two blocks and two steals. Serge Ibaka had 13 points. Pascal Siakam added 14 off the bench. Michael Beasley was New York's top scorer with 21 points and seven rebounds. Jarrett Jack had 10 points with five assists, and Trey Burke had 12 points in just 13 minutes off the bench. Luke Kornet added 11 points. Tim Hardaway Jr. struggled, however, and finished with only nine points on 4 - 14 from the field.
