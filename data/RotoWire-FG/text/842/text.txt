The Sacramento Kings defeated the visiting Cleveland Cavaliers, 109 - 95, at Golden1 Center on Thursday evening. The Cavs got out to a quick 12 - 6 lead but the Kings battled back to take a 28 - 27 lead by the end of the first quarter. The Kings continued the strong effort through the second quarter, outscoring the Cavs, 34 - 28, to take a 62 - 55 by halftime. The Cavs tightened things up through the third quarter, but still trailed 85 - 80 heading into the final frame. The Kings pushed their lead to 14 early in the fourth and were able to keep enough of a cushion to come out with the impressive win. Vince Carter was on fire and came up clutch in the final minutes for Sacramento. Carter finished with a team - high 24 points on 10 - of - 12 shooting, along with five rebounds, three assists, one steal and one block, in 29 minutes of action off the bench. Willie Cauley-Stein finished with 17 points, nine rebounds, four assists and one block, while Bogdan Bogdanovic tallied 16 points, eight assists, four steals, three rebounds and one block, in 30 minutes. The Kings bench outscored the Cavs bench 68 - 32. The Kings shot 52 percent from the field, including 12 - of - 31 from long range. Meanwhile, Kevin Love finished with a team - high 23 points on 7 - of - 12 shooting for Cleveland. LeBron James tallied a triple - double with 16 points, 14 assists and 10 rebounds, in 37 minutes. J.R. Smith was the only other player to score in double digits for the Cavs, with 15 points. The Cavs shot 44 percent from the field, including 12 - of - 31 from long range. The Cavaliers (24 - 11) will look to bounce back as they take on the Jazz in Utah on Saturday. The Kings (12 - 22) play host to the Suns on Friday.
