The Atlanta Hawks defeated the Cleveland Cavaliers, 126 - 125, in overtime at Philips Arena on Sunday. In this potential playoff preview, Cleveland suffered an incredible collapse. In fact, they led by 26 headed into the fourth quarter, but allowed Atlanta to outscore them 44 - 18 in the final quarter to send it to overtime. Despite the Cavaliers leading for most of the overtime, Atlanta finished the game on a 15 - 9 run to comeback yet again and win. Scoring in the paint was the only real advantage for the Hawks, as they won that battle, 58 - 48. Their bench was huge as well, outscoring Cleveland's bench, 44 - 16. The Cavaliers (52 - 28) simply fell apart in this game and it'll go down as their worst loss of the season. LeBron James picked up his 15th triple - double of the year though, collecting 32 points, 16 rebounds and 10 assists before fouling out in overtime. Kyrie Irving led the team in scoring, accruing 45 points, two rebounds and nine assists. Kevin Love finished with 11 points, 15 rebounds and five assists. Channing Frye started and totaled 12 points and eight rebounds. On deck for the Cavaliers is a road matchup versus the Miami Heat on Monday. The Hawks (41 - 39) had one of their greatest comebacks in franchise history here, as they've now handed the Cavaliers back - to - back devastating losses. Paul Millsap returned to his starting role to provide 22 points, nine rebounds and two assists. Dwight Howard amassed 19 points, five rebounds and two assists on 9 - of - 12 shooting. Tim Hardaway Jr. accumulated 21 points, nine rebounds and four assists. Kent Bazemore was a nice spark off the bench, contributing 13 points, two rebounds, four assists and four steals. Mike Muscala also played well off the bench, dropping 12 points, five rebounds and four assists. Atlanta will look to keep rolling on Tuesday against the Charlotte Hornets.
