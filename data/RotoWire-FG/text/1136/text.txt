The Los Angeles Clippers defeated the Golden State Warriors, 129 - 121, in Game 5 of the Western Conference first - round playoffs, at Oracle Arena on Wednesday evening. The Clippers game out on fire in this must - win contest, as they set the tone by leading for almost the entire first quarter. However, the Warriors managed to keep up and took the lead in the final minutes of the quarter, to take a four - point advantage into the second. The Clippers stayed relentless and outscored the Warriors 34 - 22 in the second quarter, to take an eight point lead into halftime. The Clippers charged out into the third and pushed their lead to 13 early in the quarter. The Warriors made a bit of a run, but the visitors held a 10 - point lead heading into the fourth. The Clippers resisted an intense push from the Warriors through the fourth, as Lou Williams helped propel his team in the final minutes, to save their season and head back home for Game 6. Williams went off for the Clippers, with 33 points on 12 - of - 19 shooting, 10 assists and four rebounds, in 34 minutes. Patrick Beverly had an amazing game as well, with 17 points, 14 rebounds, four assists and a steal, while Danilo Gallinari finished with 26 points, seven rebounds, two assists and a steal. Montrezl Harrell came up big with energy plays and major effort, finishing with 24 points, five rebounds, two assists and a block, in 27 minutes off the bench. Meanwhile, Kevin Durant led the Warriors with 45 points, six rebounds, six assists, two steals and a block, in 41 minutes. Stephen Curry followed up with 24 points, four assists, three rebounds and a block, while Klay Thompson was the only other player to score in double digits for the Warriors, with 22 points, in 37 minutes. Game 6 will take place in L.A. on Friday.
