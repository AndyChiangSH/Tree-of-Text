The Milwaukee Bucks defeated the Boston Celtics, 123 - 116, at TD Garden on Friday night in Game 3 of their Eastern Conference semi - final round series. The Bucks now lead the series, 2 - 1. The Bucks (2 - 1) had done an impressive job bouncing back from a relatively shocking Game 1 loss, but they had another challenge in front of them in the form of a road game at raucous TD Garden. The Celtics (1 - 2) appeared buoyed by their passionate home crowd to start the game, as they took a 30 - 25 lead through one. However, Milwaukee had whittled down their deficit to one point by intermission. They then carried that momentum into the third quarter, shifting the dynamic of the contest with a 40 - point tally that gave them an eight - point lead they wouldn't relinquish. Boston finished on a 15 - 7 run over the final 3:49 to make matters interesting, but the outcome was never truly in doubt. Giannis Antetokoumpo led the way for the Bucks with 32 points, adding 13 rebounds, eight assists, three blocks and two steals. George Hill turned in a critical performance off the bench, supplying 21 points, four rebounds and three assists. Khris Middleton registered 20 points, five assists, four rebounds and three steals. Patrick Connaughton poured in 14 points and added seven rebounds, two steals and one block. Nikola Mirotic turned in 13 points, three rebounds and one steal. Kyrie Irving paced the Celtics with 29 points, complementing them with six assists, three rebounds and three steals. Jayson Tatum rebounded from a pair of sub - par games to start the series, delivering 20 points, 11 rebounds, three assists and three steals. Jaylen Brown totaled 18 points, five rebounds, two assists, two steals and one block. Al Horford collected 17 points, eight boards and five assists. Marcus Morris netted 16 points, eight rebounds, two assists and two blocks. The series moves on to a critical Game 4 on Monday night in TD Garden.
