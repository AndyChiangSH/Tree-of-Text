The Toronto Raptors defeated the host Milwaukee Bucks, 92 - 89, in Game 6 of the Eastern Conference first - round playoffs at BMO Harris Bradley Center on Thursday. This was a game full of swings, but the Raptors eventually prevailed. It wasn't easy for Toronto though, as they blew a 25 - point lead in the victory. In fact, the Raptors led by 25 with five minutes left in the third quarter and the Bucks came all the way back to take an 82 - 80 lead with four minutes left in the fourth quarter, thanks to a 36 - 9 run. Toronto responded from there though with a 90 run, which ultimately was the deciding factor. Free-throw shooting really doomed the Bucks, who missed nine freebies in the fourth quarter and finished the game just 18 - of - 28 from the line, while the Raptors went 19 - of - 23. The Raptors did blow a monster lead in this game, but received some vital scoring from All-Star DeMar DeRozan down the stretch. DeRozan carried Toronto, as he collected 32 points, four rebounds, three assists and five steals. Kyle Lowry was the only other starter in double figures, totaling 13 points, four rebounds and four assists. Serge Ibaka started at center and amassed seven points, 11 rebounds and two blocks before fouling out. The Bucks did an incredible job of fighting back in this game, but they simply didn't have enough in the tank for a full comeback. Giannis Antetokounmpo left it all on the floor, accumulating 34 points, nine rebounds, three assists, two steals and two blocks in 46 minutes. Khris Middleton was the only other starter with more than six points, as he tallied 19 points, four rebounds, five assists and two steals. Matthew Dellavedova led the bench with 12 points, four rebounds and four assists. On deck for Toronto is a quarterfinals matchup against the Cleveland Cavaliers, who swept the Indiana Pacers and have had a week off thanks to that dominating series.
