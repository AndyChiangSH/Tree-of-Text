The Golden State Warriors defeated the visiting Houston Rockets, 104 - 100, in Game 1 of the Western Conference semi - finals at Oracle Arena on Sunday. This has become one of the biggest rivalries in the NBA and this game wasn't decided until the final seconds. In fact, there were seven ties and eight lead changes in total, with neither team leading by more than nine points. Golden State did most of their damage out of the locker room, as they outscored Houston in the first and third quarters, 58 - 42. Rebounding was key for the Warriors, as they won that battle, 36 - 26. In addition, Golden State out - assisted the Rockets, 24 - 14. That allowed Golden State to shoot 51 percent from the field while holding Houston to just 42 percent from the field. The Rockets (0 - 1) were led by James Harden, as he tallied 35 points, four rebounds, six assists and three steals. Eric Gordon totaled 27 points, four rebounds and two blocks. Chris Paul finished with 17 points, three rebounds, four assists and three steals while no other player reached double figures in scoring. The Warriors (10) were led by Kevin Durant, as he accrued 35 points, five rebounds and three assists. Stephen Curry amassed 18 points, seven rebounds and four assists. Andre Iguodala started and generated 14 points, four rebounds and two assists while Draymond Green accumulated 14 points, nine rebounds and nine assists. These teams will return to action on Tuesday for Game 1.
