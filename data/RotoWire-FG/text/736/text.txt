The San Antonio Spurs defeated the Memphis Grizzlies, 95 - 89, in overtime Tuesday at AT & T Center. It was a low - scoring, back - and - forth affair Tuesday night, which kept the game relatively close throughout the the original 48 minutes of the contest. With the Spurs (60 - 17) looking to pull away with just over three minutes left in the fourth quarter, the Grizzlies (42 - 36) managed to go on a quick 80 spurt that put them up by one point with a minute and a half remaining. For the final minute of Tuesday's game, the teams went on to trade baskets, with three made buckets coming in the last four seconds and putting the game into overtime. From there, it was all Spurs, who dominated on the defensive end throughout the extra period to earn the victory. Kawhi Leonard put together yet another tremendous performance for San Antonio, finishing with a game - high 32 points to go along with 12 rebounds and four assists in 40 minutes. LaMarcus Aldridge added 15 points and six rebounds of his own in 41 minutes, while Pau Gasol came off the bench to notch a double - double himself, posting 12 points and 11 boards in 31 minutes as a reserve. As a team, the Spurs were absolutely on fire from downtown, shooting 11 - of - 21 (52 percent) from behind the arc. As for the Grizzlies, they battled hard despite dealing with multiple injuries to their star players, as Mike Conley was forced to leave injury after an eye injury, while Marc Gasol was limited with a foot injury and held out of the overtime period as a result. Conley still managed to lead the team in scoring with 19 points to go along with six assists in 33 minutes, but the biggest performance came off the bench from Zach Randolph, who finished with a double - double of 18 points and 16 rebounds in 35 minutes as a reserve. Overall, however, it was a struggle offensively for Memphis on Tuesday night, as they managed to hit just 39 percent of their shots from the field.
