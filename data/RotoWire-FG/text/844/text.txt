The Toronto Raptors defeated the Atlanta Hawks, 111 - 98, at Air Canada Center on Friday. Toronto were heavy favorites headed into this matchup and this dominating scoreline shows just why. In fact, the Raptors led by eight at the end of the first quarter, by 15 at the half and carried a 19 - point lead headed into the fourth quarter. A 29 - 23 fourth quarter for the Hawks made the scoreline respectable, but they still never got the game within nine points for the entirety of the second half. Turnovers really killed Atlanta, as they committed 18 of them, while forcing just 11 from Toronto. The Raptors did a great job of protecting the rim too, recording 15 blocks to the Hawks seven. The Hawks (9 - 26) got a season - high 30 points out of Taurean Prince, as he added 10 rebounds on 12 - of - 16 shooting. Dennis Schroder collected 14 points, five rebounds, nine assists and two steals. No other starter reached double figures in scoring. Marco Belinelli did provide 14 points and three assists to lead the bench though. The Raptors (24 - 10) were led by DeMar DeRozan, as he tallied 25 points, five assists and two blocks. Jonas Valanciunas recorded a 13 - point, 11 - rebound double - double. Kyle Lowry accumulated 12 points, five rebounds and five assists. Delon Wright led the bench with 13 points, seven rebounds, two assists and two steals. On deck for Atlanta is a home matchup versus the Portland Trail Blazers on Saturday. As for Toronto, they're off until Monday when they host the Milwaukee Bucks.
