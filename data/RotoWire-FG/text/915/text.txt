The New Orleans Pelicans defeated the host San Antonio Spurs, 121 - 116, at AT & T Center on Wednesday. San Antonio held the lead for the first 44 minutes of this game, but New Orleans prevailed when it mattered most. In fact, the Pelicans rode a 34 - 21 fourth quarter to a comeback victory, as the Spurs led by 15 points late in the third quarter. The only real difference in the stat sheet was scoring in the paint, with New Orleans winning that battle, 68 - 58. The Pelicans starters also dominated, as they outscored the Spurs starters, 81 - 48. The Pelicans (35 - 26) were led by Anthony Davis, as he accumulated 26 points, 15 rebounds and two steals. Jrue Holiday finished with 25 points, four rebounds and three assists. Rajon Rondo accrued 13 points, eight rebounds and 12 assists. Ian Clark was a nice spark off the bench, generating 10 points, four rebounds and four assists. The Spurs (36 - 26) were unfortunate to lose their leading scorer in this game, as LaMarcus Aldridge departed in the first half due to a right ankle sprain. Dejounte Murray led the way in his absence, as he collected 18 points, nine rebounds and five assists. Patty Mills amassed 11 points, three rebounds and two assists. Rudy Gay led the bench with 19 points, five rebounds, four assists and three blocks. Manu Ginobili also played well off the bench, providing 17 points, two rebounds and three assists. San Antonio's next game will be on Saturday when they play host to the Los Angeles Lakers. As for New Orleans, they're off until Sunday when they travel to face the Dallas Mavericks.
