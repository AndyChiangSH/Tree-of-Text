The Utah Jazz defeated the Dallas Mavericks, 97 - 90, at Vivint Smart Home Arena on Saturday. Despite the fact that Utah were sizable favorites in this matchup, eight ties and six lead changes show just how close this game was. In fact, this game was within a single - digit margin for all but six minutes, as neither team led by more than seven points for the final 30 minutes of action. Free-throw shooting was huge for the Jazz, as they connected on 20 - of - 26 while the Mavericks hit just 12 - of - 14. The assist - to - turnover ratio was decisive as well, with Utah recording seven more assists than Dallas while committing five fewer turnovers. Scoring down low was also big, with Utah winning that battle, 46 - 30. The Mavericks (18 - 42) were led by J.J Barea, as he tallied 17 points, three rebounds and 12 assists. Dirk Nowitzki finished with 12 points, 10 rebounds and three blocks. Yogi Ferrell led the bench with 13 points, five rebounds and four assists. Dennis Smith Jr. had a game to forget, as he totaled seven points, seven rebounds, no assists and five turnovers on 2 - of - 11 from the field. The Jazz (31 - 29) got another gem out of Donovan Mitchell, as he led the team with 25 points, six rebounds, five assists and two steals. Derrick Favors accrued 14 points and six rebounds. Rudy Gobert amassed 13 points and 10 rebounds while Joe Ingles accumulated 12 points, four rebounds, eight assists, two steals and two blocks. Both teams play home games on Monday for their next fixtures, with Utah welcoming the Houston Rockets and Dallas playing the Indiana Pacers.
