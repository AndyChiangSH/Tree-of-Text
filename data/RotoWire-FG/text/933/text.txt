The New York Knicks defeated the Chicago Bulls, 110 - 92, at Madison Square Garden on Monday. While there were seven ties and 14 lead changes in the first half, New York really dominated in the second half. The middle quarters were the difference, with the Knicks outscoring the Bulls, 64 - 49. Defense was key for New York, as they held Chicago to 33 - of - 92 from the field and 3 - of - 30 from three - point range. Rebounding was also a huge factor, with the Knicks outrebounding the Bulls, 61 - 48. The Bulls (24 - 46) were led by Cristiano Felicio, as he tallied 17 points and six rebounds. No other starter had more than eight points, as Cameron Payne tallied four points, six rebounds and six assists starting for Kris Dunn. Bobby Portis led the bench with 16 points, seven rebounds and four assists. Noah Vonleh also played well off the bench, providing nine points and 12 boards. The Knicks (26 - 45) were led by Tim Hardaway Jr., as he amassed 22 points, two rebounds and two steals. Michael Beasley finished with 17 points, six rebounds and two assists. Enes Kanter accrued 10 points and 13 rebounds in 17 minutes of action. Both teams return to action on Wednesday, with New York traveling to face the Miami Heat and Chicago playing host to the Denver Nuggets.
