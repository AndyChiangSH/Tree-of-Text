The Portland Trail Blazers defeated the Utah Jazz, 101 - 86, at Moda Center on Saturday. These are two teams that look poised to reach the postseason, as Portland showed how dangerous they can be. It was a near wire - to - wire win for the Blazers, with Utah maintaining just one lead in the opening two minutes. The first and third quarters is where Portland did their damage, outscoring Utah, 63 - 38, in those two quarters. Turnovers were the difference in the game, as Portland committed just three of them, while forcing the Jazz to commit 14 on their end. The Blazers also took advantage of the long ball, hitting 13 - of - 30 from three - point range, while the Jazz went just 6 - of - 19. The Jazz (49 - 31) are now tied with the Los Angeles Clippers for the fourth - seed in the west, as this loss could be devastating for homecourt advantage. Gordon Hayward did all he could for Utah, as he led the way with 21 points, five rebounds and four assists. Rudy Gobert finished with 13 points, 11 rebounds and four blocks The other three starters combined for just eight points. Joe Johnson and Rodney Hood both scored in double figures off the bench. On deck for Utah is a road matchup versus the Golden State Warriors on Monday. The Trail Blazers (40 - 40) are now 2 games up on the Denver Nuggets for the eighth - seed in the west, as this win got them one step closer to securing a playoff berth. Damian Lillard was the player of the game, as he scored 26 of his career - high 59 points in the first quarter. Lillard did that damage on 18 - of - 34 shooting, while also adding six rebounds and five assists to his tally. C.J. McCollum struggled, as he accrued nine points, three rebounds and five assists on 4 - of - 20 from the field. Maurice Harkless was the only other Blazer in double figures, as he amassed 12 points, three rebounds and three steals. Portland will look to secure that playoff berth on Monday, when they host the San Antonio Spurs for their next game.
