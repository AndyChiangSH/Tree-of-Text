The Sacramento Kings took down the Orlando Magic, 94 - 88, at Golden 1 Center on Friday night. The Kings (21 - 45) led by as much as 15 in what was a fairly cut - and - dry win for the struggling team. Head coach Dave Joerger earned his 200th career win with the victory, though wins have been few and far between for Sacramento this season. The same can be said for Orlando (20 - 46), who struggled to make anything happen on Friday night. The team shot just 42 percent in the game and 31 percent from beyond the arc. Garrett Temple led the Kings with 23 points and knocked down four of the team's nine three - pointers. Big man Kosta Koufos, meanwhile, scored a season - high 18 points to go with 13 rebounds. Koufos logged a team - high 38 minutes as he enjoyed a breakout night, shooting 9 - of - 12 from the field and committing only one turnover. Orlando, meanwhile, saw a game - high 25 points from Jonathan Simmons. Simmons reached the free - throw line nine times, accounting for more than half of Orlando's makes and attempts from the charity stripe. Unfortunately for the Magic, their bench was unable to contribute anything meaningful. The Orlando second unit had only 21 points on 8 - of - 24 shooting in the loss. The Magic will visit the Clippers in Los Angeles on Saturday, while the Kings travel to Denver to see the Nuggets on Sunday.
