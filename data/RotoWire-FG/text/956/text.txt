The Philadeplhia 76ers defeated the Dallas Mavericks, 109 - 97 at Wells Fargo Center on Sunday. The 76ers (50 - 30) had a clear advantage on paper in this game against the Mavericks (24 - 57), and they didn't have problems to translate that dominance into the score. The Sixers jumped to a 28 - 20 advantage in the first quarter, and they extended it to a 13 - point lead at the half time break. Dallas won the third quarter, 28 - 24, and it seemed as if they were going to make things tough for Philadelphia down the stretch, but the hosts didn't rattle and closed things out with ease in the fourth. With the win, the Sixers extended their winning streak to 14 games and also hit the 50 - win mark. J.J. Redick was Philadelphia's top scorer with 18 points and four assists. Ben Simmons had 16 points, but the talented rookie also filled the stat sheet thanks to nine assists, seven rebounds, four steals and one block. Robert Covington made an impact on both ends of the court, thanks to 15 points, 10 rebounds, three assists, Mario Belinelli (15 points) and Ersan Ilyasova (11 points, 12 rebounds) made their presence felt off the bench. Six out of Dallas' nine players scored in double digits in this one, and the top scorer was Harrison Barnes who finished with 21 points. Dennis Smith Jr. finished with 20 points, 11 assists, five rebounds, three steals and two blocks. Dwight Powell had 13 points and 10 boards. Dorian Finley-Smith and Doug McDermott both netted 10 points in the same exact 26 minutos of action.
