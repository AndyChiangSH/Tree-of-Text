The Miami Heat defeated the host Charlotte Hornets, 106 - 105, at Spectrum Center on Saturday evening. The Heat started out well and controlled the lead for most of the first half, before taking a 57 - 48 lead into halftime. The Hornets came out much stronger in the third quarter, and chipped away until they tied it up at 73, with 3:12 left in the quarter. They then went on a 100 run to close the quarter and take an 83 - 73 lead into the final frame. Despite trailing for most of the quarter, the Heat snuck up on the Hornets, as they closed the gap to 105 - 102 on a driving layup by James Johnson with 30 seconds left. The Heat then immediately stole the inbound pass and Johnson knocked down a game - tying three. The Hornets flopped on their last possession, which allowed the Heat one more push up the floor, where Kelly Olynyk was fouled. Olynyk knocked down one - of - two free throws to take the win. Wayne Ellington was the high - point man for Miami, with 26 points on 10 - of - 15 shooting, in 36 minutes. James Johnson followed up with 22 points, seven assists and six rebounds, in 29 minutes, while Hassan Whiteside tallied 10 points, 14 rebounds and three assists, in 24 minutes. Kelly Olynyk added 16 points, 10 rebounds and three assists, in 24 minutes off the bench. The Heat shot 49 percent from the field, including 13 - of - 28 from long range. Meanwhile, Nicolas Batum led the Hornets with 26 points, six assists, three rebounds and three steals, in 33 minutes. Kemba Walker followed up with 22 points, seven assists, three rebounds and three steals, while Dwight Howard added 14 points, 15 rebounds, three blocks, an assist and a steal. The Hornets shot 46 percent from the field, including 7 - of - 27 from long range. The Hornets (18 - 26) will look to bounce back as they play host to the Kings on Monday. The Heat (27 - 19) take on the Rockets in Houston on Monday.
