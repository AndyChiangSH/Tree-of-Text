The Atlanta Hawks defeated the Orlando Magic, 94 - 88, at Philips Arena on Sunday. In a battle between two bottom dwellers, Atlanta was able to prevail thanks to a big first half. In fact, a 35 - 25 second quarter in advantage of Atlanta really set the tone, as they carried a 13 - point lead into the half and never trailed in the second half. The shooting disparity was critical, as Atlanta shot 48 percent from the field while holding Orlando to just 34 percent. Rebounding was also huge, with the Hawks winning that battle, 52 - 41. Ball movement was also a huge factor, with Atlanta recording 33 assists and Orlando handing out just 19. The Hawks won despite attempting just one free - throw attempt, the lowest total from any team all season. The Magic (22 - 54) were led by D.J. Augustin, as he tallied 20 points, seven rebounds and three assists. Jonathan Issac totaled 15 points and four rebounds. Nikola Vucevic went just 3 - of - 19 from the field but still finished with 10 points, 14 rebounds, three assists and three blocks. Aaron Gordon amassed 10 points, seven rebounds and three assists. The Hawks (22 - 55) were led by Dewayne Dedmon, as he accrued 17 points, 10 rebounds and three assists. John Collins generated 10 points, 11 rebounds and two blocks. Tyler Dorsey led the bench with 19 points and two assists. Mike Muscala provided 13 points, 10 rebounds and three assists off the bench as well. On deck for Atlanta is a road matchup versus the Miami Heat on Tuesday. Orlando also returns to action on Tuesday, as they travel to face the New York Knicks.
