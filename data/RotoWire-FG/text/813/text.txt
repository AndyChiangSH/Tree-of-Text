The Golden State Warriors defeated the host New Orleans Pelicans, 125 - 115, at Smoothie King Center on Monday. Despite the fact that New Orleans dominated throughout the first half, Golden State responded by running away in the second half. In fact, the Pelicans led by 20 at the half and ended up being outscored by 30 in the second half. Golden State's offense was key, as they recorded 36 assists, while shooting 52 percent from the field. The Warriors dominated with transition offense as well, winning the fastbreak differential, 35 - 20. The Warriors (19 - 6) were led by Stephen Curry, as he collected 31 points, five rebounds and 11 assists. Klay Thompson amassed 22 points and three rebounds. Kevin Durant accumulated 19 points, seven rebounds, five assists and two steals. Draymond Green totaled 19 points, nine rebounds, seven assists, four steals and two blocks. The Pelicans (12 - 12) played without Anthony Davis (groin), as Jrue Holiday led the team 34 points, six rebounds and four assists. E'Twaun Moore scored a season - high 27 points, while adding six rebounds and two assists. DeMarcus Cousins accrued 19 points, 11 rebounds, seven assists, five steals and three blocks. Rajon Rondo provided 10 points, five rebounds, 11 assists and two steals. On deck for New Orleans is a home matchup versus the Denver Nuggets on Wednesday. As for Golden State, they travel to play the Detroit Pistons on Friday.
