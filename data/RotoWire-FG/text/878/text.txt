The Oklahoma City Thunder defeated the Washington Wizards, 121 - 112, at Chesapeake Energy Arena on Thursday. While this was a wire - to - wire win for Oklahoma City, it was much closer than that would indicate. In fact, Washington turned a 14 - point deficit into just a two - point margin at one point int he third quarter, as they simply couldn't grab a lead. Turnovers really killed the Wizards, as they committed 23 turnovers while forcing just 14 from the Thunder. A huge part of that was the 16 steals that Oklahoma City recorded. Scoring down low was also huge for the Thunder, as they outscored the Wizards in the paint, 60 - 40. The Wizards (26 - 22) got a gem out of Bradley Beal, as he tallied 41 points, 12 rebounds, seven assists and two steals on 15 - of - 26 shooting. John Wall amassed 21 points and 12 assists. Markieff Morris accrued 20 points, nine rebounds and two steals. Otto Porter was the only other Wizard in double figures, as he totaled 11 points, six rebounds and three assists. The Thunder (28 - 20) were led by Russell Westbrook, as he collected 46 points, six rebounds, six assists and two steals on 19 - of - 29 from the field. Paul George accumulated 18 points, two rebounds, four assists and four steals. Carmelo Anthony finished with 13 points and eight rebounds, while Steven Adams provided 12 points, 10 rebounds and three assists. Both teams return to action on Saturday, with Oklahoma City traveling to play the Detroit Pistons and Washington playing the Atlanta Hawks on the road.
