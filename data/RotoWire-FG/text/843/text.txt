The Memphis Grizzlies defeated the Los Angeles Lakers, 109 - 99, at Staples Center on Wednesday. Memphis (11 - 23) had been in the midst of a free - fall coming into Wednesday's matchup. The Grizzlies had lost 20 of their last 23 games before picking up a double - digit win on Wednesday. While the Grizzlies initially pulled ahead, a tough third - quarter rally from the Lakers had the game tied heading into the fourth quarter. In the fourth, though, Memphis' offense was able to pull away with the game. 12 of the team's 33 fourth - quarter points came from veteran guard Tyreke Evans, who finished the night with a team - high 32. Evans added seven rebounds and seven assists. Marc Gasol also made a splash in the game, finishing with nine rebounds and five assists in addition to his 11 points. Los Angeles (11 - 22), meanwhile, was shorthanded on Wednesday. Guard Lonzo Ball (shoulder) and center Brook Lopez (ankle) sat out of Wednesday's action. It was a rough night for rookie Kyle Kuzma without his fellow rook, as he shot just 4 - of - 24 from the field including 1 - of - 11 from three - point range. Kuzma was a primary contributor to the Lakers' rough team shooting splits. The team shot just 38 percent from the field on the night and 29 percent from beyond the arc. Brandon Ingram led the team with 23 points, but had some frustration throughout the night, as he committed five turnovers and went just 5 - of - 11 at the free - throw line. Ingram was assessed a technical for complaining about a call in the game's final minute. The Lakers will tangle with the Clippers on Friday, while the Grizzlies visit the Golden State Warriors on Saturday.
