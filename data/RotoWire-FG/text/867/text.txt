The Indiana Pacers defeated the host Phoenix Suns, 120 - 97, at Talking Stick Resort Arena on Sunday. This game was over early, as a 30 - 16 first quarter was too much for Phoenix to overcome. In fact, Indiana led by double - digits for the final three quarters, as they led by at least 20 points for the entirety of the fourth quarter. The shooting disparity was huge, with the Pacers hitting 55 percent of their shots, while holding the Suns to just 41 percent from the field. Three-point shooting was equally as lopsided, with Indiana hitting 11 - of - 23 and Phoenix going just 9 - of - 37 from long range. The nine steals were also huge for Indiana, as Phoenix could only muster up two steals. The Pacers (23 - 20) were led by Darren Collison, as he tallied 19 points, three rebounds and two assists. Victor Oladipo collected 17 points, five rebounds and four steals. Domantas Sabonis started and totaled four points and 14 boards. Cory Joseph led the bench with 16 points and six assists. The Suns (16 - 28) best player was Josh Jackson, as he amassed 21 points, six rebounds and three assists. Devin Booker accrued 15 points, five rebounds and seven assists. Tyson Chandler provided nine points and 14 rebounds as the starting center. Alex Len double - doubled off the bench, putting in 12 points, 10 rebounds and three assists. Indiana will have a quick turnaround, as they travel to face the Utah Jazz on Monday. As for Phoenix, they travel to play the Portland Trail Blazers on Tuesday for their next fixture.,
