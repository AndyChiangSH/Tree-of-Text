The Boston Celtics defeated the Golden State Warriors, 92 - 88, at TD Garden on Thursday. Despite the fact that Golden State led for the majority of this game, Boston were able to prevail and extend their winning streak to 14 games. It was competitive throughout though, as there were 11 ties and 10 lead changes in total. Defense was on display for both teams, as Golden State hit just 40 percent of their shots and Boston shooting just 33 percent from the field. The difference for Boston was getting to the free - throw line, as they went 33 - of - 38, while holding Golden State to just 12 - of - 19. The Warriors (11 - 4) were led by Kevin Durant, as he tallied 24 points, three rebounds, three assists and two blocks. Klay Thompson, Stephen Curry and Draymond Green combined for just 33 points on 11 - of - 45 shooting. Green did provide eight rebounds, five assists and three blocks, while Curry added six rebounds, five assists and four steals to his line. The Celtics (14 - 2) got 22 points, seven rebounds, two steals and two blocks out of Jaylen Brown to lead the way. Al Horford totaled 18 points, 11 rebounds and two assists. Kyrie Irving amassed 16 points, five rebounds and six assists on 4 - of - 16 shooting. Marcus Morris and Jayson Tatum each scored 12 points. Both teams return to action on Saturday, as the Celtics travel to play the Atlanta Hawks and the Warriors playing the Philadelphia 76ers on the road as well.
