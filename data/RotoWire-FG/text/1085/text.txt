The Sacramento Kings defeated the visiting Miami Heat, 102 - 96, at Golden 1 Center on Friday evening. The Heat got out to a great start on the road, with a 28 - 17 lead after one quarter. The Kings picked it up in the second quarter, but could only make a small dent in the deficit, as they trailed by nine at the half. The Heat came out strong in the third, and held a 17 - point lead with three minutes left in the quarter, but the Kings rallied back to make it an eight - point game by the start of the final frame. The Kings carried the momentum into the fourth, where they outscored the visitors by 14, to steal the win at home. Buddy Hield led the Kings with 23 points, seven rebounds, two blocks and an assist, in 34 minutes. Bogdan Bogdanovic followed up with 16 points, five rebounds, four assists and two steals, while Harrison Barnes added 12 points, seven rebounds and two assists. The Kings shot 41 percent from the field, including 9 - of - 26 from long range. Josh Richardson led the Heat with 21 points, four assists, three blocks and two rebounds, in 37 minutes. Hassan Whiteside followed up with 17 points, 19 rebounds, three steals, two blocks and an assist, while Dwyane Wade added 15 points, four rebounds and four assists, in 25 minutes off the bench. The Heat shot 43 percent from the field, including 11 - of - 33 from long range. The Heat (25 - 28) will look to bounce back as they take on the Warriors in Golden State on Sunday. The Kings (29 - 26) play host to the Suns on Sunday.
