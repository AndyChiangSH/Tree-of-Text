The Utah Jazz protected their home court on Saturday, defeating the visiting Milwaukee Bucks, 121 - 108, at Vivint Smart Home Arena. Utah (9 - 11) has started the season slowly, but remains a tough matchup on its home court. The Jazz are now 8 - 4 in Utah, and won Saturday's home stand in impressive fashion. The team made 18 three - pointers, a new single - game franchise record. While it was the team's eighth straight game without star center Rudy Gobert, the Jazz were able to excel without their franchise player. Rookie Donovan Mitchell led the team, pouring in 24 points including six made three - pointers. Off the bench, sixth man Rodney Hood added 21 points and knocked down three three - pointers. The inconsistent duo had a stellar night, helping Utah's impressive efficiency with a combined 17 - of - 31 shooting performance. Utah also set a new season high in assists, dishing out 31 en route to the win. Milwaukee (9 - 9) is not lacking in talent, but has struggled to stay firmly over.500 early in the season. The Jazz were the second team in the last two weeks that Milwaukee allowed to set a franchise high in three - pointers. On Saturday, defense proved a major issue for the Bucks, as the team allowed over 56 percent of Utah's shots to be successful. The Bucks were able to construct their own stellar offensive outing, shooting 53 percent as a team. Superstar forward Giannis Antetokounmpo led the team with 27 points and had 14 rebounds. However, perimeter defense proved to be too large an issue to overcome, as the Bucks were sent home easily on Saturday night. Utah will host the Denver Nuggets on Tuesday, while the Bucks will visit Sacramento.
