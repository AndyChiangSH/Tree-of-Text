The host Utah Jazz blew out the Washington Wizards at Vivint Smart Home Arena on Monday, 116 - 69. The Jazz (13 - 11) have now won six straight, with Monday's win coming in historic fashion. Monday's blowout marked the second - largest margin of victory in franchise history for Utah. Center Rudy Gobert made his return to the lineup Monday after missing the past four weeks with a knee injury. Gobert contributed in limited fashion on the offensive end (four points), but blocked two shots and had a steal on defense. Defense was the key for Utah, as Washington was held to just 28 percent shooting from the field. On the offensive end, guard Alec Burks had a team - high 27 points. The veteran has now turned in three straight performances of at least 24 points. Washington (12 - 11), meanwhile, had its largest deficit of the season in Monday's loss. The Wizards shot only 28 percent from the field and hit just four three - pointers on the night. Starters Bradley Beal and Marcin Gortat combined to go just 4 - of - 20 from the field in the loss, also contributing three turnovers. Washington also had a significant deficit on the glass, pulling down just 32 boards as opposed to 52 from Utah. It was an all - around off night for the Wizards, as only one Wizard scored more than 11 points in the game. The Jazz will visit OKC on Tuesday, while the Wizards will travel to Portland.
