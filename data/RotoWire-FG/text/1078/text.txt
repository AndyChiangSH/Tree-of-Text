The Sacramento Kings defeated the Atlanta Hawks, 135 - 113, at Golden 1 Center on Wednesday. The Kings (26 - 25) let their visitors stick around for one quarter Wednesday before deciding to start pouring it on in the second quarter and never look back. After holding a one - point lead after the first 12 minutes, Sacramento went on to win the next two quarters by a total of 24 points, making for light work for one of the NBA's most promising young teams on a Wednesday night. The most impressive part about the Kings' win, however, was the play of their second unit, which continues to be one of the league's best. With the starters getting a light workload in the blowout, the bench took full advantage. Seven of the eight players who saw work off the bench scored and the unit combined to score 80 of the team's 135 points. Harry Giles lead the way off the bench with a career - high 20 points, while rookie Marvin Bagley III scored 17 and Bogdan Bogdanovic added 16 of his own. As for the Hawks (16 - 34), they were unable to replicate the performance that lead to an impressive victory over the Clippers on Monday night. The team did shoot a respectable 35 percent from behind the arc, but Atlanta hit just 44 percent of its shots from the field overall. The biggest downfall for the Hawks, however, was their inability to take care of the basketball, as they recorded 20 turnovers Wednesday night. Trae Young was responsible for five of those turnovers, but he did have yet another impressive offensive performance, finishing with a game - high 23 points and eight assists in 29 minutes. Six other Hawks players managed to score in double figures, but none scored higher than John Collins' 14 points in 25 minutes.
