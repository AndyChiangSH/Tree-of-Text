The Minnesota Timberwolves defeated the visiting Golden State Warriors in overtime 131 - 130, at Target Center on Friday. This game was full of swings, as it wasn't decided until the final shot. In fact, Stephen Curry hit a game - tying three with 0 seconds left in OT but they fouled Minnesota on the inbound in the following possession. That allowed Karl-Anthony Towns to nail the game - winning free - throw, who then missed the second free - throw intentionally to run out the clock. There were nine ties and 12 lead changes in total, as Minnesota never trailed in the OT. Rebounding was the clear - cut difference, with the Timberwolves outrebounding the Warriors, 59 - 45. Their bench also came up big, outscoring Golden State's bench, 52 - 25. The Warriors (51 - 24) were led by Stephen Curry, as he went 11 - of - 19 from long range to tally 37 points, three rebounds, five assists and two steals. Kevin Durant finished with 23 points, 12 rebounds, seven assists and two blocks. Klay Thompson amassed 20 points, two rebounds and five assists. DeMarcus Cousins accrued 16 points and nine boards. The Timberwolves (34 - 41) were led by Andrew Wiggins, as he accumulated 24 points, nine rebounds and three assists. Josh Okogie amassed 21 points, five rebounds and four steals. Karl-Anthony Towns accumulated 15 points, 13 rebounds and seven assists. Gorgui Dieng was huge off the bench, providing 14 points and 10 boards. Minnesota suits up again on Saturday, as they play host to the Philadelphia 76ers. As for Golden State, they return to action on Sunday against the Charlotte Hornets.
