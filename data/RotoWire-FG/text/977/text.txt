The Chicago Bulls defeated the visiting Charlotte Hornets, 112 - 110, at United Center on Wednesday. Chicago was looking for their first win of the season in this competitive matchup, as it wasn't decided until the final seconds. In fact, there were 12 ties and 21 lead changes in total, as neither club led by more than 11 points. The Bulls actually scored the final four points of this game in the final 80 seconds, as that turned a two - point deficit into a two - point victory. Rebounding was the difference for Chicago, as they outrebounded Charlotte, 45 - 36. Transition offense was also key, with the Bulls winning the fastbreak differential, 16 - 4. Chicago ended up shooting 48 percent from the field and 35 percent from three - point range while holding Charlotte to just 44 percent from the field and 29 percent from long range. The Hornets (2 - 3) were led by Kemba Walker, as he tallied 23 points, three rebounds and six assists. Cody Zeller totaled 14 points, six rebounds and two assists. Nicolas Batum accrued 13 points, three rebounds and four assists while Jeremy Lamb amassed 12 points, three rebounds and three assists. The Bulls (1 - 3) got another gem out of Zach LaVine, as he led the way with 32 points, four rebounds and five assists. Cameron Payne was the only other starter in double figures, as he accumulated 21 points, five rebounds and four assists. Jabari Parker was huge off the bench, providing 11 points, nine rebounds, four assists and two steals. These teams will have a rematch on Friday, but that will be played in Charlotte.
