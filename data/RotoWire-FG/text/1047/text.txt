The Houston Rockets defeated the host Golden State Warriors, 135 - 134, at Oracle Arena on Thursday evening. The Warriors got out to a great start at home with a 34 - 22 lead after one quarter and expanded their lead to 70 - 53 by halftime. The Rockets turned it up in the third quarter and outscored the Warriors by 11, to cut the deficit to six at the start of the fourth. They kept the pressure on through the fourth and managed to tie the game in the final second of regulation thanks to pure heroics from James Harden. The Rockets scored the first points in OT, but the Warriors battled back and held a two - point lead with five seconds remaining. However, Harden had more magic left in his bag, as he drilled a seemingly impossible game - winning three - pointer over two Warriors defenders to steal the win on the road. Harden led the Rockets with 44 points, 15 assists, 10 rebounds, two blocks and one steal, in 44 minutes. Clint Capela followed up with 29 points, 21 rebounds, two steals, one assist and one block, while Austin Rivers added 18 points, four rebounds, four assists and one steal. The Rockets shot 46 percent from the field, including 21 - of - 54 from long range. Meanwhile, Stephen Curry led the Warriors with 35 points, six assists and three rebounds, in 40 minutes. Kevin Durant finished with 26 points, seven rebounds, three assists and one block, while Klay Thompson tallied 26 points, five rebounds, four assists, three steals and two blocks. The Warriors shot 38 percent from the field, including 14 - of - 37 from long range. The Warriors (25 - 14) will look to bounce back as they take on the Kings in Sacramento on Saturday. The Rockets (22 - 15) will look for their eighth straight win as they take on the Trail Blazers in Portland on Saturday.
