The Brooklyn Nets defeated the host Phoenix Suns, 98 - 92, at Talking Stick Resort Arena on Monday. This was a competitive game throughout, as it was within a 10 - point margin for the final 30 minutes. Despite that fact, Brooklyn never relinquished the lead in the second half. The key to victory for the Nets was defense, as they held the Suns to 35 percent from the field and 24 percent from three - point range. Brooklyn's ability to score in the paint was huge, as they scored 44 paint points to Phoenix's 30. The Nets (4 - 6) were led by D'Angelo Russell, who scored 14 of his 23 points in the fourth quarter. He also added six rebounds, nine assists and two steals on 8 - of - 13 shooting to his line. DeMarre Carroll recorded a double - double, totaling 14 points and 11 rebounds. Allen Crabbe was the only other starter in double figures, as he amassed 15 points and four rebounds. Joe Harris led the bench with 18 points and three rebounds. The Suns (4 - 7) had just four players score over seven points, as T.J. Warren led the team with 20 points, nine rebounds, two steals and three blocks. Devin Booker accumulated 18 points, two rebounds and two steals. Mike James collected 16 points, three rebounds, two assists and two steals. Alex Len scored just seven points, but provided 14 rebounds and two blocks off the bench. On deck for Phoenix is a home matchup against the Miami Heat on Wednesday. As for Brooklyn, they travel to face the Denver Nuggets on Tuesday for their next game.
