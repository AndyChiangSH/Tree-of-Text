The Houston Rockets defeated the Minnesota Timberwolves, 126 - 108, at Target Center on Tuesday night. The Rockets (43 - 13) and the Timberwolves (35 - 25) had a very intense game that went back and forth during the first half, but the Rockets pulled away in the fourth quarter and never looked back. Minnesota jumped to an eight - point lead in the first quarter, but Houston answered with a 12 - point lead in the second quarter, so they built a four - point lead at half time. A 30 - 30 third quarter kept things close heading into the fourth, but the Rockets dominated with a 42 - 28 lead in the final quarter that allowed them to ran away with the game with ease. With the win, Houston extended their winning run to nine games and remain one of the top teams in the Association right now. James Harden led the way for Houston with 34 points, 12 assists, six rebounds, and one block. Chris Paul came close to a triple - double since he finished with 13 points, eight rebounds, and seven assists. P.J. Tucker added 10 points, and the bench did a very good job as well. Ryan Anderson contributed 21 points while going 6 - for - 10 from three - point range, while Eric Gordon finished with 13 points. Karl-Anthony Towns was Minnesota's best player since he finished with 35 points and 12 rebounds. Jeff Teague chipped in with 25 points, eight assists and five rebounds. Jimmy Butler added 15 points, seven rebounds, and six assists. Taj Gibson had 12 points with five rebounds, and Jamal Crawford contributed off the bench with 11 points in 20 minutes. Andrew Wiggins struggled, however, finishing with just seven points (2 - 14 FG).
