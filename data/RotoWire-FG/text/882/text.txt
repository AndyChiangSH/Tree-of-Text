The Toronto Raptors defeated the Minnesota Timberwolves, 109 - 104, at Air Canada Center on Tuesday. It took 10 ties and five lead changes to determine a winner, as this game wasn't decided until the final seconds. In fact, it was a single - digit margin for the entirety of the second half, as Toronto outscored Minnesota, 24 - 17 in the fourth quarter. Ball movement was key for the Raptors, as they recorded 28 assists to the Timberwolves 21. Free-throw shooting was also a big factor, with Minnesota shooting 22 - of - 26 and Toronto hitting just 15 - of - 24. Transition offense was also a big factor, with Toronto winning the fastbreak battle, 13 - 1 The Timberwolves (32 - 22) had all five starters score in double figures, as Jimmy Butler led the way with 25 points, five rebounds and six assists. Andrew Wiggins amassed 15 points, eight rebounds and two assists. Karl-Anthony Towns amassed 11 points, 10 rebounds and three assists. The Raptors (34 - 15) were led by DeMar DeRozan, as he collected 23 points, four rebounds and eight assists. Kyle Lowry finished with 15 points, three rebounds and nine assists. Jonas Valanciunas tallied 18 points and 11 rebounds. Serge Ibaka accrued 15 points, six rebounds and three assists. Both teams return to action on Thursday, with Toronto traveling to face the Washington Wizards and Minnesota playing host to the Milwaukee Bucks.
