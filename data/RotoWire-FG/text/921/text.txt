The Milwaukee Bucks defeated the New York Knicks, 120 - 112, at BMO Harris Bradley Center on Friday. Milwaukee was squarely in control for the majority of this game, as they held the lead for all but one minute. In fact, the Bucks held a 16 - point lead in the second and fourth quarters, as Milwaukee led by at least eight points for the final eight minutes. Milwaukee's offense was unstoppable, as they connected on 56 percent from the field and 44 percent from three - point range. Free-throw shooting was huge as well, with the Bucks hitting 30 - of - 39 and New York connecting on just 11 - of - 14. The Knicks (24 - 42) were led by Tim Hardaway Jr., as he tallied 26 points and seven rebounds. Emmanuel Mudiay amassed 19 points, two rebounds and four assists. Enes Kanter amassed 16 points and eight rebounds while Troy Williams led the bench with 18 points. Kyle O'Quinn also played well off the bench, accumulating 14 points, six assists and three blocks. The Bucks (35 - 31) got a gem out of Khris Middleton, as he accrued 30 points and four rebounds on 11 - of - 16 from the field. Giannis Antetokounmpo collected 28 points, 10 rebounds, five assists, two steals and two blocks. John Henson amassed 14 points, four rebounds and four blocks. Jabari Parker provided 12 points, four rebounds and five assists off the bench. On deck for Toronto is a home matchup versus the Toronto Raptors on Sunday. As for Milwaukee, they travel to face the Memphis Grizzlies on Monday for their next fixture.
