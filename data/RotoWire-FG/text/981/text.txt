The visiting Utah Jazz defeated the Dallas Mavericks, 113 - 104, at American Airlines Center on Sunday. Utah withstood a tough comeback effort from the Mavericks, re - establishing a strong lead in the third quarter after having given away an 11 - point advantage in the second. The Jazz went on an 18 - 6 run in the late third and early fourth quarters, rebuilding a lead after the Mavericks tied it at 73 - 73. Dallas cut the lead to as little as six in the fourth, but Utah was able to hold firm until the final buzzer. Rudy Gobert led the Jazz (4 - 3) with 23 points and 16 rebounds in the win. Six of Gobert's rebounds came on the offensive end, and he shot 9 - of - 13 from the field. The reigning Defensive Player of the Year also tallied two steals and three blocks on the defensive end. Sophomore guard Donovan Mitchell also made a big contribution, scoring 20 points on 10 - of - 19 shooting. Dallas (2 - 4) saw a similarly impressive night from its own All-Star center, DeAndre Jordan. Jordan flirted with a triple - double, as he scored 12 points, grabbed 19 rebounds, and dished out nine assists in the loss. Dennis Smith, Jr. led the team with 27 points and hit 3 - of - 4 three - point attempts on the night. As a whole, though, the Mavericks could not find their rhythm offensively. The team shot just 42 percent on the night. The Mavericks will travel to San Antonio to take on the Spurs on Monday, while the Jazz have three days of rest before taking on the Timberwolves in Minnesota on Wednesday.
