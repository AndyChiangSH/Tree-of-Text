The Denver Nuggets defeated the visiting Toronto Raptors, 129 - 111, at Pepsi Center on Wednesday evening. The Nuggets (4 - 4) were not messing around in their first game back home after a four - game trip, as they poured in a season - high 129 points in a convincing victory over the Raptors (4 - 3). They pounced on the Raptors, taking a 34 - 19 lead by the end of the first quarter. Toronto woke up in the second quarter but only managed the trim one point off the lead before halftime. However, the Nuggets poured in 43 third - quarter points to take a 103 - 69 lead into the final frame, where they turned on cruise control. Jamal Murray was the high - point man for Denver, with 24 points on 8 - of - 10 shooting, along with three rebounds and two assists in 21 minutes. This marked the third straight 20 - point game for Murray. Nikola Jokic only scored eight points on 4 - of - 10 shooting, but dominated the glass with 16 rebounds and dropped 10 dimes, along with two blocks and a steal in 30 minutes. Paul Millsap added 20 points, four assists, three rebounds and two steals in 26 minutes. The Nuggets shot 53 percent from the field and 50 percent from long range, while assisting on 35 of their 47 field goals. Meanwhile, Norman Powell led the Raptors with 14 points on 6 - of - 11 shooting in 28 minutes. Four other players scored in double digits for Toronto, including Delon Wright, who finished with 12 points and six assists in 25 minutes off the bench. Serge Ibaka chipped in 11 points, while DeMar DeRozan finished with 10 points. Kyle Lowry had nine points, five rebounds and four assists. The Raptors will continue their road trip as they take on the Jazz in Utah on Friday. The Nuggets play host to the Heat on Friday.
