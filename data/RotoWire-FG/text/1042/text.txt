The Dallas Mavericks defeated the visiting Oklahoma City Thunder, 105 - 103, at American Airlines Center on Sunday. In a battle between Western Conference rivals, this game wasn't decided until the final seconds. Dallas made the run that mattered most though, as they finished the game on a 10 - 2 spurt to turn a six - point deficit into a two - point victory. There were 11 ties and 18 lead changes though, as neither club led by more than 11 points. Three-point shooting was the difference, with Dallas connecting on 14 - of - 39 and Oklahoma City hitting just 9 - of - 38. Rebounding was also critical, with the Mavericks outrebounding the Thunder, 49 - 39. The 13 steals recorded by Oklahoma City was the only thing keeping it close, as they won the turnover differential, 23 - 11. The Thunder (22 - 13) were led by Paul George, as he accrued 36 points, five rebounds and two steals. Russell Westbrook went just 4 - of - 22 from the field but still finished with nine points, nine rebounds, eight assists and six steals. Steven Adams accumulated eight points, nine rebounds, two assists and two steals while Dennis Schroder led the bench with 19 points, four rebounds and three assists. The Mavericks (17 - 18) were led by Luke Doncic, as he tallied 25 points, three rebounds and seven assists. Harrison Barnes totaled 16 points, six rebounds and two steals. Dennis Smith Jr. finished with 14 points and four rebounds while DeAndre Jordan collected 12 points, 17 rebounds, two steals and two blocks. J.J. Barea provided 10 assists off the bench to lead the team. These teams next game will actually be a rematch on Monday, although that game will be played in Oklahoma City.
