The Minnesota Timberwolves defeated the Orlando Magic, 120 - 103, at Target Center on Friday evening. The Magic (17 - 21) may have caught the host Timberwolves (18 - 21) a bit by surprise early, as they ambushed them out of the gates to the tune of a 40 - 31 first - quarter lead. That seemed to serve as a wake - up call for Minnesota, however, as they would go on to outscore their visitors by an 89 - 63 margin the rest of the way. Despite the double - digit margin of victory, the T-Wolves clearly dominated in only one area, that being rebounding - - they won the battle of the boards over Orlando by a 54 - 35 margin. Nikola Vucevic led the way for the Magic with 22 points, adding seven rebounds, three assists, one steal and one block. Evan Fournier followed with 21 points, four assists, two rebounds and one steal. Terrence Ross followed with a bench - leading 13 points and supplemented them with two rebounds and one assist. Jonathan Isaac and Aaron Gordon turned in 10 points and three rebounds apiece, with Gordon also dishing out six assists. Karl-Anthony Towns paced the Timberwolves with a 29 - point, 15 - rebound double - double that also included two assists, one steal and one block. Jeff Teague managed 23 points, 10 assists, two rebounds and one steal. Andrew Wiggins poured in 16 points. Taj Gibson generated a 14 - point, 10 - rebound double - double. Anthony Tolliver led the second unit with 11 points and also offered four rebounds, one assist, one steal and one block. Dario Saric contributed 10 points. The Magic remain on the road and visit the Los Angeles Clippers on Sunday afternoon, while the Timberwolves remain home and host the Los Angeles Lakers on Sunday afternoon as well.
