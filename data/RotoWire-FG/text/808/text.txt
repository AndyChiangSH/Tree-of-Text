The Golden State Warriors escaped a near - upset at Staples Center on Wednesday, defeating the Los Angeles Lakers, 127 - 123 in overtime. Golden State (16 - 6) looked shockingly sluggish throughout regulation, committing 20 turnovers and trailing for large portions of the second half. Former back - to - back MVP Steph Curry, in particular, started the game on a slow note, going just 3 - of - 10 and committing five turnovers in the first two and a half quarters. By overtime, however, Curry looked back to his MVP form, as he finished the game 9 - of - 20 from the field with 28 points. Curry sealed the game at the line in the final seconds after Draymond Green regained possession for the Warriors following a rare Curry miss at the charity stripe. Kevin Durant added an additional 29 points for the Warriors, shooting 12 - of - 25 from the field. Durant hit multiple late - game jumpers to help force overtime. Los Angeles (8 - 13) put up a valiant effort against the reigning champions, but ultimately came up short. Still, the team saw a career night from sophomore star Brandon Ingram. Ingram was the game's leading scorer at 32 points, as he put up a new career high on 12 - of - 21 shooting. Rookie Lonzo Ball also turned in an impressive performance. Although he has largely struggled with his shot this season, Ball hit three three - pointers in Wednesday's game. Two of those came during a personal 80 run that Ball went on in the third quarter. The Lakers added 49 points off the bench in what was a well - rounded team effort on Wednesday. The Warriors will visit Orlando on Friday, while the Lakers will visit Denver on Saturday.
