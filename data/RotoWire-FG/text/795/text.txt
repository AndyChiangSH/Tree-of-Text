The host Portland Trail Blazers narrowly defeated the Orlando Magic, 99 - 96, on Wednesday night at Moda Center. Portland (8 - 6) has had an up - and - down start to the season, but is now on a two - game win streak. While Orlando jumped out to an early lead, Portland fought back with a strong second quarter run, taking a four - point lead by halftime. As is routine at this point, the Blazers' success hinged largely on the play of star guards Damian Lillard and C.J. McCollum. Lillard flirted with a triple - double, scoring a team - high 26 points, pulling down 11 rebounds, and dishing seven assists. The star point guard helped put the game away, hitting a layup with 1:16 remaining in the fourth quarter to put Portland up by eight. McCollum, meanwhile, hit multiple big shots late in the game. He finished with 24 points and also came away with three steals on the defensive end. Orlando (8 - 7) has now lost three straight games after a red - hot start to the season. Though the Magic are having a down stretch, they remain in the top five in both three - point makes and three - point percentage. The uptick in shooting has helped fuel the new - look Orlando offense, which is seeing more success in head coach Frank Vogel's second season with the team. On Wednesday, the team again shot well from deep, hitting 11 three - pointers on 38 percent shooting from beyond the arc. Each member of the Magic's starting five hit at least one shot from beyond the arc. The team also saw strong bench play, headed up by sixth man Jonathan Simmons. Simmons scored 16 points off the bench, and has now scored 13 or more points in six straight games. The Blazers will visit Sacramento on Friday, while Orlando will host the Jazz on Saturday.
