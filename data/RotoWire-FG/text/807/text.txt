The Houston Rockets defeated the Indiana Pacers, 118 - 97, at Toyota Center on Wednesday evening. The Rockets (17 - 4) entered this game as one of the best offenses in the league, once again proving it with a stellar performance. The Pacers (12 - 10) simply didn't have answers for Houston and at times it felt as if they were playing at an entirely different pace. Houston built a five - point lead at halftime after Indiana won the first quarter, but they pulled away in the third with a 35 - 22 win over those 12 minutes that put the game away for good. The Pacers couldn't react in the fourth, and Houston extended their winning streak to six games with another huge performance at home. James Harden was the biggest star of the game for the Rockets, as the MVP candidate came close to a triple - double following a huge performance of 28 points, 10 assists, and eight rebounds. Chris Paul added 13 points with seven assists, while Clint Capela once again contributed with a double - double of 22 points and 13 boards. All five members of Houston's starting lineup scored in double digits. Thaddeus Young led the way for the Pacers since he finished with 23 points, but his efforts were not enough. Victor Oladipo filled the stat sheet nicely thanks to 19 points, five assists, five steals and three blocks. Myles Turner added 15 points, 10 rebounds, and four blocks. Lance Stephenson also provided a boost off the bench since he contributed with 11 points, four boards, and four assists.
