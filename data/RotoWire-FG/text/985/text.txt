The Charlotte Hornets defeated the visiting Cleveland Cavaliers, 126 - 94, at Spectrum Center on Saturday. Charlotte came into this game as a sizable favorite and they showed why in this 31 - point win. They really took control in the second half, outscoring Cleveland, 67 - 42. Rebounding was the difference for the Hornets, as they outrebounded the Cavaliers, 49 - 28. Charlotte ended up shooting 57 percent from the field while holding Cleveland to just 43 percent shooting. The Hornets did most of their damage down low, as they outscored the Cavaliers, 70 - 40, in the paint. The Cavaliers (1 - 8) had just one starter reach double figures in scoring, as Sam Dekker led them with 11 points. Cedi Osman accrued nine points, four rebounds and three assists. Tristan Thompson amassed eight points and eight boards. J.R. Smith led the bench with 14 points, two rebounds, four assists and three steals. The Hornets (5 - 5) were led by Jeremy Lamb, as he totaled 19 points, eight rebounds and two assists. Kemba Walker finished with 18 points, five rebounds and four assists. Nicolas Batum accumulated nine points, seven rebounds and eight assists. Malk Monk led the bench with 16 points and five assists. Tony Parker also played well off the bench, providing 12 points, four rebounds and four assists. On deck for Cleveland is a road matchup versus the Orlando Magic on Monday. As for Charlotte, they return to action on Tuesday, when they play host to the Atlanta Hawks.
