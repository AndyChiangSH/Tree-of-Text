The Orlando Magic trounced the New Orleans Pelicans, 118 - 88, at Smoothie King Center on Tuesday. It was a full - fledged blowout, as Orlando (26 - 32) never let New Orleans (25 - 33) come especially close. Four Orlando starters scored at least 20 points in the win, including a team - high 25 points from Nikola Vucevic. The newly - minted All-Star added 17 rebounds in the win and out - dueled Pelicans superstar Anthony Davis. Davis, who has had a rocky month since requesting a trade and then not being dealt, went just 1 - of - 9 from the field in 24 minutes of play. Forwards Jonathan Isaac and Aaron Gordon contributed another 20 points apiece for Orlando. In general, the team saw stellar offensive play, knocking down 13 three - point shots and shooting 21 - of - 22 at the free - throw line. The Magic also held a substantial advantage on the glass, outrebounding New Orleans 57 - 41. The Pelicans, meanwhile, were limited to just 38 percent shooting in the loss. What's more, New Orleans could not seem to get to the line, attempting only eight free - throws on the night. E'Twaun Moore led the team with only 19 points in the loss. Both teams will suit up once more before the All-Star break, with Orlando hosting the Charlotte Hornets on Thursday and New Orleans hosting the red - hot Oklahoma City Thunder.
