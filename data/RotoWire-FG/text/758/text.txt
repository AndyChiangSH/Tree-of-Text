The Boston Celtics defeated the Washington Wizards, 123 - 101, in Game 5 of the Eastern Conference semifinals at TD Garden on Wednesday. Home teams have been dominant in this series thus far and that proved to be the case yet again here. In fact, Boston led by 12 points after the first quarter and never allowed Washington to get within that 12 - point margin for the rest of the game. The Celtics offense was unstoppable, as they shot 53 percent from the field and 49 percent from three - point range. They held the Wizards to just 39 percent from the field and 24 percent from three - point range on their end. Much of that was due to the ball movement, with Boston recording 33 assists and Washington dishing out just 21. The Wizards (2 - 3) are going to need to win out in this series to reach the Eastern Conference Finals, as this was one of their worst performances of the postseason. John Wall led Washington with 21 points, four rebounds, four assists and two blocks. Bradley Beal collected 16 points, two rebounds and four assists. Those two combined to go 14 - of - 36 from the field. Otto Porter accumulated 13 points, four rebounds and two assists. Kellu Oubre Jr. led the bench with 13 points and three rebounds. The Celtics (3 - 2) are now one win away from an Eastern Conference Finals appearance, as they have two opportunities to achieve that goal. Avery Bradley was the player of the game, as he tallied 29 points, six rebounds and three assists on 12 - of - 19 from the field. Isaiah Thomas accrued 18 points, three rebounds and nine assists. Al Horford totaled 19 points, six rebounds, seven assists and three blocks on 8 - of - 9 shooting. Jae Crowder amassed 18 points and eight rebounds. Marcus Smart was a huge spark off the bench, providing nine points, 11 rebounds, six assists and two steals. Game 6 will be in Washington on Friday.
