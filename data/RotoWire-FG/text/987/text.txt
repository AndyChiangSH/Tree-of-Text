The Houston Rockets defeated the Indiana Pacers, 98 - 94, at Bankers Life Fieldhouse on Monday night. The Rockets (4 - 5) and the Pacers (7 - 4) played a very interesting game on Monday, as neither club gave an inch to the other and the game had to be decided in the final minutes thanks to an impressive comeback from the visiting team. Houston was up 29 - 22 following the first quarter, but Indiana flipped the script and won the second quarter 33 - 24, as well as the third one 20 - 16, to put themselves ahead by six points entering the final 12 minutes of action. But the Rockets didn't give up, and won the final quarter 29 - 19 to pull away for good. Both teams struggled with their shooting percentages, as Houston shot 44 percent from the field and 32 from three - point range, as opposed to Indiana's 44 and 29, respectively. James Harden led the Rockets offensively with 28 points, but he also added six assists, four rebounds and three steals, while Clint Capela back him up with a double - double of 18 points and 10 rebounds. Chris Paul only had nine points, but contributed with 13 assists and five boards. James Ennis III had 13 points, while Carmelo Anthony chipped in with eight points and five rebounds off the bench. Victor Oladipo paced the Pacers offensively with 28 points, six rebounds and two assists, while Bojan Bogdanovic contributed with 11 points. Thaddeus Young and Myles Turner disappointed, however, since they combined for only 15 points while going 6 - of - 17 from the field between them. Domantas Sabonis (17 points, eight rebounds, five assists) and Tyreke Evans (11 points in only 16 minutes) looked solid coming off the bench, though.
