The Los Angeles Lakers took down the Utah Jazz, 113 - 109, at staples Center on Sunday evening. It was the second straight win for the Lakers (37 - 44) against a playoff team, as LA managed to upset the Jazz (49 - 31) despite its slough of injuries. Operating without four members of their opening day starting five, the Lakers shot over 51 percent against a typically ironclad Utah defense. LA was tenacious on the glass, grabbing 16 offensive rebounds and outrebounding Utah 49 - 35 overall. Recent G-League callup Jemerrio Jones stood out after earning a start on Sunday. Jones grabbed 16 rebounds and dished out five assists while playing a whopping 38 minutes. Kentavious Caldwell-Pope logged a game - high 42 minutes and scored a team - high 32 points in the win. LA also saw 22 points from veteran center JaVale McGee. Utah was led by 21 points and 10 rebounds from Rudy Gobert. Donovan Mitchell scored another 19 points for the Jazz. The loss comes at an inopportune time for the Jazz, who are fighting for playoff seeding in a crowded Western Conference. With the loss, they now sit two games behind the fourth - place Portland Trail Blazers. Utah will host the Denver Nuggets on Tuesday, while the Lakers will clash with Portland.
