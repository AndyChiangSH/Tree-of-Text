The Cleveland Cavaliers defeated the New York Knicks, 113 - 106, at Quicken Loans Arena on Wednesday evening. The Cavaliers (7 - 21) and Knicks (8 - 21) demonstrated that their records are almost identical for good reason, as they engaged in an entertaining back - and - forth battle. Cleveland jumped out to a 39 - 26 first - quarter lead, but New York erased that deficit and then some by outscoring their hosts by a 63 - 47 margin over the middle periods. That set up a thrilling fourth quarter, one that saw the Knicks take a 106 - 105 lead with just 32 seconds remaining. However, an 80 run by the Cavs sealed the win, with Rodney Hood, Cedi Osman, Matthew Dellavedova and David Nwaba all contributing. Enes Kanter and Tim Hardaway, Jr. co - led the Knicks with 20 points apiece. Kanter added 10 rebounds and three assists, while Hardaway brought in five boards, dished out one assist and recorded both a steal and a block. Kevin Knox turned in 19 points, seven rebounds, one assist and two blocks. Emmanuel Mudiay provided 16 points, eight rebounds, seven assists and two steals. Frank Ntilikina matched Mudiay's scoring total off the bench. Jordan Clarkson paced the Cavaliers with 28 points off the bench, a total he supplemented with five rebounds and two assists. Rodney Hood supplied 23 points and five rebounds. Collin Sexton registered 19 points, three rebounds and two assists. Matthew Dellavedova enjoyed a successful return to Cleveland, posting 15 points, four rebounds, three assists and a steal. The Knicks remain on the road and take on the Charlotte Hornets on Friday night, while the Cavaliers remain home and take on the Milwaukee Bucks on Friday evening as well.
