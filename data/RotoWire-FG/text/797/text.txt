The Houston Rockets defeated the host Phoenix Suns, 142 - 116, at Talking Stick Resort Arena on Thursday evening. The Rockets (11 - 4) took off right out of the gate, scoring 45 points in each of the first two quarters, on their way to a franchise - best 90 - point first half. The 90 - point first half ties the record for the second - most points scored in a half in NBA history. This game marked Chris Paul's return to the line up for the Rockets, after he missed all but the first game of the season. Paul finished with 11 points, 10 assists, four rebounds and two steals, in just 21 minutes of action. James Harden was the high - point man in the contest, with a ridiculous 48 points, seven assists, two rebounds, two steals and one block, in only 35 minutes. Clint Capela notched a double - double with 12 points and 10 rebounds, while Ryan Anderson poured in 24 points on 7 - of - 12 shooting. The Rockets shot 48 percent from the field and tallied 27 assists. Meanwhile, the Suns (5 - 11) shot 45 percent from the field, including 33 percent from long range, rebounded competitively, tallied 28 assists and tied the Rockets with 14 turnovers, but fell short as they were out done from three - point range and from the foul line. Greg Monroe logged 26 minutes in his debut for the Suns, and finished with 20 points and 11 rebounds. Devin Booker finished with 18 points and 10 assists, while Josh Jackson chipped in 16 points, in 28 minutes off the bench. Troy Daniels added 23 points, in 26 minutes off the bench. The Suns will be right back in action, as they head straight to L.A., where they will take on the Lakers on Friday. The Rockets take on the Grizzlies in Memphis on Saturday.
