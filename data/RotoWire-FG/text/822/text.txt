The Toronto Raptors defeated the Sacramento Kings, 102 - 87, at Golden 1 Center on Sunday afternoon. The Raptors (17 - 7) had reason to believe they'd have a relatively easy assignment despite the road setting, as the Kings (8 - 18) didn't have the talent to match Toronto on paper. While that's not always the best measuring stick for how a game will unfold, it was reasonably accurate Sunday. The visitors started fast and kept their foot on the gas, turning a modest 22 - 17 first - quarter lead into a 54 - 46 halftime advantage. The Raptors would go on to outscore Sacramento by a 48 - 41 margin in the second half, sealing the double - digit road victory in efficient fashion. While the Kings actually enjoyed a strong shooting afternoon marked by a 48 percent success rate from the field, they ultimately committed 21 turnovers while only eliciting 13 from Toronto. DeMar DeRozan led the way for the Raptors with 25 points, nine assists and three rebounds. Serge Ibaka managed 20 points, four rebounds, one assist and one block. Kyle Lowry contributed a 15 - point, 12 - rebound double - double that also included six assists and one steal. C.J. Miles tallied a bench - leading 11 points and added two rebounds, one assist and five steals. Jonas Valanciunas contributed nine points, eight boards and a steal. Backup Jakob Poeltl matched his scoring total and also hauled in four rebounds. Zach Randolph paced the Kings with a 19 - point, 11 - rebound double - double that also included one assist and one steal. Buddy Hield led the bench with 17 points and complemented them with three rebounds, two assists and one steal. Bogdan Bogdanovic supplied 15 points, one rebound and one assist. Jakarr Sampson was solid off the bench with 11 points, four rebounds, three blocks and one steal. The Raptors continue their West Coast swing with a visit to the Los Angeles Clippers on Tuesday evening, while the Kings remain home and tangle with the Phoenix Suns on Wednesday night.
