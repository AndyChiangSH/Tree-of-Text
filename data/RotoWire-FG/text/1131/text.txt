The Dallas Mavericks defeated the visiting Phoenix Suns, 120 - 109, at American Airlines Center on Wednesday. While this wasn't a wire - to - wire win for the Mavericks, they dominated the final three quarters. In fact, a 33 - 18 first quarter really set the tone, as Phoenix could never get the game within four from there. Rebounding was a clear difference, with Dallas outrebounding Phoenix, 64 - 38. Ball movement was also key, with the Mavericks out - assiting the Suns, 28 - 21. The Suns (19 - 63) were led by Jamal Crawford, as he provided 51 points, five rebounds and five assists off the bench. He became the oldest player in NBA history to score at least 50 points. Josh Jackson led the starters with 17 points and eight rebounds. Dragan Bender totaled 12 points, 11 rebounds and six assists. The Mavericks (33 - 48) were led by Dirk Nowitzki, as he tallied 30 points, eight rebounds and three assists on 31 shots. Luka Doncic picked up another triple - double, collecting 21 points, 16 rebounds, 11 assists and three steals. Dwight Powell amassed 11 points and 11 boards while Dorian Finney-Smith led the bench with 15 points and 10 boards. This marked the season finale for the Suns, who finished with the second - worst record in the league. The Mavericks will have to play again on Wednesday when they travel to face the San Antomio Spurs.
