The Minnesota Timberwolves defeated the Toronto Raptors, 115 - 109, at Target Center on Saturday. Six ties and 11 lead changes don't show how close this game was, as neither team ever led by more than 11 points. The difference for the T'Wolves was their ability to get to the free - throw line, as they went 32 - of - 42 from the free - throw line, while the Raptors went just 19 - of - 21. Rebounding was also key to victory for Minnesota, as they outrebounded Toronto, 44 - 35. The Raptors (31 - 14) were led by Kyle Lowry, as he tallied 40 points, five rebounds and five assists. DeMar DeRozan amassed 20 points, three rebounds, six assists and three steals. Delon Wright led the bench with 12 points, six rebounds and six assists. The Timberwolves (30 - 18) were led by Andrew Wiggins, as he accrued 29 points, five rebounds and three assists. Karl-Anthony Towns totaled 22 points, 10 rebounds and two assists. Jeff Teague only scored five points on 2 - of - 11 from the field but did hand out 10 assists. On deck for Minnesota is a road matchup versus the Los Angeles Clippers on Monday. As for Toronto, they travel to play the Atlanta Hawks for their next fixture on Wednesday.
