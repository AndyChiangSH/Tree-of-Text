The Indiana Pacers defeated the host Milwaukee Bucks, 103 - 96, at BMO Harris Bradley Center on Friday evening. It was a very low - scoring first quarter, as it was tied 8 - 8 six minutes into the game. The Bucks emerged with a 21 - 18 advantage heading into the second. Things picked up in the second quarter, where the teams traded buckets, with the Pacers outscoring the Bucks, 27 - 25, in the quarter to cut the deficit to one, as they trailed 46 - 45 at the half. The Bucks started out well in the third quarter, but the Pacers turned it up late with a 12 - 1 run to over take the lead, and take a 78 - 67 lead into the final frame. The Bucks battled in the fourth, chipping away at the lead and managed to make it a three - point game with eight seconds left, however the Pacers closed it out with four made free throws. Six players scored in double digits for the Pacers, led by Victor Oladipo, who finished with 21 points, seven rebounds, six assists, two steals and one block, in 35 minutes. Lance Stephenson came up big with 16 points, three rebounds, three assists and two steals, in 25 minutes off the bench. Bojan Bogdanovic came up clutch with four free throws to seal the win, and finished with 14 points, four rebounds and four assists, in 29 minutes. The Pacers shot 45 percent from the field, including 6 - of - 16 from long range. Meanwhile, Khris Middleton led the Bucks with 30 points on 11 - of - 21 shooting, in 40 minutes of action. Giannis Antetokounmpo followed up with 24 points, 10 rebounds, three assists, three blocks and one steal, while Eric Bledsoe tallied 15 points, seven rebounds, six assists, two rebounds and two steals. The Bucks shot 38 percent from the field, including 9 - of - 26 from long range. The Bucks (33 - 29) will look to bounce back as they play host to the 76ers on Sunday. The Pacers (35 - 27) take on the Wizards in Washington on Sunday.
