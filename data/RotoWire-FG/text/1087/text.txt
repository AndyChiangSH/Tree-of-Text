The Sacramento Kings defeated the visiting Phoenix Suns, 117 - 104, at Golden 1 Center on Sunday. A 33 - 23 first quarter really set the foundation for Sacramento in this game, as they won in wire - to - wire fashion. The Kings defense was critical, as they recorded 12 steals and 11 blocks while holding the Suns to 42 percent from the field and 22 percent from three - point range. Sacramento ended up shooting 45 percent from the field. The Kings also recorded four more assists than the Kings while winning the turnover differential, 17 - 12. Sacramento's bench also outscored Pheonix's bench, 53 - 25. The Suns (11 - 47) were led by Devin Booker, as he tallied 27 points, eight rebounds and six assists. Josh Jackson accrued 18 points, five rebounds and three blocks. DeAndre Ayton amassed 16 points and nine boards. Richaun Holmes led the bench with 10 points and nine boards while Tyler Johnson totaled nine points, five rebounds and three assists in his first start. The Kings (30 - 26) were led by Marvin Bagley III, as he provided 32 points and seven rebounds on 10 - of - 15 shooting off the bench. Bogdan Bogdanovic also played well off the bench, accumulating 14 points, five rebounds, five assists and three steals. Buddy Hield led the starters with 18 points and six boards while De'Aaron Fox collected 17 points, six rebounds and nine assists. Both teams return to action on Wednesday, with Phoenix traveling to face the Los Angeles Clippers and Sacramento facing the Denver Nuggets on the road as well.
