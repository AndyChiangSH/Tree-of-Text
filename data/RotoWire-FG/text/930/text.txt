The Indiana Pacers defeated Los Angeles Lakers, 110 - 100, at Bankers Life Fieldhouse on Monday. The Lakers (31 - 39) jumped to a quick lead against the Pacers (41 - 30), but they couldn't maintain what they did during the first half and crumbled in the second half, as Indiana took advantage of that to secure a massive win at home that puts them one step closer to playoff contention. The Lakers built a 37 - 29 lead after the first quarter, but the Pacers showed signs of a potential comeback when they made it a two - point game at halftime (59 - 57). Indiana took control of the game with a 35 - 21 third quarter, and the Lakers didn't have enough in them to turn things around in the fourth despite the fact they won the final 12 minutes (20 - 18). Indiana closed things out with ease, securing a huge win in front of their own crowd. Myles Turner returned to the starting lineup in this game and did with a great performance, as he finished with 21 points, seven rebounds and three assists. Victor Oladipo added 20 points with four rebounds and three steals. Thaddeus Young had 18 points, nine rebounds, four assists and two blocks. Darren Collison had 15 points and eight assists, while Lance Stephenson added 16 points and five rebounds coming off the bench. Kyle Kuzma led the way for the Lakers with a 27 - point, eight - rebound performance. Robin Lopez had 23 points, eight rebounds, and three assists. Julius Randle chipped in with 21 points, nine boards, and three assists. Kentavious Caldwell-Pope contributed 12 points and seven rebounds, while Lonzo Ball chipped in with just four points (1 - 4 FG) but added eight assists.
