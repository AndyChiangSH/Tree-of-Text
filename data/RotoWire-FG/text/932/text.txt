The Brooklyn Nets defeated the visiting Memphis Grizzlies, 118 - 115, at Barclays Center on Monday evening. The first quarter kicked off the high - scoring contest with both the Nets and Grizzlies getting out to a good start. The Nets took a 32 - 31 lead after the first quarter. The Grizzlies rallied ahead to a 47 - 40 lead mid way through the second quarter, but the Nets battled back to take a two - point, 65 - 63, lead by halftime. The Grizzlies came out strong in the third and outscored the Nets 31 - 22 in the quarter, to take a 94 - 87 lead heading into the final frame. The Nets answered back in the fourth, tying it up at 94 with 10 minutes left in the game. They then used a 90 run mid way through the quarter to take an eight point lead, which was enough of a cushion to keep them ahead the rest of the way. Allen Crabbe led the Nets with 22 points, three rebounds, two assists and one block, in 27 minutes. Caris LeVert tallied 22 points, three rebounds, two assists and one block, in 24 minutes off the bench. D'Angelo Russell finished with 14 points, seven assists, five rebounds, two steals and two blocks, in 29 minutes. The Nets shot 50 percent from the field, including 16 - of - 40 from long range. Meanwhile, JaMychal Green came up big for Memphis, with 14 points, 16 rebounds, three assists, three steals and one block in 29 minutes. Andrew Harrison tallied 19 points, eight assists, one rebound and one block, while Wayne Selden added 17 points, three rebounds and two assists, in 23 minutes off the bench. The Grizzlies shot 52 percent from the field, including 10 - of - 23 from long range. The Grizzlies (19 - 51) will look to bounce back as they take on the 76ers in Philadelphia on Wednesday. The Nets (23 - 48) play host to the Hornets on Wednesday.
