The Miami Heat defeated the New York Knicks, 110 - 87, at American Airlines Arena on Wednesday evening. Despite the lopsided final score in favor of the home squad, it was the Knicks (1 - 4) that looked like the early favorite for a blowout win. New York held a 27 - 16 lead after one quarter, but the Heat (2 - 2) quickly erased their deficit with a 31 - 18 advantage in the second period. Their dominance only expanded coming out of intermission, as they compiled 45 points while limiting their visitors to 20 in a decisive third period that essentially put the game away. As the final score implies, Miami won the battle on the stat sheet in the majority of categories, and they out - shot the Knicks by a 48 percent - 36 percent margin. Dewayne Dotson paced the Knicks with 20 points from the bench, a total he supplemented with 10 rebounds, two assists, one steal and one block. Tim Hardaway, Jr. poured in 14 points and added three rebounds and two assists. Mario Hezonja was the only other double - digit scorer with 13 points. Enes Kanter logged only 19 minutes on his way to an eight - point, five - rebound night. Hassan Whiteside led the Heat with a 22 - point, 14 - rebound double - double that also included three blocks. Josh Richardson supplied 21 points, eight rebounds, one assist and one steal. Rodney McGruder drew another start and posted 19 points, six rebounds, five assists and one steal. Goran Dragic pitched in 13 points, five rebounds and three assists. Kelly Olynyk poured in 11 points, dished out six assists, hauled in three rebounds and recorded one steal. The Knicks head back home and await a visit from the Golden State Warriors on Friday night, while the Heat are also home for their next game, a Saturday night matchup versus the Portland Trail Blazers.
