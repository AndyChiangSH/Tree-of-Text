The Cleveland Cavaliers defeated the Indiana Pacers, 92 - 91, at Bankers Life Fieldhouse on Tuesday. The Cavaliers (8 - 23) were one of the worst teams in the Eastern Conference entering Tuesday's action, so there's no question that this win against the Pacers (20 - 11), who were riding a seven - game winning streak, should qualify as quite an upset. The game was tight and sloppy during most of the game, as both teams struggled from the field during the entire contest, but they saved the best for last. With Indiana up by only one point, Victor Oladipo missed two pivotal free throws, and that gave Cleveland a chance they wouldn't waste away. Rodney Hood missed a mid - range jumper, but Larry Nance Jr. tipped it home on the rebound to give the Cavs a huge win on the road, and their first one this season when they are trailing after three quarters. The final score in each quarter was: 21 - 19, 24 - 25, 22 - 24, 25 - 23. Larry Nance Jr. might have been the hero for Cleveland, but he also filled the stat sheet admirably in this game since he finished with 15 points, 16 rebounds, six assists, and three steals. Rodney Hood was the leading scorer with 17 points, and rookie Collin Sexton had 12 points. Cedi Osman chipped in with 13 points and seven rebounds, while Matthew Dellavedova (10 points, four assists) and Alec Burks (14 points) looked solid off the bench as well. Six Indiana players scored in double digits and Domantas Sabonis was the leading scorer thanks to a 17 - point, eight - rebound performance off the bench. Victor Oladipo had 12 points, but struggled from the field (4 - 15) and missed two clutch free throws. Bojan Bogdanovic added 14 points, and Myles Turner had a double - double of 12 points and 10 rebounds.
