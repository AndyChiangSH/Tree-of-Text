The Indiana Pacers defeated the visiting Chicago Bulls, 125 - 86, at Bankers Life Fieldhouse on Saturday evening. The first quarter was close but the Pacers pulled away in the last couple minutes to take a four - point lead heading in the second. They took off in the second quarter, outscoring the Bulls, 33 - 11, to take a 64 - 37 lead into halftime. They kept the pressure on in the second half, outscoring the Bulls 61 - 49 the rest of the way to complete the blowout win. The win ends a five - game losing streak for the Pacers. Victor Oladipo led the Pacers with 23 points, nine assists, six rebounds and five steals, in just 23 minutes, in his first game back from injury. Domantas Sabonis followed up with 22 points on 9 - of - 12 shooting, in 26 minutes off the bench, while Darren Collison and Myles Turner added 13 points each. The Pacers shot 56 percent from the field, including 15 - of - 31 from long range. Meanwhile, Bobby Portis was the high - point man for the Bulls, with 15 points on 4 - of - 7 shooting, in 22 minutes off the bench. Lauri Markkanen and Denzel Valentine finished with 13 points each, while Kris Dunn chipped in eight points and eight assists. The Bulls shot 38 percent from the field, including 10 - of - 32 from long range. The Bulls (14 - 26) will look to bounce back as they play host to the Rockets on Monday. The Pacers (20 - 19) play host to the Bucks on Monday.
