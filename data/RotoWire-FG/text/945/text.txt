The Indiana Pacers defeated the host Los Angeles Clippers, 111 - 104, at Staples Center on Sunday. Despite the fact that Los Angeles led for the majority of this game, Indiana led when it mattered most. In fact, the Pacers rode a 35 - 26 fourth quarter en route to their comeback win, as they ended the game on a 60 run. While many of the statistics were even, Indiana won because of rebounding and turnovers. The Pacers won the rebounding differential, 40 - 36 and forced 13 turnovers from the Clipper while committing just 10 on their end. The Indiana starters really set the tone, as they outscored Los Angeles starters, 91 - 63 The Pacers (46 - 31) got a gem out of Victor Oladipo, as he tallied 30 points, four rebounds, 12 assists and five steals on 11 - of - 17 from the field. Myles Turner amassed 24 points and eight rebounds. Bojan Bogdanovic totaled 15 points and five rebounds while Darren Collison finished with 14 points and four assists. The Clippers (41 - 36) were led by Tobias Harris, as he collected 21 points, four rebounds and seven assists. Austin Rivers accrued 18 points, three rebounds, seven assists and two steals. No other starter reached double figures, as DeAndre Jordan amassed eight points and 12 boards. Lou Williams led the bench with 20 points, two rebounds, three assists and two steals. Montrezl Harrel provided 19 points, three rebounds and two assists off the bench as well. Both teams return to action on Sunday, with Los Angeles playing host to the San Antonio Spurs and Indiana traveling to face the Denver Nuggets.
