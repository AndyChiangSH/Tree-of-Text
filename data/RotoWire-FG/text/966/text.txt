The Golden State Warriors defeated the visiting San Antonio Spurs, 99 - 91, in Game 5 of the Western Conference first - round playoffs at Oracle Arena on Tuesday. This will mark the end of the Spurs season, as they were simply outmatched by the Warriors in this series. Despite the fact that San Antonio made this game close at the end thanks to a 26 - 20 fourth quarter, Golden State held the lead for all but two minutes early in the first half. Defense was key for the Warriors, as they held the Spurs to just 37 percent from the field and 23 percent from three - point range. Fastbreak scoring was also a decisive statistic, with Golden State winning that battle, 210. The Warriors won despite going just 5 - of - 27 from long range as well. Rim protection was also key for Golden State, as they recorded five blocks while the Spurs failed to record one. The Spurs (1 - 4) were led by LaMarcus Aldridge, as he tallied 30 points, 12 rebounds and four assists. Patty Mills was the only other starter in double figures, as he amassed 18 points and five boards. Manu Ginobili led the bench with 10 points, five rebounds and seven assists. Rudy Gay went just 4 - of - 15 from the field to finish with nine points, four rebounds and two assists. The Warriors (4 - 1) were led by Kevin Durant, as he accrued 25 points, six rebounds, five assists and two blocks. Klay Thompson totaled 24 points, five rebounds and four assists. Draymond Green collected 17 points, 18 rebounds and seven assists. David West was a nice spark off the bench, providing four points, seven rebounds, three assists and two steals. Golden State will now face the New Orleans Pelicans in the second - round, as the Warriors will host for the first two games of that seven - game series.
