The Philadelphia 76ers defeated the visiting New York Knicks, 118 - 101, at Wells Fargo Center on Wednesday evening. The 76ers got out to a good start, with a 10 - point lead midway through the first quarter, where they went on to outscore the Knicks, 37 - 28. The Sixers expanded their lead to 15 early in the second quarter, but the Knicks rallied back to tie it up at 56 with just under three minutes left in the half, before trailing 67 - 63 at halftime. The Knicks came out strong in the third quarter and kept it close for the first few minutes, however the 76ers took over the remainder of the quarter with a 19 - 8 run, to take a 93 - 80 lead into the fourth. The rest of the game belonged to Philly, as the 76ers cruised to their eighth straight win. Joel Embiid (face) was forced to leave the game after a collision with Markelle Fultz, early in the second quarter. Dario Saric led the Sixers with 26 points, 14 rebounds, five assists and two steals, in 35 minutes. Ben Simmons finished with 13 points, 10 assists, eight rebounds and one blocks, while J.J. Redick tallied 21 points, six rebounds, five assists and one steal. The 76ers shot 46 percent from the field, including 9 - of - 31 from long range. Meanwhile, Michael Beasley was big for the Knicks, with 22 points, eight rebounds, three assists and two blocks. Emmanuel Mudiay also finished with 22 points, and added four rebounds, two assists, two blocks and one steal. Enes Kanter tallied 17 points, 14 rebounds, one assist and one block, while Trey Burke finished with 18 points, six assists, two rebounds and one steal. The Knicks shot 38 percent from the field, including 9 - of - 31 from long range. The Knicks (27 - 49) will look to avoid a third straight loss, as they play host to the Pistons on Saturday. The 76ers (44 - 30) take on the Hawks in Atlanta on Friday.
