The Trail Blazers (14 - 11) were seemingly set up for success Thursday, considering they were back on their home floor and facing a significantly short - handed Suns (4 - 21) squad. Both Devin Booker (hamstring) and T.J. Warren (ankle) were out for Phoenix, while first overall pick Deandre Ayton was bumped to the second unit in favor of Richaun Holmes. The disparity between the two clubs was evident from the opening tip, as Portland jumped out to a jaw - dropping 34 - 9 first - quarter lead. The Blazers ultimately built a 62 - 41 halftime advantage and cruised through the second half as well. As the final score implies, they won the battle in the majority of categories handily, including a significant 56 - 41 edge in the rebounding department. Troy Daniels led the Suns in the rout with 15 points off the bench. Justin Jackson followed with 14 points, seven rebounds, three assists and three steals. Elie Okobo and Ayton followed with matching 11 - point tallies. Okobo added five rebounds, an assist, a steal and a block, while Ayton brought down eight rebounds, dished out three assists, and recorded both a steal and a block. Holmes finished with nine points, four rebounds and one assist. Damian Lillard paced the Trail Blazers with 25 points, adding eight assists, five rebounds, one steal and one block. Jake Layman followed with a bench - leading 24 points, complementing them with seven rebounds and two steals. Zach Collins poured in 10 points, while Jusuf Nurkic mustered nine points, 14 rebounds, two assists, one steal and one block. The Suns return home to tangle with the Miami Heat on Friday night, while the Trail Blazers remain home and face the Minnesota Timberwolves on Saturday evening.
