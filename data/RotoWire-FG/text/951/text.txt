The Houston Rockets defeated the visiting Portland Trail Blazers, 96 - 94, at Toyota Center on Thursday evening. The Rockets got out to a great start at home, as they used an 18 - 5 run to close the first quarter with a 36 - 17 lead. The Rockets maintained a solid lead through the second, and built their cushion to 22 points, as they led 66 - 44 by halftime. The Trail Blazers came out strong in the third quarter and began chipping away. They outscored the Rockets by nine in the quarter to cut the deficit to 13, as they trailed 84 - 71 heading into the fourth. The Rockets looked in control for most of the fourth, even leading by 10 with 1:47 left in the game. However, the Trail Blazers made a brilliant run to tie it up at 94 with just six seconds left. Finally, the Rockets got the ball into the hands of Chris Paul who buried the game - winning floater, off the glass, for the win at the buzzer. Paul led the way for Houston, with 27 points, five assists and four rebounds, in 31 minutes. James Harden followed up with 24 points, seven assists, six rebounds and one steal, while Clint Capela tallied 11 points, 10 rebounds, two steals, one block and one assist. The Rockets shot 47 percent, including 13 - of - 37 from long range. Meanwhile, C.J. McCollum led the way for the Trail Blazers with 16 points, five rebounds, four assists and one block, in 32 minutes. Jusuf Nurkic tallied 14 points, 11 rebounds, one assist and one steal, while Evan Turner added 10 points, three rebounds and three assists. The Trail Blazers shot 46 percent from the field, including 7 - of - 22 from long range. The Trail Blazers (48 - 31) will look to bounce back as they take on the Spurs in San Antonio on Saturday. The Rockets (64 - 15) will be looking for their third straight win as they play host to the Thunder on Saturday.
