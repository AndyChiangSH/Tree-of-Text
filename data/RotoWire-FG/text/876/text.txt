The Brooklyn Nets defeated the host Detroit Pistons, 101 - 100, at Little Caesars Arena on Sunday evening. The Nets got out to a great start on the road and led 30 - 20 after the first quarter. The Pistons rebounded in the second quarter and tightened the gap, but still trailed 53 - 48 at halftime. The Nets kept up the pressure in the third quarter and quickly stretched their lead to double digits. They held on to take a nine - point lead into the fourth. The Pistons made a strong push in the fourth and were able to tie things up at 94, with 2:16 left in the game. It looked like Detroit would pull off the comeback, as Andre Drummond hit a go - ahead bucket, with 5 seconds left. However, after a timeout, Spencer Dinwiddie maneuvered his way through the lane and hit the game - winner for Brooklyn. Dinwiddie led the Nets with 22 points on 9 - of - 13 shooting, in 30 minutes of action. Rondae Hollis-Jefferson followed up with 21 points, eight rebounds, seven assists and a block, in 30 minutes, while DeMarre Carroll added 16 points, five rebounds, three assists and one steal, in 34 minutes. The Nets shot 46 percent from the field, including 13 - of - 31 from long range. Meanwhile, Tobias Harris was the high - point man for the Pistons with 20 points on 8 - of - 18 shooting, in 35 minutes. Ish Smith tallied 11 points, three assists and two rebounds, while Andre Drummond finished with seven points, 13 rebounds, four assists and one block. Stanley Johnson played well off the bench, with 18 points and four rebounds, in 26 minutes. The Pistons shot 42 percent from the field, including 9 - of - 29 from long range. The Pistons (22 - 23) will look to bounce back as they play host to the Jazz on Wednesday. The Nets (18 - 29) take on the Thunder in OKC on Tuesday.
