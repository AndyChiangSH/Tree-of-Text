The Sacramento Kings defeated the host Dallas Mavericks, 114 - 109, at American Airlines Center on Tuesday. Despite the fact that Dallas attainted the first lead of the game, that would mark their only lead in this matchup as Sacramento controlled from there. In fact, Sacramento led by at least five points from the end of the first quarter all the way to the final whistle. The Kings actually led by 23 points at the half, but Dallas got it close at the end thanks to a 38 - 26 fourth quarter. The shooting disparity was vast, with Sacramento connecting on 54 percent of their shots and Dallas hitting just 46 percent from the field. The rest of the statistics were eerily similar, with the rebounding, assisting, blocks and turnover differentials all within one. The Kings (18 - 38) were led by Zach Randolph, as he tallied 22 points, seven rebounds and three assists on 10 - of - 15 shooting. Bogdan Bogdanovic accrued 19 points, five rebounds and four assists. Justin Jackson totaled 12 points, six rebounds and three assists while De'Aaron Fox finished with 11 points, four rebounds and seven assists. The Mavericks (18 - 40) got a gem from J.J. Barea in his return from injury, as he led the team with 19 points, five rebounds and 13 assists. Harrison Barnes accumulated 18 points, five rebounds and two assists. Dennis Smith Jr. provided 17 points, three rebounds and three assists while Dwight Powell led the bench with 18 points, six rebounds and five assists. Sacramento will have to play again on Wednesday, as they travel to play the Houston Rockets. Dallas is now on their all - star break, as they won't return to action until February 23rd in a road matchup versus the Los Angeles Lakers.
