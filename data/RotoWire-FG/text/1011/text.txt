The Charlotte Hornets defeated the visiting Milwaukee Bucks, 110 - 107, at Spectrum Center on Monday. This was a true tale of two halves, as there was only one lead change in this game. In fact, Milwaukee held the lead for the first 20 minutes while Charlotte held the lead for the entirety of the second half. The Hornets actually led by 25 at times in the second half, as Milwaukee rode a 27 - 17 fourth quarter to make the game close. A 33 - 17 second quarter ultimately decided the game for Charlotte though, as that was too much for Milwaukee to overcome. The shooting disparity really decided things, with the Hornets shooting 47 percent from the field and 45 percent from three - point range while holding the Bucks to 41 percent from the field and 35 percent from long range. The Bucks (14 - 6) were led by Giannis Antetokounmpo, as he tallied 20 points, 13 rebounds, nine assists and three blocks. Eric Bledsoe finished with 17 points, four assists and two steals. Khris Middleton accrued 14 points, nine rebounds, two assists and two steals. Malcolm Brogdon totaled 11 points, eight rebounds and six assists while Pat Connaughton led the bench with 15 points and five boards. The Hornets (10 - 10) were led by Kemba Walker, as he tallied 21 points, six rebounds, five assists and two steals. Jeremy Lamb matched those 21 points, adding eight rebounds, four assists and two steals to his tally. Marvin Williams totaled 16 points and eight boards. Tony Parker led the bench with 15 points and six assists. Both teams return to action on Wednesday, with Charlotte playing host to the Atlanta Hawks and Milwaukee welcoming the Chicago Bulls to Fiserv Forum.
