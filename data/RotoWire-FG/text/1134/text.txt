The Portland Trail Blazers took down the host Oklahoma City Thunder, 111 - 98, at Chesapeake Energy Arena on Sunday evening. Portland now leads the series, 3 - 1. It was a similar story to Game 2, as the Trail Blazers (3 - 1) relied heavily on their star - studded backcourt to get the job done. The Trail Blazers held a healthy double - digit lead for much of the third quarter before OKC (1 - 3) cut the deficit to just nine points. C.J. McCollum quickly led the Blazers on an 80 run, capping it off with a long three - pointer. The Thunder cut the lead to as little as seven with just over a minute remaining, but Portland was able to continue its strong offensive showing and close out the game. Superstar point guard Russell Westbrook struggled once again, shooting just 5 - of - 21 for 14 points. Westbrook recorded only one point in the second half while being outdueled by Damian Lillard, who poured in 15 third - quarter points. McCollum led Portland with 27 points, including five three - pointers. In total, the Trail Blazers knocked down 15 three - pointers in the win. For OKC, Paul George led the way with 32 points and 10 rebounds. It was not enough to get the job done, though, as the Thunder shot a collective 37 percent from the field in Sunday's loss. Steven Adams had a rough night, finishing with just six points and seven rebounds. Portland will host Game 5 on Tuesday.
