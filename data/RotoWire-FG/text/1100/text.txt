The Boston Celtics defeated the visiting Washington Wizards, 107 - 96, at TD Garden on Friday. Boston desperately needed a win here and a double - digit victory shows just how important it was. They did all their damage in the first and fourth quarters, outscoring Washington, 55 - 35. That allowed the Celtics to lead for 45 minutes of this 48 - minute game. The Celtics defense was critical, as they held the Wizards to 40 percent from the field and 26 percent from three - point range while recording 12 steals. The assist - to - turnover ratio was huge as well, with Boston recording six more assists than Washington while committing six fewer turnovers. The Wizards (25 - 37) were led by Bradley Beal, as he tallied 29 points, 11 rebounds, six assists and two steals. Jeff Green totaled 15 points and four boards. Tomas Satoransky accrued 15 points, five rebounds and five assists while Bobby Portis amassed seven points and 11 boards. Jabari Parker led the bench with 12 points, five rebounds and four assists. The Celtics (38 - 25) were led by Al Horford, as he finished with 18 points, six rebounds and three assists. Kyrie Irving tallied 13 points, six rebounds and 12 assists. Marcus Morris generated 17 points and nine boards while Jayson Tatum accumulated 10 points, eight rebounds, three assists and five steals. Both teams return to action on Sunday, with Washington playing host to the Minnesota Timberwolves and Boston welcoming the Houston Rockets to TD Garden.
