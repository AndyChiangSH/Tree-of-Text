The Denver Nuggets defeated the visiting Boston Celtics, 115 - 107, at Pepsi Center on Monday evening. The Celtics got out to a great start on the road, with a quick 10 - 2 run, before going on to build a 34 - 19 lead by the end of the quarter. The Nuggets answered in the second, as they kicked things off with a 90 run, just minutes into the quarter, to cut the deficit to six. The Celtics managed to push their lead back to 10, but the Nuggets did not back down, as they outscored the Celtics, 19 - 11, through the rest of the quarter, to trail by just two at halftime. Both teams came out strong in the third, as they went back - and - forth to keep the score within one or two possessions, however, the Nuggets took over in the final minutes, where they outscored the Celtics 11 - 4, to take a seven - point lead into the fourth. The Nuggets did not look back from there, as they managed to stay one step ahead throughout the fourth, to earn their fifth straight win, and move to 60 at home. Jamal Murray went off for a career - high 48 points on 19 - of - 30 shooting, in 38 minutes, to lead the Nuggets. Gary Harris and Trey Lyles chipped in 13 points each, while Nikola Jokic tallied eight points, 10 rebounds and eight assists. The Nuggets shot 46 percent from the field, including 13 - of - 34 from long range. Meanwhile, Kyrie Irving led the Celtics with 31 points on 13 - of - 17 shooting, five rebounds, five assists, three steals and one block, in 33 minutes. Jayson Tatum and Jaylen Brown tallied 15 points each, while Al Horford finished with 12 points, six assists, four rebounds and one steal, in 32 minutes. The Celtics shot 51 percent from the field, including 9 - of - 31 from deep. The Celtics (6 - 4) will look to avoid a third straight loss, as they take on the Suns in Phoenix on Thursday. The Nuggets (9 - 1) take on the Grizzlies in Memphis on Wednesday.
