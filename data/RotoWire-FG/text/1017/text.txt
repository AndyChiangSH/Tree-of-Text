The Washington Wizards defeated the Atlanta Hawks, 131 - 117, at State Farm Arena on Wednesday evening. The Wizards (11 - 14) came into the contest seemingly building some momentum after a forgettable start to their season. However, their Wednesday fortunes appeared somewhat in peril earlier in the day, when it was announced that star point guard John Wall would miss the game for personal reasons. The rest of the roster helped ensure that his absence would be made up for, however, starting with Wall's backcourt mate Bradley Beal. The sharpshooting two - guard racked up a game - high 36 points and supplemented them with nine assists, six rebounds and one steal. Washington sent the tone with a 72 - point first half, which allowed them to withstand a 68 - point surge by the Hawks (5 - 20) over the final 24 minutes. Otto Porter, Jr. followed Beal with a 20 - point, 11 - rebound double - double that also included five assists and one steal. Kelly Oubre, Jr. managed a bench - leading 19 points while adding five rebounds and three steals. Thomas Bryant posted 16 points, nine rebounds, one assist, one steal and one block. Tomas Satoransky chipped in 14 points, seven assists, four rebounds, two steals and one block. Markieff Morris poured in 12 points off the bench. John Collins generated a 26 - point, 14 - rebound double - double to lead the Hawks. Kent Bazemore drew the start at small forward and supplied 22 points, six assists, two rebounds, four steals and one block. Trae Young registered 14 points, five assists, two rebounds and two steals. Vince Carter led the bench with 13 points. Dewayne Dedmon totaled 10 points, eight rebounds, one assist and two steals. The Wizards are off until Saturday night, when they'll hit the road and tangle with the Cleveland Cavaliers. The Hawks remain home and next take the court against the Denver Nuggets on Saturday evening as well.
