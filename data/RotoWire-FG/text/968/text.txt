The visiting Golden State Warriors defeated the Houston Rockets, 119 - 106, in Game 1 of the Western Conference Finals at Toyota Center on Monday night. Golden State took a 10 series lead with the win. In characteristic fashion, the Warriors (10) did the bulk of their damage in the third quarter, going on a 14 - 4 run in the middle of the third to take control of the game. From there, Houston (0 - 1) never came closer than a two - possession deficit, and the Warriors were able to put the game out of reach in the final minutes thanks to a hot fourth quarter from All-Star shooting guard Klay Thompson. Thompson had 11 fourth - quarter points, including three made three - pointers. Golden State's Kevin Durant was able to get the best of his former Oklahoma City Thunder teammate, James Harden, as both superstars delivered impeccable performances. Harden led all scorers with 41 points, shooting 14 - of - 24 and reaching the free - throw line 10 times. Durant, though, led his team to victory while scoring 37 points of his own. The two traded baskets for the majority of the game, but Durant was aided by 28 points from All-Star teammate Klay Thompson. Two-time MVP Steph Curry added another 18 points and dished out eight assists along the way. For the Rockets, James Harden and Chris Paul led the way. In addition to his significant scoring output, Harden dished a team - high seven assists. Paul, meanwhile, scored 23 points and grabbed 11 rebounds. No other Rocket scored more than 15 points, though, and Houston saw only 21 combined points from its bench unit in the loss. Houston will host Game 2 on Wednesday evening.
