The Memphis Grizzlies defeated the Phoenix Suns, 117 - 96, at FedEx Forum on Saturday night. The Grizzlies (3 - 2) had some solid momentum coming into Saturday's game, and they finished the contest even hotter than when they started. Memphis jumped on the Suns (1 - 4) early by building a 26 - 18 first - quarter lead, and they extended their advantage to 25 points by halftime. That essentially decided the contest, and the home squad closed things out in decisive fashion with a 30 - point fourth quarter. Both teams shot well overall, but the Grizzlies managed to coax 19 turnovers from the visitors while only committing 12 themselves. Deandre Ayton paced the Suns with 24 points, a total he supplemented with eight rebounds, five assists and one steal. Elie Okobo followed with 12 points and two assists. Isaiah Canaan and Jamal Crawford generated matching 11 - point tallies from the first unit and bench, respectively. Josh Jackson drew the start for Devin Booker (hamstring) and turned in eight points, three rebounds, three assists and three steals. Marc Gasol led the Grizzlies with 19 points and added eight rebounds, three assists and three steals. Mike Conley registered 18 points, seven assists and two steals. Garrett Temple managed 15 points, three rebounds, one assist and one steal. MarShon Brooks led the bench with 18 points, while Wayne Selden contributed 14. The Suns remain on the road and battle the Oklahoma City Thunder on Sunday evening, while the Grizzlies are home again versus the Washington Wizards on Tuesday evening.
