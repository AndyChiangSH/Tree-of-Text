The Indiana Pacers defeated the visiting Cleveland Cavaliers, 106 - 102, at BankersLife Fieldhouse on Friday evening. The Pacers (15 - 11) came from behind and secured a win no one expected them to clinch. The Cavaliers (18 - 8) were riding a franchise - record 13 - game winning streak, but Indiana ended their streak with a strong performance in the second half. The Cavs had managed to build a 63 - 57 advantage at the half, but the Pacers came from behind and used a stellar third - quarter to gain a three - point lead heading into the final 12 minutes. Cleveland tried hard to recover the lead, but Indiana made some clutch buckets towards the end of the game, and sealed a huge win in front of their own crowd. The biggest star for the Pacers in this game was Victor Oladipo, as the shooting guard once again emerged as a huge scoring threat when the team needed him the most. Oladipo finished the game with 33 points, seven rebounds and five assists, and hit a clutch three - pointer with 1:1 left in the fourth that sealed the deal for Indiana. Myles Turner added 15 points, and Thaddeus Young contributed with 13 points and nine boards. LeBron James led the way for the Cavs, as he came close to a triple - double thanks to his 29 points, 10 rebounds and nine assists. Kevin Love added 20 points with seven rebounds, while J.R. Smith contributed with 15 points. But the Cavs' bad shooting doomed them in this game, as they only shot 31 percent from three - point range.
