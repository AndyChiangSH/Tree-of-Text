The Boston Celtics defeated the Washington Wizards, 116 - 110, at Capital One Arena on Tuesday. The Celtics (49 - 33) and the Wizards (32 - 50) both played their regular - season finale although both clubs had different objectives in mind - - while the Celtics had already clinched a playoff berth and are vying for home - field advantage in the first round of the postseason, Washington has been eliminated from quite a while and only wanted this season to come to an end. But despite the difference on both team's approach, this turned out to be a very competitive contest. The Wizards managed to jump to a 14 - point lead in the first quarter (39 - 25) but Boston responded with a 38 - 25 second quarter so the Wizards only had a one - point lead at the break. It seemed Washington would break free once again following a 31 - 26 third quarter, but Boston won the final 12 minutes by an expressive 27 - 15 and that notched them the win. The Celtics rested several starters for this game and Terry Rozier was the top scorer since he finished with 21 points, four rebounds and four assists. Semi Ojeleye had 17 points, four boards and three assists. Jaylen Brown contributed with 15 points but also filled the stat sheet with three steals, three rebounds and two assists. Both Brad Wanamaker and R.J. Hunter had 17 points, although Wanamaker also added six boards and seven assists. Tomas Satoransky led the way for the Wizards with 19 points, while Bradley Beal delivered 16 points, five boards and four assists. Thomas Bryant had 14 points with six rebounds, and ended with an impressive 22 in 17 minutes. Bobby Portis had 11 points, while Devin Robinson contributed 14 points, five rebounds and three assists off the bench.
