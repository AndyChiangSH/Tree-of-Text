The Los Angeles Lakers defeated the Dallas Mavericks on overtime, 107 - 101, at American Airlines Center on Saturday afternoon. The Lakers (15 - 27) have been one of the hottest teams in the NBA of late, and they delivered another impressive outing as they went out to defeat the Mavericks (15 - 29) on the road. It was a back - and - forth game during the first half, however, as the Lakers jumped to a 31 - 20 lead in the first quarter, only to see Dallas storming back and creating a six - point advantage (52 - 46) at the break. The Lakers won the remaining two quarters by three points each, so the game needed five extra minutes to decide what would have happened. And Los Angeles won the OT period 12 - 6, pushing their winning streak up to four games. Meanwhile, Dallas has lost four of their last six. Julius Randle was the biggest star for the Lakers, as the power forward delivered a strong double - double of 23 points and 15 rebounds. Lonzo Ball struggled from the field going 4 - for - 13, but he filled out the stat sheet thanks to nine points, seven rebounds, and seven assists. Kentavious Caldwell-Pope had 17 points. Kyle Kuzma (18 points, 10 rebounds) and Jordan Clarkson (19 points) starred off the bench as well. Yogi Ferrell led the Mavericks offense, finishing with 11 points, 10 rebounds, and four assists. The highest scorer was Dennis Smith Jr., however, as the rookie point guard chipped in with 23 points. Harrison Barnes and Wesley Matthews had 17 points each, and Dwight Powell left his mark off the bench after contributing with 13 points and four boards.
