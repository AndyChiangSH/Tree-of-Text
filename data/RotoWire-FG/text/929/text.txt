The Oklahoma City Thunder defeated the Toronto Raptors, 132 - 125, at Air Canada Centre on Sunday. The Thunder (43 - 29) and the Raptors (52 - 18) played one of the most intense, thrilling games in the NBA in recent weeks. In the end, the visitors came out on top thanks to a huge comeback in the fourth quarter that propelled them to victory. OKC actually jumped in front after the first quarter (40 - 34), but the Raptors won the subsequent two quarters (30 - 26 and 34 - 31) to make it a one - point game heading into the third quarter, as Toronto was up 98 - 97. But the Thunder came from behind and after winning the final quarter 35 - 27, they secured a much - needed win that should boost their confidence for the final part of the season. Russell Westbrook was the best player for the Thunder, as he finished with 37 points, 14 assists and 13 rebounds to notch his fifth consecutive triple - double. Steven Adams contributed 25 points, eight rebounds and four assists, while Paul George added 22 points, five boards, three assists and three steals. Carmelo Anthony had 15 points, and Corey Brewer finished with 10. DeMar DeRozan led the way for the Raptors with a 24 - point performance, but it wasn't enough. Kyle Lowry delivered a double - double of 22 points and 10 assists, while Jonas Valanciunas had 10 points. Delon Wright chipped in with 15 points and eight assists coming off the bench, while substitutes C.J. Miles (15 points) and Pascal Siakam (10) also scored in double figures.
