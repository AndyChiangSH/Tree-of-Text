The Minnesota Timberwolves defeated the host Cleveland Cavaliers, 102 - 95, at Quicken Loans Arena on Monday evening. Both teams got out to a good start, as they went back and forth through the first quarter, before the Timberwolves emerged with a, 29 - 26, lead heading into the second. The Timberwolves made the most of their momentum, as they pushed to a 10 - point lead early in the quarter. The managed to hold the lead for the entirety of the quarter, and take a, 54 - 47, lead into halftime. The Timberwolves kept it up through the third quarter, but the Cavaliers did not go away, as they cut the deficit to just four, after outscoring the visitors, 24 - 21, in the quarter. However, the T-Wolves would not be had that easily, as they once again pushed their lead back to double digits early in the fourth, and held on down the stretch, to earn their third straight win. Robert Covington led Minnesota with 24 points, seven rebounds, two steals, two blocks and one assist, in 38 minutes. Karl-Anthony Towns tallied 21 points, 10 rebounds, one assist and one block, while Jeff Teague added 13 points, six assists, three blocks, two rebounds and one steal. The Timberwolves shot 46 percent from the field, including 8 - of - 26 from long range. Meanwhile, Kyle Korver led the Cavs with 22 points on 7 - of - 12 shooting, in 21 minutes off the bench. Rodney Hood followed up with 20 points, three assists, two rebounds and one steal, while Tristan Thompson finished with 16 points, 11 rebounds, three assists, one steal and one block. The Cavs (4 - 15) will look to bounce back as they take on the Thunder in OKC on Wednesday. The Timberwolves (10 - 11) play host to the Spurs on Wednesday.
