The Indiana Pacers defeated the Toronto Raptors, 108 - 90, at Bankers Life Fieldhouse on Tuesday. Indiana needed this game to keep playoff hopes alive and they rode a furious comeback to increase their odds. In fact, after trailing by 11 at the half, the Pacers outscored the Raptors, 68 - 39, in the second half to comeback and win by 18. The big advantage for Indiana was their shooting, as they dominated the three - point shooting, while going a perfect 18 - of - 18 from the free - throw line. The Pacers ended up shooting 12 - of - 25 from long distance, while Toronto went just 5 - of - 23. The Raptors (47 - 31) are now tied with the Washington Wizards for the three - seed in the East, as this loss could cause complications for playoff positioning. DeMar DeRozan led the way for Toronto, as he tallied 27 points, three rebounds, two assists and three steals. Jonas Valanciunas recorded a double - double, totaling 10 points and 10 rebounds. Serge Ibaka went just 3 - of - 13 from the field to finish with eight points and 10 rebounds. Toronto's next game will be on Wednesday in a road matchup versus the Detroit Pistons. The Pacers (38 - 40) desperately needed this win with their playoff hopes slimming and this win showed some serious resiliency. Paul George scored a game - high 35 points to add to his 10 rebounds, three assists and two steals. Thaddeus Young accrued 15 points, 11 rebounds and two steals. Jeff Teague collected 20 points and six assists. Lance Stephenson came off the bench to provide 12 points, two rebounds and three assists in his return to Indy. On deck for the Pacers is a home matchup against the Milwaukee Bucks on Thursday.
