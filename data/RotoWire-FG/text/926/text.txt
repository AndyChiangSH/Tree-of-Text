The Minnesota Timberwolves defeated the Washington Wizards, 116 - 111, at Capital One Arena on Tuesday. The Timberwolves (40 - 29) looked defeated at one point, but they managed to pull an impressive, out - of - nowhere comeback to secure a huge win against a Wizards (38 - 30) team that has been one of the best in the Eastern Conference all season long. Washington had the advantage in the first half after putting a 59 - 53 following the first 24 minutes of action, and they even extended it to an eight - point advantage after three. All signs pointed to a Washington win, but Minnesota won the final 12 minutes with a 34 - 21 score and that allowed them to escape with a massive win on the road. Karl-Anthony Towns was the best player for Minnesota, as he finished with a 37 - point, 10 - rebound, three - assist and two - block performance, filling out the stat sheet and making an impact on both ends of the pitch. Nenad Bjelica had 17 points, eight rebounds and seven assists. Andrew Wiggins had 16 points, while Jeff Teague delivered 13 points and five assists. Jamal Crawford had 11 points off the bench. Markieff Morris was the top scorer for the Wizards since he finished with 27 points. Bradley Beal only had 19 points, but he also added five assists and four rebounds. Tomas Satoransky had 15 points, eight rebounds and seven assists. Ian Mahinmi had an impressive performance since he finished with 10 points and nine rebounds in 23 minutes coming off the bench.
