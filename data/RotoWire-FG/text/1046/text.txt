The Denver Nuggets defeated the host Sacramento Kings, 117 - 113, at Golden One Center on Thursday. This was a true tale of two halves, as Denver role a big second half to victory. In fact, Sacrament blew a 13 - point halftime lead, as the Nuggets tied the game with a 31 - 18 third quarter. The Nuggets then held the lead for the entirety of the fourth quarter, as they made their final five shots of the game. Rebounding was the difference for Denver, as they outrebounded Sacramento, 54 - 33. Getting to the free - throw line was also critical, with the Nuggets hitting 21 - of - 30 and the Kings connecting on just 10 - of - 15 from the charity stripe. This win gives Denver their best start in franchise history, as they maintain the top - seed in the Western Conference. The Nuggets (25 - 11) were led by Jamal Murray, as he scored 34 of his 36 points in the second half, adding seven rebounds and six assists to his impressive line. Nikola Jokic was just as valuable, collecting 26 points, 13 rebounds and six assists. Mason Plumlee accrued 12 points, 10 rebounds and four assists while Gary Harris led the bench with 14 points and three boards. The Kings (19 - 19) were led by Buddy Hield, as he tallied 29 points, two rebounds and two assists. Nemanja Bjelica amassed 17 points, six rebounds and three assists. De'Aaron Fox finished with 16 points, two rebounds and eight assists while Bogdan Bogdanovic led the bench with 14 points, five rebounds and two assists. Both teams return to action on Saturday, with Denver playing host to the shorthanded Charlotte Hornets and Sacramento welcoming the Golden State Warriors to Sac Town.
