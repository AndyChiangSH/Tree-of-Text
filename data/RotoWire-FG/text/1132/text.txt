The Utah Jazz defeated the visiting Denver Nuggets, 118 - 108, at Vivint Smart Home Arena on Tuesday. Utah (50 - 31) secured its 50th win of the season and its 13th in its last 15 games with Tuesday's comfortable victory. Utah shot 53 percent from the field in its final regular season home game of the season. The strong offensive showing gave the Jazz a lead for the final 18 minutes of action, making Tuesday's win a relatively safe one for the playoff - bound team. Denver (53 - 28) was short - staffed for most of the game after big man Nikola Jokic fouled out early on in the going. Jokic had two points on 1 - of - 6 shooting before his early exit. With their loss, the Nuggets are now tied for second in the Western Conference with the Houston Rockets. The Jazz were led by a season - high 46 points from sophomore guard Donovan Mitchell. Mitchell shot 14 - of - 26 from the field to lead Utah's scalding - hot offensive attack. Star big man Rudy Gobert finished with 20 points and 10 rebounds, while Joe Ingles recorded another double - double with 10 points and 13 assists. Denver, meanwhile, saw 64 points from its bench unit in the tough loss. Malik Beasley recorded a team - high 25 points off the bench, while sixth man Monte Morris pitched in another 22. Neither showing was enough for the Nuggets, who struggled at the free - throw line for the second straight game. Denver went just 14 - of - 22 at the stripe on Tuesday. Both teams will close out their regular seasons on Wednesday. Denver will host the Minnesota Timberwolves, while the Jazz will visit the Clippers in LA.
