The Indiana Pacers defeated the visiting Sacramento Kings, 107 - 97, at Banker's Life Fieldhouse on Saturday. This was a tale of four quarters, as Indiana rode the second and fourth quarters to victory. In fact, the Pacers outscored the Kings, 64 - 48, in those 24 minutes. Indiana really finished strong, ending the game on a 23 - 14 run across the final eight minutes. Rebounding was key for the Pacers, as they out - rebounded the Kings, 52 - 43. They also dominated scoring down low, as they outscored the Kings in the paint, 56 - 46. Bench production was also huge, as Indiana had three bench players reach double figures in scoring while Sacramento had just one player reach that threshold. The Kings (13 - 12) were led by Buddy Hield, as he tallied 20 points and four rebounds. De'Aaron Fox accrued 18 points, six assists and two blocks. Willie Cauley-Stein totaled 14 points, nine rebounds, two assists and two blocks. Bogdan Bogdanovic started and finished with 11 points, three rebounds, six assists and three steals while Nemanja Bjelica amassed six points and 12 boards. The Pacers (16 - 10) were led by Thaddeus Young, as he generated 20 points and nine rebounds. Bojan Bogdanovic accumulated 18 points, four rebounds and two assists. Darren Collison finished with 14 points, three rebounds and six assists while Myles Turner provided nine points, 13 rebounds and four blocks. Domantas Sabonis, Cory Joseph and Doug McDermott all reached double figures in scoring off the bench. Both teams return to action on Monday, with Indiana playing host to the Washington Wizards and Sacramento traveling to face the Chicago Bulls.
