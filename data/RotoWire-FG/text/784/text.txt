The visiting Miami Heat defeated the Phoenix Suns, 126 - 115, at Talking Stick Resort Arena on Wednesday night,. The Heat (5 - 6) grabbed a decent lead in the first quarter, and they would never let it go. After jumping to a 69 - 54 following the first half, Phoenix (4 - 8) showed signs of potentially turning things around but every time they got closer, Miami pushed them away, and ultimately came away on top after finishing things in the fourth quarter. The win represented Miami's third victory over their last six games, while the Suns dropped their third straight contest. This means that Phoenix has had at least two three - game losing streaks during the current campaign. The Heat enjoyed a balanced scoring effort this Wednesday, as seven of their eight players that saw the floor contributed with at least 10 points. Goran Dragic led the way with 29 points, nine rebounds and four assists, while Hassan Whiteside added a double - double of 23 points and 10 rebounds. James Johnson produced on both ends of the court coming off the bench (15 points, seven rebounds, five assists and two steals), and both Wayne Ellington (15 points) and Kelly Olynyk (10 points) added double figures ias well. The one player who didn't reach the 10 - point plateau was Josh Richardson, who only got four points. On the other hand, Devin Booker led the way for the Suns with 30 points although he only made nine of his 23 shots. Mike James had 18 points, and T, J. Warren added 16. Jared Dudley had a decent effort off the bench as well, finishing the contest with 13 points and six boards.
