The Toronto Raptors defeated the Philadelphia 76ers, 113 - 105, at Air Canada Center on Sunday. Toronto were expected to win this game easily and they did just that in this eight - point win. In fact, Toronto actually rested many of their starters for the entire fourth quarter, as they led by at least six points for the final 15 minutes. The second quarter is where the Raptors separated themselves, as they outscored the Sixers, 31 - 20. Shooting was the difference in the game, with Toronto shooting 54 percent and Philadelphia hitting just 45 percent of their shots. The Raptors also took care of the interior, outscoring the Sixers, 58 - 42, in the paint. The Sixers (28 - 49) have now lost six of their last eight games, as they continue to limp to the finish line. Timothe Luwawu-Cabbarot led Philly, as he tallied 23 points and three rebounds. T.J. McConnell accrued four points, six rebounds and 11 assists. Dario Saric amassed 16 points and three rebounds. Nik Stauskas was a nice spark off the bench, producing 14 points and four assists. On deck for Philly is a home matchup versus the Brooklyn Nets on Tuesday, The Raptors (47 - 30) are 8 - 1 across their last nine games, with the one - seed in the east now being in reach. Serge Ibaka had one of his best games in a Raptors uniform, collecting 24 points, six rebounds and six blocks on 11 - of - 17 shooting. DeMar DeRozan finished with 17 points and nine assists. Cory Joseph got another start and provided nine points, five rebounds and six assists. Jonas Valanciunas totaled 14 points, eight rebounds and two steals on 7 - of - 9 from the field. Delon Wright had a nice game off the bench, accumulating four points, five assists and four steals. Toronto will look to keep rolling on Tuesday in a road matchup against the Indiana Pacers.
