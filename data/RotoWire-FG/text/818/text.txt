The Golden State Warriors defeated the host Detroit Pistons, 102 - 98, at Little Caesar's Arena on Friday. Despite the fact that there were only three ties in this game, it was a competitive matchup throughout. In fact, there were nine lead changes, as neither team ever led by more than 12 points. In addition, it was a single - digit margin for all but one minute. Defense was key for Golden State, as they held Detroit to just 41 percent from the field. The Warriors actually recorded 15 blocks in their win, as the Pistons swatted away just two shots. Golden Stat's ball movement was pristine as well, outasssiting Detroit, 29 - 20. The Pistons also killed themselves at the free - throw line, hitting just, 10 - of - 19. The Warriors (21 - 6) were led by Kevin Durant, as he collected 36 points, 10 rebounds, seven assists and five blocks. Klay Thompson amassed 21 points, four rebounds and five assists. Draymond Green scored just two points, but still provided six rebounds, 13 assists, three steals and six blocks in an all - around gem. The Pistons (14 - 11) best player was Andre Drummond, as he totaled eight points, 17 rebounds and four assists. Avery Bradley led the team in scoring, accruing 25 points and four rebounds. Reggie Jackson finished with 16 points, three rebounds and two assists. Detroit will return to action on Sunday in a home matchup versus the Boston Celtics. As for Golden State, they play host to the Portland Trail Blazers on Monday for their next fixture.
