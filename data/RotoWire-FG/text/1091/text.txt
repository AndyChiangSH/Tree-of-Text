The Golden State Warriors defeated the Utah Jazz, 115 - 108, at Oracle Arena on Tuesday evening. Utah (32 - 25) seemed ready to test the Warriors' (41 - 15) dominance, holding a lead until late in the fourth quarter. The Jazz's top - five defense shined in the loss, limiting Golden State's high - powered offense to 81 points through three quarters. However, Utah was unable to hold onto the game late, as Golden State poured in 34 fourth - quarter points to earn the victory. Steph Curry, who finished with 24 points, helped to seal the win in the final five minutes, knocking down a pull - up three - pointer and an ensuing layup to put Golden State up 10. Kevin Durant was the game's high scorer with 28 points. Durant also tallied seven assists in the win. Klay Thompson scored another 22 points, while DeMarcus Cousins contributed 12 points, 10 rebounds, and five assists. The Warriors shot over 49 percent from the field and knocked down 13 three - pointers. The Jazz were led by 25 points from sophomore guard Donovan Mitchell, who shot a dismal 10 - of - 29 from the field. Outside of Mitchell, Utah was remarkably efficient. The Jazz finished shooting over 47 percent from the field. Rudy Gobert had a double - double, finishing with 13 points and 16 rebounds. The Jazz will have two days of rest before enjoying the All-Star break, while Golden State will travel to Portland to tangle with the Trail Blazers on Wednesday.
