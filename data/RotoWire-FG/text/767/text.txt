The Toronto Raptors defeated the Philadelphia 76ers, 128 - 94, Air Canada Center on Saturday evening. The Raptors (20) controlled this one from the start, bursting out to a 36 - 19 lead after the first quarter. Philly (0 - 3) rebounded slightly in the second quarter, before the Raptors poured in 40 points in the third quarter to put the game out of reach. DeMar DeRozan and Kyle Lowry did not hit the floor in the fourth quarter. However, the Raptors experienced a bit of a downer as Jonas Valanciunas left the game in the second quarter with a sprained ankle. DeRozan led the way for Toronto with 30 points on 8 - of - 12 shooting, along with three rebounds and three assists, in only 26 minutes. Serge Ibaka was on fire from long range, hitting 5 - of - 9 from deep, on his way 21 points, five rebounds, two assists and two blocks, in 24 minutes. Kyle Lowry finished with 17 points, five rebounds and five assists. Meanwhile, the Sixers were with out Joel Embiid, who sat out the second night of their back - to - back. Ben Simmons was impressive once again, nearing a triple - double with 18 points, 10 rebounds and eight assists. Jarryd Bayless followed up with 16 points, while Justin Anderson added 12 points. The Sixers gave up 20 turnovers in the contest and hit only 22 - of - 36 free throws. The Sixers look ahead to Monday, when they play in Detroit. The Raptors will begin a road trip with a contest in San Antonio on Monday.
