The San Antonio Spurs defeated the New York Knicks, 100 - 91, at Madison Square Garden on Tuesday evening. The Spurs (26 - 12) had to come from behind after losing the first quarter to the Knicks (18 - 19) by a 29 - 25 score, but eventually settled and delivered another strong performance in what was a record - setting night for Gregg Popovich. The long - time head coach registered his 1,176 career win, passing George Karl for fifth most in the history of the NBA. Despite the fact the Spurs had to rally for this win, they looked absolutely in control as soon as the second quarter started. The biggest difference here was the third quarter, since the 29 - 18 lead from the Spurs over that period was a differential the Knicks could mount back. LaMarcus Aldridge and Kawhi Leonard carried with the Spurs' offense in this game. Aldridge finished with 29 points and five rebounds, while Leonard filled the stat sheet and proved to be quite a weapon on both ends of the court after contributing with 25 points, eight boards, four assists, and four steals. Pau Gasol chipped in with 11 points and eight rebounds, while Manu Ginobili added 12 points off the bench. Michael Beasley led the way for the Knicks after registering 18 points and nine rebounds coming off the bench. Kristaps Porzingis contributed with 13 points, the same amount as Courtney Lee and Lance Thomas, although the Latvian star also added nine boards. Enes Kanter had 12 points and Jarrett Jack added 10, meaning all five starters for New York scored in double digits.
