The Los Angeles Clippers defeated the host Boston Celtics, 129 - 119, at TD Garden on Wednesday. Eight ties and 16 lead changes make this game look much closer than it actually was, as the Clippers held the lead for over 40 minutes. The first and fourth quarters were key for Los Angeles, outscoring Boston, 70 - 53. The shooting disparity was a big factor as well, with the Clippers shooting 50 percent while holding Boston to just 43 percent from the field. Much of that was done down low, with Los Angles outscoring Boston in the paint, 56 - 36 The Clippers (30 - 26) were led by DeAndre Jordan, as he collected 30 points, 13 rebounds, three assists and four steals on 11 - of - 14 from the field. Tobias Harris accrued 21 points, eight rebounds and five assists. Danilo Gallinari amassed 20 points and nine rebounds while Lou Williams led the bench with 19 points, two rebounds and six assists. The Celtics (40 - 20) got a gem out of Kyrie Irving, as he totaled 33 points and eight assists. Al Horford finished with 20 points, four rebounds and two blocks. Marcus Morris led the bench with 13 points, 11 rebounds and three assists. The Clippers are off until the 22nd, when they travel to play the Golden State Warriors. As for Boston, they're off until the 23rd, when they travel to play the Detroit Pistons.
