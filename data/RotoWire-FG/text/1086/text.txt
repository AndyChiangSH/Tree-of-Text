The Toronto Raptors defeated the host New York Knicks, 104 - 99, at Madison Square Garden on Saturday. While Toronto came into this game as a sizable favorite, New York made it closer than most expected. In fact, there were nine ties and eight lead changes in total, as is stayed within a seven - point margin for the final 15 minutes of action. Free-throw shooting was the difference in the game, with the Raptors connecting on 24 - of - 29 while the Knicks hit just 11 - of - 19. Ball movement was also key, with Toronto recording 27 assists and New York handing out 22. The Raptors (41 - 16) were led by Kyle Lowry, as he tallied 22 points, four rebounds, five assists, two steals and two blocks. Serge Ibaka totaled 15 points, 13 rebounds and four assists. Kawhi Leonard finished with 11 points, five rebounds and six assists. Marc Gasol made his debut and provided seven points and six rebounds off the bench. The Knicks (10 - 45) lost their 16th - straight game here, as Kevin Knox led the way with 20 points, four rebounds, three assists and two steals. DeAndre Jordan accumulated 10 points, 18 rebounds and three assists. Dennis Smith Jr. collected 13 points, four rebounds, six assists and three steals. Kadeem Allen led the bench with 14 points, four rebounds and six assists. Both teams return to action on Monday, with New York traveling to face the struggling Cleveland Cavaliers and Toronto playing host to the Brooklyn Nets.
