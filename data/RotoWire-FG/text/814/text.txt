The Dallas Mavericks defeated the Denver Nuggets, 122 - 105, at American Airlines Center on Monday. Dallas have struggled all season, but a wire - to - wire win here may be what they need to get going. In fact, they led by at least eight points for every second after the first six minutes. Shooting was key, with the Mavericks hitting 57 percent of their shots, while holding the Nuggets to under 42 percent. Scoring down low was huge as well, as Dallas outscored Denver, 64 - 42, in the paint. The Nuggets (13 - 10) had to play this game without Nikola Jokic and Paul Millsap, as they simply couldn't match up with his hungry Dallas team. Will Barton led the team, as he provided 23 points, eight rebounds and six assists off the bench. Jamal Murray led the starters with 22 points, despite not recording a rebound or an assist. Gary Harris accrued 21 points, three rebounds, four assists and three steals. Kenneth Faried amassed 13 points and 11 rebounds. The Mavericks (7 - 17) were led by Harrison Barnes, as he amassed 22 points, 10 rebounds and three assists. Dennis Smith Jr. collected 20 points, five rebounds and three assists. Devin Harris, Dwight Powell and Yogi Ferrell all reached double figures in scoring off the bench. Both teams return to action on Wednesday, as Dallas travel to play the Boston Celtics, while Denver travels to face the New Orleans Pelicans.
