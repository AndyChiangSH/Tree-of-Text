The visiting Los Angeles Clippers took down the Golden State Warriors, 125 - 106, at Oracle Arena on Wednesday night. The win was the Clippers' first against Golden State since December 25, 2014. With All-Star guards Steph Curry (ankle) and Klay Thompson (rest) sitting out for the Warriors (33 - 9), the game looked like a prime opportunity for superstar forward Kevin Durant to take over on offense. Durant, who had missed the last four games with a calf strain, came out strong, scoring 25 first half points. Despite his early efforts, Golden State was able to carry only a four - point lead into halftime. Durant's early dominance was overshadowed by a miraculous second - half outing from Clippers guard Lou Williams. Williams scored 27 points in the third quarter alone, finishing with a career high of 50 points in the commanding win. He shot 16 - of - 27 from the field and 10 - of - 10 from the free - throw line, hit eight three - pointers, and dished out seven assists. Williams helped the Clippers (19 - 21) earn a double - digit lead early in the fourth quarter, which the team built up to 21 points by the middle of the fourth. For the Warriors, Durant was the only player to score more than 12 points. He finished with 40 points on 14 - of - 18 shooting and scored his 20,0th career point in the blowout loss. Fellow All-Star Draymond Green shot just 1 - of - 9 from the field and had only seven points. The Clippers, meanwhile, were without All-Star forward Blake Griffin (concussion), but found offense in Williams and others on Wednesday. Bench guard Tyrone Wallace shot 7 - of - 11 to add 22 points off the bench in the win. DeAndre Jordan, meanwhile, had 12 rebounds to lead the team, including three on the offensive end. Both teams will hit the road, as the Clippers visit Sacramento on Thursday while the Warriors are slated to play in Milwaukee on Friday.
