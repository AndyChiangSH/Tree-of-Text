In a game that may go down as a modern classic, the Cleveland Cavaliers defeated the Minnesota Timberwolves, 140 - 138, in overtime on Wednesday. The game featured 34 lead changes and a late 80 run to help Minnesota regain the lead. The two teams shot the lights out from three - point range, setting a new NBA record by combining for 40 made three - pointers. In overtime, the team traded buckets back and forth, with Jimmy Butler doing most of Minnesota's damage and LeBron James leading the charge for Cleveland. With 24 seconds left and the game tied, Minnesota had the ball and was able to run an isolation for Butler, but Butler was rejected by James at the rim. Cleveland's J.R. Smith recovered the ball and called a quick time - out with one second left on the clock. The Cavaliers managed to inbound the ball from the backcourt up to James in the paint, who knocked down a turnaround jumper at the buzzer to give Cleveland a much - needed win. For the Cavaliers (31 - 22), it was all about LeBron James. James shot 16 - of - 22 from the field en route to a team - high 37 points. He had a triple - double as well, notching 16 assists and 10 rebounds. The Cavs also saw a rare strong game from veteran starter J.R. Smith, who has seen his production fall off a cliff this season. Despite his poor season to this point, Smith scored 20 points on 7 - of - 14 shooting in Wednesday's win. Minnesota (35 - 22), on the other hand, balanced its attack between its two All-Stars. Butler was the team's leading scorer with 35 points on 14 - of - 21 shooting. Seven of those points came in the overtime period. Karl-Anthony Towns, meanwhile, had a ridiculously efficient 10 - of - 12 shooting night. Towns scored 30 points and went a perfect 6 - of - 6 from three - point range. Towns has now made his last nine straight three - point shots, tying a Timberwolves franchise record. The Cavaliers will visit Atlanta on Friday, while the Timberwolves will travel to Chicago.
