The Utah Jazz defeated the visiting Atlanta Hawks, 128 - 112, at Vivint Smart Home Arena on Friday evening. The contest got out to a hot start, as the Jazz outscored the Hawks, 36 - 34, in the first quarter. The Hawks picked it up in the second and pulled ahead, as they outscored the Jazz by four in the quarter, to take a two - point lead by halftime. However, the Jazz came out on fire in the third quarter, where they outscored the visitors, 38 - 27, to take a nine - point lead into the final frame. The Jazz held strong through the fourth and earned their 30th win of the season. Rudy Gobert led the Jazz with 25 points, 13 rebounds, two blocks and one assist, in 27 minutes. Ricky Rubio followed up with 22 points, 11 rebounds, two rebounds and one steal, while Joe Ingles tallied 19 points, seven rebounds, five assists and one block. The Jazz shot 53 percent from the field, including 12 - of - 35 from long range. Meanwhile, Trae Young led the Hawks with 28 points on 9 - of - 20 shooting, nine assists, three rebounds and one steal, in 37 minutes. John Collins followed up with 19 points, four rebounds, one assist, one steal and one block, while Dewayne Dedmon chipped in 15 points, seven rebounds, three steals, two assists and one block. The Hawks shot 40 percent from the field, including 14 - of - 46 from deep. The Hawks (16 - 35) will look to bounce back as they take on the Suns in Phoenix on Saturday. The Jazz (30 - 23) play host to the Rockets on Saturday.
