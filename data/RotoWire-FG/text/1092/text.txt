The Denver Nuggets defeated the Sacramento Kings, 120 - 118, in a last - second thriller at Pepsi Center on Wednesday. All eyes were on former Celtics star Isaiah Thomas, who made his debut with Denver (39 - 18) on Wednesday. Thomas had not seen NBA action in 11 months, as he had been sidelined by a hip injury. The diminutive All-Star made an impact in 13 minutes on the court, scoring eight points on 3 - of - 6 shooting. His Nuggets debut was overshadowed, though, by a back - and - forth game that culminated in a thrilling final sequence. Down two late in the game, Sacramento (30 - 27) tied it up at 118 on a layup from shooting guard Buddy Hield. Hield could not force overtime, though, as the Nuggets capitalized on their last possession with a buzzer - beating floater from superstar Nikola Jokic. On top of his late - game heroics, Jokic led his team with 18 rebounds and 11 assists. The standout showing marked Jokic's 12th triple - double this season. Paul Millsap also delivered a gem for Denver, scoring 25 points to go with 12 rebounds. The win marked the second straight for the Nuggets after the team suffered a three - game losing streak last week. Hield was Sacramento's leading scorer with 25 points. The Kings also saw 11 assists from star point guard De'Aaron Fox. Newly-acquired forward Harrison Barnes had his best night since joining the team, tallying 19 points and 11 rebounds. Wednesday's contest carries both teams into the All-Star break.
