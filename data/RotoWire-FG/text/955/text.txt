The visiting New Orleans Pelicans took down the Golden State Warriors, 126 - 120, at Oracle Arena on Saturday night. The Pelicans (46 - 34) established themselves early, scoring 40 first - quarter points and taking an early eight - point lead. However, the Warriors (57 - 23), led by All-Star forward Kevin Durant, battled back, taking the lead back midway through the third quarter. The final 15 minutes of the game was a back - and - forth slog, but the Pelicans were able to take a commanding edge with just under four minutes left. A Nikola Mirotic three - pointer gave New Orleans an eight - point lead, and while the Warriors were able to whittle that down to a one - possession game, the Pelicans retained the lead until time expired. Anthony Davis led the Pelicans with 34 points and 12 rebounds, padding his stellar season averages of 28 points and 11 rebounds per game. Veteran point guard Rajon Rondo dished out a whopping 17 assists while also scoring 12 points. Mirotic scored another 28, while Jrue Holdiay added 25 points to the winning effort. All five New Orleans starters scored in double digits in the win. Durant, meanwhile, was the Warriors' offensive leader, scoring a team - high 41 points despite the loss. Durant also grabbed 10 rebounds, as did fellow All-Star forward Draymond Green, who added 11 points and nine assists. Guard Quinn Cook scored another 21 points and dished out seven assists. On Sunday, the Warriors will visit Phoenix, while the Pelicans will travel to Los Angeles on Monday.
