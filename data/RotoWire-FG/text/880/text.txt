The Golden State Warriors defeated the Boston Celtics, 108 - 105, at Oracle Arena on Saturday. Despite the fact that Boston led for the entirety of the first half, Golden State prevailed thanks to a big second half. The third quarter was the difference in the game, with the Warriors outscoring the Celtics, 30 - 19. It was competitive throughout though, as there were seven ties and seven lead changes in total, with neither team ever leading by more than 10 points. The only notable difference in the stat sheet was rim protection, as Golden State swatted away nine shots, while Boston recorded just two blocks. Fastbreak scoring was also a big factor, with the Warriors winning that battle, 17 - 7. The Celtics (35 - 15) were led by Kyrie Irving, as he amassed 37 points, two rebounds and four assists on 13 - of - 18 from the field. Jaylen Brown totaled 20 points and two rebounds. Al Horford accumulated 15 points, 13 rebounds, three assists and two blocks. Marcus Morris led the bench with 12 points and eight rebounds. The Warriors (40 - 10) got a season - high 49 points out of Stephen Curry, as he also added four rebounds, five assists and two steals on 16 - of - 24 from the field. Kevin Durant finished with 20 points, nine rebounds and two assists. Draymond Green collected 15 points, 11 rebounds, five assists and three blocks. On deck for Boston is a road matchup versus the Denver Nuggets on Monday. As for Golden State, they travel to play the Utah Jazz on Tuesday.
