The Golden State Warriors defeated the Dallas Mavericks, 125 - 122, at American Airlines Center on Wednesday evening. The Warriors (30 - 8) entered this game as heavy favorites, but they had to work harder than expected to defeat a Mavericks (13 - 26) team that made things quite difficult for the reigning NBA champions. Golden State built a decent 35 - 26 lead in the first quarter, but Dallas answered back in the second and third quarters to make it a two - point game heading into the fourth. Both teams went back and forth and it was tied at 122 late in the game. But a late three - pointer from Steph Curry and solid defense in the final play of the game allowed Golden State to come away with a win on enemy soil. Stephen Curry led the way offensively for the Warriors with 32 points, eight assists and six rebounds while shooting 6 - of - 13 from three - point range. Both Klay Thompson and Kevin Durant ended with 25 points, although the former Oklahoma City Thunder filled the stat sheet since he also added 10 rebounds, six assists, and four blocks. Draymond Green chipped in with 18 points, 10 boards, and six assists. Seven Dallas players scored in double figures, as Dwight Powell led the way off the bench with 21 points and seven rebounds. Wesley Matthews had 19 points, while Harrison Barnes contributed with 18. Dennis Smith Jr. chipped in with 14 points and eight assists, and Dirk Nowitzki came close to a double - double after registering 12 points and eight boards.
