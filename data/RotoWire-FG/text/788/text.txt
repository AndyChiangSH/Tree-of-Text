The Phoenix Suns defeated the Minnesota Timberwolves, 118 - 110, at Talking Stick Resort Arena on Saturday evening. The Suns (5 - 9) used a 140 run late in the fourth quarter to steal one from the Timberwolves. The Timberwolves (7 - 5) held a slight lead for most of the game, but went dry with a four - minute scoring drought, that allowed the Suns to pass them by. After winning five straight, this marks the second loss in a row for Minnesota. T.J. Warren and Devin Booker poured in 35 points each to lead Phoenix in the win. Booker added nine rebounds and six assists, while Warren grabbed six boards of his own. Marquese Chriss added 13 points, seven rebounds, five blocks and two assists, in 26 minutes. No other player scored in double digits for the Suns, but Mike James was solid in 18 minutes off the bench, with nine points, five assists and three rebounds. The Suns shot a blistering 53 percent from the field, including 38 percent from long range. Meanwhile, Andrew Wiggins led the Timberwolves with 27 points on 10 - of - 22 shooting, in 38 minutes. Jimmy Butler followed up with 25 points, seven rebounds, five assists, one block and one steal, in 40 minutes of action. Karl-Anthony Towns notched a double - double with 17 points and 12 rebounds, in 38 minutes. The only other player to scored in double digits for Minnesota was Taj Gibson, who also recorded a double - double, with 16 points and 11 rebounds, in 36 minutes. The Timberwolves shot 40 percent from the field, including only 20 percent from long range. The Timberwolves will move on to face the Jazz in Utah on Monday. The Suns play host to the Lakers on Monday.
