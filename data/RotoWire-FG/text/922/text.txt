The Denver Nuggets defeated the visiting Los Angeles Lakers, 125 - 116, at Pepsi Center on Friday evening. The Nuggets got out to a great start at home, as they outscored the Lakers 37 - 28 in the first quarter. They expanded their lead to 13 early in the second, before the Lakers made a push to cut the deficit to just one, as they trailed 47 - 46, 5:30 into the quarter. The Nuggets managed to stay ahead until halftime, where they led 65 - 62. The Nuggets came out strong in the third and led for most of the quarter, until the Lakers made a push to finish the quarter on a 11 - 4 run, and take a 96 - 94 lead into the fourth. The Lakers kept a slight edge for most of the fourth quarter, but the Nuggets kept chipping away and managed to reclaim a 112 - 110 lead with 4:24 left in the contest. The Nuggets closed the game on a 13 - 6 run, that propelled them to the much - needed win. All five starters scored in double digits for Denver, led by Jamal Murray who finished with 22 points, eight assists, four steals, three rebounds and one block, in 35 minutes. NIkola Jokic and Paul Millsap followed up with 21 points each, while Gary Harris tallied 19 points, five rebounds, five assists and three steals. Will Barton added 13 points, five rebounds, five assists and one steal. The Nuggets shot 51 percent from the field, including 15 - of - 31 from long range. Meanwhile, Brook Lopez led the Lakers with 29 points on 12 - of - 18 shooting, in 32 minutes. Julius Randle followed up with 18 points, six rebounds, four assists and one steal, while Kentavious Caldwell-Pope added 17 points, nine rebounds, one assist and one steal. The Lakers shot 50 percent from the field, including 14 - of - 34 from long range. The Lakers (29 - 36) will look to bounce back as they play host to the Cavaliers on Sunday. The Nuggets (36 - 30) play host to the Kings on Sunday.
