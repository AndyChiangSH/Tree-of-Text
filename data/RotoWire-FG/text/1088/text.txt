The Golden State Warriors took down the Miami Heat, 120 - 118, in a down - to - the - wire tilt at Oracle Arena on Sunday. Miami (25 - 29) caught Golden State (40 - 15) off guard, jumping out to a 26 - 7 lead in the first quarter thanks to a flurry of perimeter offense. The Heat saw three quick three - pointers from Josh Richardson, who had the hot hand all night as he scored 37 points. Hassan Whiteside also contributed early for Miami, scoring six points off of offensive rebounds in the first quarter. However, the defending champs fought back in characteristic fashion. The Warriors delivered a classic third - quarter thrashing, pouring in 36 points to erase Miami's lead. Golden State held onto the lead thanks to a number of crunch - time shots from Kevin Durant, who led all scorers with 39 points. The All-Star backcourt of Klay Thompson and Steph Curry also impressed in the win, pitching in 54 combined points for the Warriors. Draymond Green took on a playmaking role in the Golden State offense, dishing out a season - high 14 assists on the night. It was a quiet night on offense for DeMarcus Cousins, who scored just seven points while pulling down only three rebounds. Cousins was a disruptive force defensively, though, logging three blocks and two steals. The Heat saw 24 points from Dion Waiters, who shot 9 - of - 19 from the field. It was also a big night for Justise Winslow, who poured in 22 points to go with six rebounds and four assists. Next on the agenda for Miami is a trip to Denver, where they will play the second half of a back - to - back on Monday. Golden State, meanwhile, is set to host the Utah Jazz on Tuesday.
