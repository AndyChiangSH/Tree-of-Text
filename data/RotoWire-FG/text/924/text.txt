The Indiana Pacers defeated the host Boston Celtics, 99 - 97, at TD Garden on Sunday evening. The game started out close, with the Celtics holding a slight edge through the first and a 25 - 24 lead entering the second quarter. The Celtics picked up the pace in the second, as they outscored the Pacers 25 - 16 in the quarter, expanding their lead to 50 - 40 by halftime. Kyrie Irving (knee) did not take the floor in the second half. The Pacers took advantage of the shorthanded Celtics in the third quarter, as they outscored the Celtics 34 - 20 in the quarter to take a 74 - 70 lead into the fourth. The Pacers maintained a lead through most of the fourth quarter, until the Celtics rallied to tie it up at 95 with 36 seconds left. The Pacers responded with a lay up by Myles Turner and then two free throws from Victor Oladipo to take a four - point lead. However, the Celtics were not finished as Jayson Tatum hit a lay up to make it a two - point game. They then immediately drew a charge to get the ball back, but missed their final shot attempt at the buzzer, leaving the Pacers to their second straight win. Victor Oladipo led the Pacers with 27 points, four assists, three rebounds, three steals and one block, in 35 minutes. Myles Turner followed up with 19 points, 10 rebounds, three blocks and one steal, while Bojan Bogdanovic added 14 points, three rebounds, three assists and one block. The Pacers shot 48 percent from the field, including 12 - of - 22 from long range. Meanwhile, Marcus Smart led the Celtics with 20 points, eight assists, seven rebounds and four steals, in 35 minutes. Jayson Tatum followed up with 19 points, five rebounds, three assists, two blocks and one steal, while Terry Rozier added 16 points, six rebounds and two assists. The Celtics shot 42 percent from the field, including 12 - of - 37 from long range. The Celtics (46 - 21) will look to bounce back as they play host to the Wizards on Wednesday. The Pacers (39 - 28) take on the 76ers in Philly on Tuesday.
