The Utah Jazz defeated the Orlando Magic, 94 - 80, at Vivint Smart Home Arena on Monday. While there were 10 lead changes in the first 20 minutes, Utah held the lead for the entirety of the second half. The second quarter is where the game was decided, as the Jazz outscored the Magic, 29 - 15, as Orlando never got the game within five points in the second half. Defense was once again the key for Utah, as they held Orlando to 34 percent from the field and 32 percent from three - point range. Defensive rebounding was a big part of that, with the Jazz winning that battle, 42 - 30. Utah ended up shooting 49 percent from the field and 32 percent from long range. The Magic (20 - 44) were led by Nikola Vucevic, as he tallied 15 points, 12 rebounds and three assists. Aaron Gordon finished with 13 points and six boards. Evan Fournier went just 4 - of - 18 from the field to total 12 points, two rebounds and two assists. The Jazz (34 - 30) got a gem out of Rudy Gobert, as he collected 21 points, 17 rebounds and two blocks. Donovan Mitchell accrued 19 points, five rebounds and four assists. Joe Ingles generated 18 points, seven rebounds, eight assists and three steals. Ricky Rubio accumulated 12 points, four rebounds and eight assists. Both teams will play road games on Wednesday for their next outings, with Utah traveling to face the Indiana Pacers and Orlando facing the Los Angeles Lakers.
