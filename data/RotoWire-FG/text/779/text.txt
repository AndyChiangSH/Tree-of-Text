The Philadelphia Sixers traveled to Wells Fargo Center and defeated the Indiana Pacers, 121 - 110, on Friday evening. The Sixers (5 - 4) showed grit and resiliency, allowing them to come from behind with an impressive reaction during the second half. Even though the Pacers (5 - 4) built a 63 - 56 after the first half, Philadelphia stormed back with a 65 - 47 victory during the final 24 minutes. The game was tight throughout most of the contest, but the Sixers pulled away in the fourth quarter and moved over.500 for the first time since 2013. J.J. Redick and Ben Simmons paved the way for the Sixers in this one. The veteran shooting guard had his best performance since joining Philadelphia in the offseason as he finished with a game - high 31 points (including 8 - of - 13 from three - point range), while Simmons posted the second triple - double of his career. The talented rookie finished the contest with 14 points, 11 assists, 11 rebounds, three blocks, and two steals. On the other end, Victor Oladipo once again led the way for the Pacers with another stellar offensive output of 31 points, seven assists, and five rebounds. Bojan Bogdanovic finished with 21 points. Former Sixer Thaddeus Young chipped in with 15 points and five boards. Myles Turner came off the bench to contribute with 11 points and nine rebounds after a seven - game absence due to a concussion.
