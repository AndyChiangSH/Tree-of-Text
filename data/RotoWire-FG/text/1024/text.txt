The Oklahoma City Thunder defeated the Utah Jazz, 122 - 113, at Chesapeake Energy Arena on Monday. The final score certainly indicates that the game was a lot closer than it actually was, as the Thunder (17 - 8) were in full control from start to finish en route to their fifth win in the last six games. After jumping out to an early lead, Oklahoma City never looked back and took a 24 - point lead into the fourth quarter. While the visiting Jazz (13 - 15) had a big final period and tried to make a push, they never had a legit chance to win Monday's game. Paul George's impressive stretch continued, as the swingman scored a game - high 31 points on 8 - of - 10 shooting from the field in just 30 minutes. Russell Westbrook struggled mightily from the field, going just 4 - of - 18 and scoring 12 points, but 11 rebounds and 10 assists gave the point guard yet another triple - double. Dennis Schroder, however, made up for Westbrook's struggles in a big way, coming off the bench to score 23 points in 30 minutes. As for the Jazz, this was their second straight loss as the team's up - and - down season continues. They had six different players score in double figures, but Donovan Mitchell scored just 19 points to lead Utah in scoring. Rudy Gobert added a double - double of 13 points and 14 rebounds, but for the most part, Utah's starters were a no - show Monday night. As a team, the Jazz shot a solid 51 percent from the field, but they couldn't get anything going from behind the arc, going just 8 - of - 29 from long range.
