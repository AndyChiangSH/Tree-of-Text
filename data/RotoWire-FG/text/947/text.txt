The Golden State Warriors defeated the host Oklahoma City Thunder, 111 - 107, at Chesapeake Energy Arena on Tuesday evening. The Thunder got out to a good start at home, with a quick 70 lead. They held the lead through the entire first quarter and took a 27 - 24 lead into the second quarter. The Warriors turned it on in the second, as they started the quarter with a 7 - 1 run and stayed in control the rest of the way until they led 57 - 47 by halftime. The Warriors maintained a double - digit lead for the first half of the third quarter, but the Thunder came back and took a momentary lead before they trailed 84 - 81 entering the fourth. The game stayed tight through the fourth, while the Warriors were able to hold the slight edge the rest of the way, en route to their third straight win. Kevin Durant led the way for the Warriors with 34 points, 10 rebounds, four assists, one steal and one block, in 35 minutes. Kay Thompson followed up with 20 points, four rebounds, three assists and three steals, while Quinn Cook chipped in 12 points, six assists and three rebounds. The Warriors shot 49 percent from the field, including 8 - of - 29 from long range. Meanwhile, Russell Westbrook led the Thunder with 44 points, 16 rebounds, six assists and two steals, in 40 minutes. Paul George finished with 20 points, eight rebounds and two assists, while Carmelo Anthony added 12 points, eight rebounds and two assists. The Thunder shot 38 percent from the field, including 9 - of - 38 from long range. The Thunder (45 - 34) will look to bounce back as they take on the Rockets in Houston on Saturday. The Warriors (57 - 21) take on the Pacers in Indiana on Thursday.
