The host Los Angeles Lakers defeated the Washington Wizards, 102 - 99, at Staples Center on Wednesday. The Lakers made up for a tough Dodgers loss after a thrilling affair matching up the elite John Wall against No. 2 overall pick Lonzo Ball. The matchup went back and forth through the first half with the Wizards eventually pulling away in the third quarter. However, the Lakers (2 - 2) defense rejuvenated the youthful team in the fourth quarter where they held the Wizards (3 - 1) to just 13 points. With just a few ticks on the clock remaining, Brandon Ingram missed a driving layup but followed up with a tip on his own rebound with one second left to send the game to overtime. The overtime period started off in favor of Bradley Beal after a basket, a pair of free throws and a floater continued to give Washington the momentum, but the Lakers kept up on each possession including two big three - pointers from Kyle Kuzma and Kentavious Caldwell-Pope. After a Julius Randle dunk which put Los Angeles up three points with four seconds remaining, John Wall missed a relatively clean look from three as the Lakers walked away with their first home win of the season. Bradley Beal led all scorers with 28 points, to go with six rebounds, two assists, three steals and one block, while John Wall struggled from the field but still posted 18 points on 7 - of - 22 shooting. Jodie Meeks was efficient off the bench for Washington and contributed 11 points in 16 minutes while adding three steals on the defensive end. Lonzo Ball struggled more than Beal with his jumper and finished just 2 - of - 11 from the field, but added 10 assists and eight rebounds, including some highlight worthy full court feeds. Ingram was the hero in the fourth quarter and added 19 points and 10 boards on 50 percent shooting. Both teams play on Friday, as the Wizards head north to face the Golden State Warriors, while the Lakers host the Toronto Raptors.
