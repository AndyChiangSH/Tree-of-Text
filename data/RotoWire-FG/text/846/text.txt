The Boston Celtics defeated the visiting Brooklyn Nets, 108 - 105, at TD Garden on Sunday evening. The Celtics (30 - 10) were massive favorites ahead of this game, but they had to work harder than expected against a hard - working Nets (13 - 23) team that made things quite complicated for Boston. Even though the Celtics jumped to a 10 - point lead after the first quarter, the Nets answered and cut the deficit to two points at the break. Both teams went back and forth during the second half, but in the end Boston came on top despite Brooklyn's attempts to send the game to overtime. One of the big differences was both teams' shooting from the field, as the Celtics hit 49 percent of their shots while Brooklyn converted just 41 of theirs. Star point guard Kyrie Irving led the way for Boston, as he finished with 28 points. Jaylen Brown had 13 points, and Al Horford contributed with 10 points, 10 rebounds, five assists, and three blocks. Markieff Morris and Terry Rozier had very good performances coming off the bench as well. The shooting guard contributed with 14 points and seven rebounds, while the power forward chipped in with 15 points of his own. Six Brooklyn players scored in double digits, led by Rondae Hollis-Jefferson who contributed with 22 points and 12 rebounds. Allen Crabbe had 12 points, and Spencer Dinwiddie chipped in with 10 points and eight assists. Caris LeVert had a monster game off the bench as well, finishing with 16 points, seven assists, six rebounds, and two steals. Quincy Acy had 14 points as well.
