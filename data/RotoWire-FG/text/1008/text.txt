The Sacramento Kings defeated the Utah Jazz, 119 - 110, at Vivint Smart Home Arena on Wednesday evening. Two of the league's more notable second - year players squared off in Wednesday's contest, as both Donovan Mitchell and De'Aaron Fox delivered impressive performances to lead their respective teams. For Sacramento (10 - 8), Fox was at the center of the win, dishing out 13 assists and scoring 17 points. His strong facilitation helped contribute to an excellent offensive night for the Kings. They finished the game shooting 53 percent from the field. Utah (8 - 10), on the other hand, shot only 43 percent in the loss, but kept the game close by making it to the free - throw line 31 times on the night. While Fox's double - double helped propel the Kings to victory, a number of other players also had big performances. Willie Cauley-Stein shot 11 - of - 15 on his way to a team - high 23 points. Nmanja Bjelica finished with 18 points and seven rebounds, while rookie big man Marvin Bagley III impressed on the defensive end, blocking two shots and grabbing two steals. Utah, meanwhile, saw 35 points from Donovan Mitchell. Mitchell shot 13 - of - 22, but his big night was not enough to earn his team a win. Rudy Gobert added 13 points and 15 rebounds, while Joe Ingles had 12 points and seven assists. The Jazz will visit the Los Angeles Lakers on Friday, while the Kings will take a short trip to face the Golden State Warriors at Oracle Arena on Saturday.
