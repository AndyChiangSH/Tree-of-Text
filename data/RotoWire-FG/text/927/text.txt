The Los Angeles Lakers took down the Denver Nuggets, 112 - 103, at Staples Center on Tuesday. It was a game of runs, and it was ultimately a fourth - quarter push by Los Angeles (31 - 36) that secured a home - court win. The Lakers, led by rookie forward Kyle Kuzma, outscored Denver 35 - 21 in the fourth to earn a victory. Kuzma led the team with 26 points and 13 rebounds, including five three - pointers. He also dished out two assists. Eight of those points came in a 90 - second span in the middle of the fourth quarter, helping the Lakers pull ahead before the game's close. Julius Randle matched Kuzma's production, also scoring 26 points and pulling down 13 rebounds. Randle, though, was especially effective on the offensive glass, where he pulled down a whopping seven boards. Randle was also assessed a technical in the closing moments after a heated exchange with Denver star Nikola Jokic. Both big men were slapped with technicals following the testy confrontation, which included Randle shoving Jokic to the ground. Veteran forward Wilson Chandler was the top scorer for Denver with 26 points on 11 - of - 16 shooting. Chandler played a whopping 41 minutes, which was also a team high. Unfortunately for the Nuggets, it was a tough night for their bench unit. Denver's second unit shot just 9 - of - 29 on the night for 25 points. The Lakers will fly to the Bay Area to take on the Golden State Warriors on Wednesday night. Meanwhile, Denver will return home to host the Detroit Pistons on Thursday.
