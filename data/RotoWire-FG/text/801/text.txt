The Cleveland Cavaliers defeated the visiting Brooklyn Nets, 119 - 109, at Quicken Loans Arena on Wednesday evening. It was a close game for most of the way, with the Nets (6 - 11) leading by one, 46 - 45, at the half, before being all tied up at 77 after three quarters. The Cavaliers (11 - 7) turned it up a notch in the fourth quarter, where they outscored the Nets, 42 - 32, on way to their sixth straight win. LeBron James took over with 23 points in the fourth quarter, on his way to a game - high 33 points, along with six rebounds, five assists and two steals, in 33 minutes. Kevin Love tallied a double - double with 18 points and 10 rebounds, along with three assists and one block, in 31 minutes. Dwyane Wade provided a nice boost off the bench, with 18 points, five assists, four rebounds, and one steal, in 28 minutes. The Cavs shot 52 percent from the field, including 40 percent from long range. Meanwhile, Rondae Hollis-Jefferson was the high - point man for the Nets, with 20 points, along with six rebounds and two assists, in 24 minutes. Spencer Dinwiddie dished 10 assists, and added eight points, one rebound, one steal and one block, in 27 minutes. Joe Harris poured in 18 points on 7 - of - 10 shooting, in 28 minutes off the bench. The Nets also shot 52 percent from the field, including 40 percent from long range. The Nets will look to bounce back as they play host to the Trail Blazers on Friday. The Cavs play host to the Hornets on Friday.
