The Los Angeles Clippers defeated the host Sacramento Kings, 116 - 109, at Golden 1 Center on Friday evening. The Clippers got out to a solid start on the road, as they led for almost the entire first quarter, before settling on a three - point lead heading into the second. The Kings responded in the second by pulling ahead in the first couple minutes and then holding the edge to take a 56 - 52 lead into halftime. The third quarter featured almost the same pattern of scoring, except that it was the Clippers that outscored the Kings, to reclaim the advantage, with a five - point lead entering the final. The Clippers stayed strong in the fourth and while the Kings managed to make it a three - point game with four minutes left, the Clippers came up clutch to seal the road win. This marks the third straight loss for Sacramento. Landry Shamet led the Clippers with 20 points, four rebounds, two assists, and a block, in 32 minutes. Danilo Gallinari tallied 19 points, nine rebounds and three assists, while Lou Williams chipped in 17 points, four assists, three rebounds and a steal, in 28 minutes off the bench. The Clippers shot 44 percent from the field, including 12 - of - 31 from long range. Meanwhile, Buddy Hield led the Kings with 23 points, seven rebounds, three assists and a steal, in 35 minutes. Willie Cauley-Stein added 16 points, 12 rebounds, four blocks and one assist, while De'Aaron Fox finished with 12 points, 12 assists, five rebounds and one steal. The Kings shot 44 percent from the field, including 6 - of - 21 from long range. The Kings (31 - 31) will look to bounce back as they play host to the Knicks on Monday. The Clippers (35 - 29) play host to the Knicks on Sunday.
