The Toronto Raptors defeated the Portland Trail Blazers, 130 - 105, at Air Canada Center on Friday. Despite the fact that there were three ties and five lead changes in the first five minutes, that's the only time this game was close. In fact, the Raptors rode a 37 - 18 first quarter lead, as Portland never got the game within single - digits from there. Offensive rebounding was the difference, with the Raptors winning that battle, 17 - 4. That allowed them to take 99 shots while the Blazers took only 73 shot attempts. Three-point shooting was also decisive, with Toronto hitting 19 - of - 40 and Portland going just 7 - of - 21. Bench production was also a huge difference, with Toronto outscoring Portland's bench, 53 - 25. The Trail Blazers (29 - 23) were led by Damian Lillard, as he tallied 32 points, three rebounds and 10 assists on 11 - of - 18 from the field, C.J. McCollum amassed 21 points, two rebounds and two assists. Jusuf Nurkic accrued 20 points, seven rebounds and three assists on 8 - of - 11 shooting. The Raptors (35 - 16) got a gem out of DeMar DeRozan, as he collected 35 points, two rebounds and three assists. Jonas Valanciunas amassed 18 points, eight rebounds and two assists. Kyle Lowry accumulated 15 points, four rebounds and five assists. Fred VanVleet, Pascal Siakam and Norman Powell all scored in double figures off the bench. Both teams return to action on Sunday, with Portland traveling to face the Boston Celtics and Toronto hosting the Memphis Grizzlies.
