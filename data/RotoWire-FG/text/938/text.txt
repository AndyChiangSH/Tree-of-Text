The Minnesota Timberwolves defeated the New York Knicks, 108 - 104, at Madison Square Garden on Friday evening. The Timberwolves (42 - 31) likely weren't taking anything for granted coming into the matchup, but the Knicks (26 - 47) probably turned out to be a more formidable opponent than they expected. New York appeared to be in a spoiler mindset, and remained as close as 105 - 101 with 1:39 remaining. A trio of free throws from Karl-Anthony Towns (one) and Jeff Teague (two) then extended the lead to seven with 11 seconds remaining, giving Minnesota the needed cushion to escape the Big Apple with a win and clinch their first winning record since the 2005 - 6 season. Towns paced the Timberwolves with 24 points, 13 rebounds, three assists, three blocks and one steal. Taj Gibson posted 18 points, seven rebounds, one assist and one steal. Andrew Wiggins generated a 17 - point, 11 - rebound double - double that also included two assists and two steals. Nemanja Bjelica matched Wiggins' scoring total and added six rebounds, two assists, two steals and one block. Jamal Crawford tallied a bench - leading 13 points and also supplied three assists and one rebound. Teague pitched in 10 points and eight assists. Tim Hardaway was brilliant while leading the Knicks, accounting for 39 points, three assists, two rebounds and three steals. Trey Burke contributed a bench - leading 15 points, which he complemented with nine assists, two rebounds and two steals. Frank Ntilikina pitched in 13 points, five boards, three assists and one steal. Enes Kanter delivered an 11 - point, 15 - rebound double - double that also included one assist. Michael Beasley mustered eight points, eight rebounds and two assists. The Timberwolves continue their trek through the Northeast and visit the Philadelphia 76ers on Saturday night, while the Knicks head to the Nation's Capital to do battle with the Washington Wizards on Sunday evening.
