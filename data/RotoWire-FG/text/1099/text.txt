The Phoenix Suns defeated the Miami Heat, 124 - 121, at AmericanAirlines Arena on Monday. The Heat (26 - 32) headed into halftime with a fairly comfortable 11 - point lead after scoring 63 points over the first 24 minutes, but the visiting Suns (11 - 50) wasted no time making their comeback in the second half. And after scoring 40 points in the final quarter and some timely free throws in the closing minutes, Phoenix was able to walk away with the upset victory. Seven different Suns players would score in double figures Monday, and the team managed to torch Miami's defense, shooting 41 percent from behind the arc and 51 percent from the field overall. Devin Booker, who hit what ended up being the game - winning free throws with 17 seconds remaining, led the way with a team - high 20 points and nine assists in 33 minutes, while former Heat player Tyler Johnson, who was traded to Phoenix at the trade deadline, was close behind with 18 points of his own. In the end, it was just a complete team effort from the Suns, who recorded 30 assists on their 45 made field goals to just 14 turnovers. As for the Heat, their shocking home struggles continue, as they dropped to 11 - 17 on their home floor this season and have now lost five of their last six games. Miami did, however, get stellar play out of their starting frontcourt, with Hassan Whiteside scoring a game - high 29 points to go along with 11 rebounds in 27 minutes and Kelly Olynyk posting 28 points and 10 rebounds of his won in 41 minutes. However, for the Heat, offensive production was not the problem, as they simply could not string together stops in the second half against this lowly Suns team. It was the second game in a row that Miami gave up more than 115 points in a game.
