The Chicago Bulls defeated the Brooklyn Nets, 112 - 73, at United Center on Wednesday. This game meant much more to Chicago than Brooklyn and a 39 - point margin shows just that. It was a wire - to - wire win for the Bulls, as they led by 14 after the first quarter and ended up winning the fourth quarter, 37 - 17, to pull away in dominating fashion. Three-point shooting was a glaring difference, with Brooklyn hitting just 3 - of - 33 and Chicago going 14 - of - 30. Rebounding was a huge factor as well, with Chicago winning that battle, 62 - 48. It was the Bulls defense that was especially feisty, as they held Brooklyn to 33 percent shooting and that aforementioned ugly three - point total. The Nets (20 - 62) played this game without most of their starters and that was evident by this final scoreline. Archie Goodwin was Brooklyn's best player, as he provided 20 points, seven rebounds and four assists. K.J. McDaniels finished second on the team, dropping 15 points and six rebounds off the bench as well. Caris LeVert was the only starter in double figures, as he totaled 10 points, five rebounds and two assists. The Bulls (41 - 41) needed this win to clinch a playoff berth and they did it in dominating fashion. Jimmy Butler was the player of the game, as he tallied 25 points, six rebounds, four assists, four steals and two blocks. Paul Zipser finished second on the team, amassing 21 points and six rebounds off the bench. Bobby Portis recorded a double - double off the bench as well, accruing 12 points and 10 rebounds.
