The Los Angeles Clippers took down the Milwaukee Bucks, 105 - 98, at Staples Center on Tuesday evening. Los Angeles (40 - 34) led by as much as 14 points early on, but Milwaukee (39 - 35) responded with a major run in the third quarter. The Bucks battled back to take a late lead, holding an advantage for much of the second half. The Clippers, though, fought back. Austin Rivers tied the game at 88 with a three - pointer, then hit a layup to give the Clippers the lead with just under six minutes remaining. The Clippers never gave up that lead, earning a much - needed win as they look to remain relevant in the Western Conference playoff picture. Tobias Harris led the Clippers with 19 points on 8 - of - 17 shooting. DeAndre Jordan added a double - double, scoring 13 points and pulling down 16 rebounds. Overall, the team benefited from strong three - point shooting. The Clippers went 15 - of - 28 from beyond the arc in the win. Milwaukee, meanwhile, saw a typically strong night from superstar forward Giannis Antetokounmpo. The 'Greek Freak' tallied a game - high 26 points along with nine rebounds and seven assists. Khris Middleton added another 22 points for the Bucks. However, the Bucks had trouble matching the red - hot three - point shooting of LA. Middleton and Antetokounmpo accounted for four of Milwaukee's six three - pointers, which came on just 6 - of - 23 shooting. The Bucks will visit Golden State on Thursday, while LA faces the second half of a back - to - back on Wednesday with a trip to Phoenix.
