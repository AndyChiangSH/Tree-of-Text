The host Portland Trail Blazers took down the Minnesota Timberwolves on Thursday, 105 - 98. Portland (39 - 40) is pushing for the playoffs and has recently taken control of the eight seed in the Western Conference. Interestingly, it was sixth man Allen Crabbe who led the team's offensive push on Thursday. Crabbe came off the bench and shot 8 - of - 12 en route to a team - high 25 points to go along with five rebounds and three assists. Thanks in large part to Crabbe, Portland enjoyed a major advantage in bench scoring. The Blazers held a 37 - 13 edge in points off the bench. Point guard Damian Lillard also enjoyed a stellar game, scoring 22 points to go along with nine rebounds and eight assists. Minnesota (31 - 47) has been eliminated from playoff contention but still put up a strong fight on Thursday night. It was a particularly great night for the young dynamic duo of Andrew Wiggins and Karl-Anthony Towns. Wiggins led the game with 36 points on 14 - of - 27 shooting to go along with eight rebounds. Towns, meanwhile, had a massive double - double, recording 24 points, 16 rebounds, a block and a steal. The rest of the team, however, had some struggles, as they shot just 43 percent from the field as a unit and saw only 13 points off the bench. The Timberwolves will visit Utah on Friday, while Portland will host the Jazz on Saturday.
