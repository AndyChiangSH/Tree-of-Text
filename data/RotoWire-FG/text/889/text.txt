The Washington Wizards defeated the host Orlando Magic, 115 - 98, at Amway Center on Saturday. Despite the fact that this game was close throughout the first half, Washington really took control from there. A 31 - 22 third quarter was the difference, as Orlando never got the game within 15 points for the entirety of the fourth quarter. Ball movement was the difference in the game, with the Wizards recording 35 assists and Orlando handing out just 17 dimes. That led to Washington shooting 54 percent from the field and Orlando hitting just 44 percent of their shots. The Magic (15 - 36) had no player surpass 15 points scored, as Jonathon Simmons led the starters with 15 points, two rebounds and two steals. Elfrid Payton accrued 14 points, four rebounds and four assists. Mario Hezonja led the bench with 15 points, four rebounds, two assists and two steals. The Wizards (30 - 22) were led by Otto Porter, as he tallied 20 points, four rebounds and five assists. Tomas Satoransky totaled 19 points, three rebounds, six assists and three steals. Bradley Beal accumulated 18 points, eight rebounds, eight assists and two steals. Markieff Morris amassed 16 points, three rebounds, three assists and three steals. Both teams play road games on Monday for their next fixture, with Orlandi traveling to play the Miami Heat and Washington facing the Indiana Pacers.
