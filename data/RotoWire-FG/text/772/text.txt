The Detroit Pistons defeated the host Los Angeles Clippers, 95 - 87, at Staples Center on Saturday. Los Angeles came into this game as the final undefeated team in the league, but they choked this game away despite a 10 - point lead at the half. In fact, Detroit outscored Los Angeles, 28 - 15, in the fourth quarter to cap off their comeback win. Defense was key for Detroit, as they held Los Angeles to 33 percent shooting, while forcing them to commit 18 turnovers. Detroit also won the points in the paint battle, 38 - 22, and the fastbreak differential, 22 - 7. The Pistons (4 - 2) were led by Reggie Jackson, who accumulated 15 points, four rebounds and seven assists. Andre Drummond also finished with 15 points, adding 17 rebounds and three blocks to his tally. Avery Bradley tallied 12 pints, five rebounds, two assists and four steals. The Clippers (4 - 1) got another double - double out of Blake Griffin, as he accrued 19 points, 11 rebounds and four assists. Austin Rivers led the team in scoring, amassing 20 points, four rebounds and four steals. DeAndre Jordan scored just seven points, but provided 14 rebounds and three blocks. Detroit will have a quick turnaround, as they play the Golden State Warriors on Sunday for their next fixture. The Clippers next opponent will also be the Warriors, as they play host to them on Monday for their next game.
