The Oklahoma City Thunder defeated the San Antonio Spurs, 90 - 87, at Chesapeake Energy Arena on Sunday. Despite the fact that San Antonio played this game without three of their best players, it came down to the final shot. In fact, there were four led changes in the fourth quarter, as neither team led by more than eight points. Rebounding carried Oklahoma City, as they outrebounded San Antonio, 47 - 39. They also dominated scoring down low as well, as they outscored the Spurs in the paint, 52 - 36. The lack of starter production really hurt San Antonio as well, as the Thunder's starters outscored their starting - five, 66 - 41. The Spurs had to play this game without Kawhi Leonard, LaMarcus Aldridge and Tony Parker, as they almost pulled out their most impressive win of the season. Dejounte Murray carried the load with all those players out, as he tallied 17 points, 10 rebounds, five assists and three steals. No other starter reached double figures in scoring. David Bertans led the bench with 16 points, four rebounds and five blocks. The Thunder were led by Russell Westbrook. as he collected 22 points, 10 rebounds and 10 assists en route to a triple - double. Steven Adams totaled 19 points, 10 rebounds and three steals. Paul George had a game to forget, as he accumulated eight points, eight rebounds and four assists on 2 - of - 17 shooting, while committing six turnovers. San Antonio will have to play again on Monday, as they host the Detroit Pistons. As for Oklahoma City, they play host to the Utah Jazz on Tuesday for their next fixture.
