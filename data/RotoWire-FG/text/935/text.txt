The New Orleans Pelicans defeated the Indiana Pacers, 96 - 92, at Smoothie King Center on Wednesday. This was a rescheduled game from a February 7 matchup in which the game got called off due to a leakage issue, as both of these teams left it all on the floor in this crucial matchup. In fact, neither team ever led by more than six points, as there were nine ties and 17 lead changes in total. The difference for New Orleans was their transition offense, with the Pelicans outscoring the Pacers, 26 - 15, in fastbreak points. New Orleans ended up shooting 43 percent from the field while holding Indiana to just 37 percent shooting. Defensive rebounding was also big, with the Pelicans winning that battle, 40 - 33. The Pacers (41 - 31) were led by Victor Oladipo, as he tallied 21 points, eight rebounds and three assists. Myles Turner amassed 13 points, 10 rebounds and two blocks. Thaddeus Young totaled nine points, 10 rebounds, two assists and three steals. The Pelicans (42 - 30) got another double - double out of Anthony Davis, as he collected 28 points, 13 rebounds and five blocks. E'Twaun Moore accumulated 23 points, three rebounds and two steals. Nikola Mirotic led the bench with 15 points, six rebounds and two blocks. Cheick Diallo also played well off the bench, providing nine points and 10 rebounds. New Orleans will have to play their third game in three days on Thursday, as they play host to the Los Angeles Lakers. As for Indiana, they play host to the Los Angeles Clippers on Friday for their next outing.
