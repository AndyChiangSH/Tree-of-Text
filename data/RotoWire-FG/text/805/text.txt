The Golden State Warriors defeated the visiting New Orleans Pelicans, 110 - 95, at Oracle Arena on Saturday evening. The Pelicans (11 - 9) jumped right out of the gate, outscoring the Warriors 31 - 17 in the first quarter. The Warriors (15 - 5) responded in the second quarter, where they outscored the Pelicans 36 - 22, to tie it up at 53 by halftime, and carried the momentum through the third to take the lead, 84 - 75, heading into the fourth. The Pelicans made a push in the final frame, bringing it to a six - point game with 4:30 to go, before the Warriors pulled away one final time. Stephen Curry was the high - point man for the Warriors, with 27 points on 9 - of - 25 shooting, in 36 minutes. Klay Thompson followed up with 24 points, four rebounds, two assists, two steals and two blocks, in 36 minutes. Andre Iguodala gave a solid boost off the bench, with 14 points, four assists, three rebounds and two steals, in 30 minutes of action. The Warriors shot 42 percent from the field, including 10 - of - 34 from long range, and dished 30 assists. Meanwhile, Anthony Davis was the high - point man for New Orleans, with 30 points, to go with 15 rebounds, two assists and two blocks, in 38 minutes. DeMarcus Cousins tallied 15 points, seven rebounds, five blocks, four assists and one steal, in 31 minutes. E'Twaun Moore chipped in 16 points, on 6 - of - 10 shooting, in 29 minutes. The Pelicans shot 41 percent from the field, including 11 - of - 36 from long range. The Pelicans will have a few days off before they play host to the Timberwolves on Wednesday. The Warriors play host to the Kings on Monday.
