The New York Knicks defeated the Los Angeles Lakers, 113 - 109, at Madison Square Garden on Tuesday. This was one of the most competitive games of the season, as it took an overtime session to decide a winner. In fact, there were 17 ties and 20 lead changes in total, with neither team ever leading by more than 10 points. Defense was key for New York, as they held Los Angeles to 41 percent shooting. The Knicks ended up hitting 48 percent of their shots and 41 percent from three - point range. The Lakers (10 - 16) were led by Kentavious Caldwell-Pope, as he tallied 24 points, four rebounds, three assists and three steals. Lonzo Ball finished with 17 points, eight points, six assists and two steals. Brook Lopez amassed 14 points, four rebounds and three assists. Kyle Kuzma led the bench with 19 points and six rebounds. The Knicks (14 - 13) got another gem out of Kristaps Porzingis, as he accrued 37 points, 11 rebounds and five blocks. Enes Kanter totaled 14 points and 11 rebounds. Jarrett Jack scored just three points, but still provided six rebounds and 10 assists. Michael Beasley, Frank Ntilinka and Doug McDermott all reached double figures in scoring off the bench.
