The Milwaukee Bucks defeated the Portland Trail Blazers, 113 - 110, at BMO Harris Bradley Center on Saturday. Giannis Antetokounmpo scored a career - high 44 points to lead the Bucks (2 - 1). He added eight rebounds, four assists and two steals. Antetokounmpo came up big in the waning moments with a steal, a block and a dunk to seal the game in the final 30 seconds. There were 15 ties and 10 lead changes with neither team leading by double - digits. The shooting disparity was the difference, with Milwaukee hitting 53 percent from the field, while holding Portland to just 43 percent. The Bucks also dominated down low, outscoring the Trail Blazers, 54 - 26, in the paint. The Blazers (2 - 1) dropped their first game in the last of a three - game road trip to start the season. Damian Lillard was Portland's best player, as he accrued 26 points, five rebounds, four assists and four steals. C.J. McCollum also scored 26 points, adding three rebounds and two steals to his stat line. Jusuf Nurkic finished with 17 points, 11 rebounds, three assists and three blocks. Khris Middleton totaled 18 points, six rebounds and three assists for Milwaukee. Tony Snell went 7 - of - 8 from the field to score 17 points. Malcolm Brogdon was the only other Buck in double figures, totaling 13 points, six assists and four steals. On deck for Milwaukee is a home matchup against the Charlotte Hornets. Portland plays its home opener Tuesday against the New Orleans Pelicans.
