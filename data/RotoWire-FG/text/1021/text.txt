The Boston Celtics defeated the host Chicago Bulls, 133 - 77, at United Center on Saturday. Boston came into this game as a big favorite but no one could ever anticipate a 56 - point win. The Celtics actually started the game with 17 unanswered points, as that obviously killed the Bulls demeanor. It's not like they rebounded in the second half though, as Boston outscored Chicago, 69 - 34. Every statistic was lopsided in the Celtics favor, as they really dominated through defense. In fact, they held the Bulls to 38 percent from the field and 25 percent from three - point range while winning the turnover differential, 15 - 7. In addition, Boston outrebounded Chicago, 54 - 37, while connecting on 54 percent from the field and 51 percent from long range. The Celtics also out - assisted the Bulls, 34 - 16, and won the steals differential, 10 - 3. The Celtics (15 - 10) had seven players reach double figures in scoring, as Jaylen Brown led the team with 23 points, five rebounds, three assists and two steals off the bench. Daniel Theis led the starters with 22 points, 10 rebounds, five assists and four blocks. Jayson Tatum amassed 18 points, three rebounds and two assists while Kyrie Irving finished with 13 points, four rebounds and five assists. Terry Rozier also played well off the bench, providing 15 points, eight rebounds, two assists and three steals. The Bulls (6 - 21) had just one starter reach double figures, as Zach LaVine totaled 11 points, three rebounds and two assists. Lauri Markkanen and Justin Holiday combined for 13 points on 5 - of - 20 shooting. Shaquille Harrison led the team in scoring, providing 20 points off the bench. Cameron Payne accrued 10 points, three rebounds and three assists off the bench as well. Both teams return to action on Monday, with Chicago welcoming the Sacramento Kings to United Center and Boston playing host to the New Orleans Pelicans.
