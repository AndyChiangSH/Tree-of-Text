The Sacramento Kings defeated the host Cleveland Cavaliers, 129 - 110, at Quicken Loans Arena on Friday evening. The Kings jumped out to a great start on the road, as they poured in 38 points in the first quarter, while holding the Cavs to 27 points, to take an 11 - point advantage into the second. The Cavs answered back in the second quarter, as they returned the favor by outscoring the visitors by 11 in the quarter, to make it a tie game at 66 by halftime. The Kings went back to work in the third quarter, as they quickly opened up a nine - point lead, and held on to outscore the Cavs, 30 - 23, to take a, 96 - 89, lead entering the final frame. The Kings kept the pressure on and pushed their lead to 15, early in the quarter. They were able to hold on from their to earn their third win in a row. De'Aaron Fox led Sacramento with 30 points, 12 assists, three steals and two rebounds, in 29 minutes. Buddy Hield added 25 points, four rebounds, two assists and two blocks, while Marvin Bagley III chipped in 17 points on 8 - of - 9 shooting, in 25 minutes off the bench. The Kings shot 58 percent from the field, including 13 - of - 28 from long range. Meanwhile, despite having three players top 20 points, the Cavs still came up short at home. Jordan Clarkson was the high - point man, with 26 points on 11 - of - 19 shooting, in 29 minutes off the bench. Collin Sexton tallied 23 points, while Alec Burks added 22 points, nine assists, seven rebounds and one steal, in 37 minutes off the bench. The Cavs (5 - 20) will look to bounce back as they play host to the Wizards on Saturday. The Kings (13 - 11) head to Indiana to take on the Pacers on Saturday.
