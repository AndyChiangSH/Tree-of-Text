The San Antonio Spurs defeated the Portland Trail Blazers 131 - 118 on Sunday at AT & T Center. The final score did not depict the back and forth nature of this battle as the Spurs were able to pull away early in the fourth quarter with big time scores from Rudy Gay, DeMar DeRozan and LaMarcus Aldridge. The game itself had over 10 ties and nearly 20 lead changes, with the all - star back court of Damian Lillard and CJ McCollum firing on all cylinders trying to match the Spurs all - stars in the process. Portland (13 - 10) entered the fourth quarter down 97 - 90, but McCollum drained a three pointer right out the gate to pull within four. Rudy Gay answered with his vintage mid - range jumper and that was the nature of the final period. Anytime the Blazers pulled within the reach, the Spurs (11 - 12) answered immediately and did damage in the paint, which led to easy free throws. DeRozan finished with 36 points, eight rebounds, six assists and two steals. Aldridge contributed 29 points and eight boards. Derrick White found a clutch rhythm off the bench and knocked down 4 - of - 7 from the field on his way to 10 points, while Patty Mills added 15 points in 25 minutes. Lillard led all scorers with 37 points, to go with 10 assists and a pair of steals. McCollum added 24 points and Al-Farouq Aminu pitched in 20 points and nine boards. Both teams play on Tuesday, as the Blazers visit the Dallas Mavericks, while the Spurs visit the Utah Jazz.
