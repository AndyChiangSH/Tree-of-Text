The Dallas Mavericks defeated the Portland Trail Blazers, 115 - 109, at American Airlines Center on Tuesday. Despite the fact that this matchup looked lopsided on paper, 10 ties and 14 lead changes show just how close this game was. With that said, Dallas held the lead for the final 20 minutes of action, as Portland simply couldn't recapture the lead. Turnovers really killed the Trail Blazers, as they committed 13 of them while forcing just seven from the Mavericks. Three-point shooting was also huge, with Dallas hitting 17 - of - 44 while Portland connected on just 10 - of - 40. Fastbreak points were also critical, with the Mavericks winning that battle, 21 - 12. The Trail Blazers (48 - 30) were led by Damian Lillard, as he tallied 29 points, five rebounds and eight assists on just 6 - of - 20 from the field. C.J. McCollum finished with 16 points, four rebounds and eight assists. Jusuf Nurkic totaled 15 points and 13 rebounds. Shabazz Napier led the bench with 13 points and four assists. The Mavericks (24 - 54) had all five starters reach double figures in scoring, as Dennis Smith Jr. led them with 18 points, eight rebounds and eight assists. J.J. Barea amassed 15 points, two rebounds and five assists. Harrison Barnes and Dirk Nowitzki each went 5 - of - 14 from the field as they scored 15 and 14 points respectively. On deck for Dallas is a road matchup versus the Orlando Magic on Wednesday. As for Portland, they travel to face the Houston Rockets on Thursday for their next outing.
