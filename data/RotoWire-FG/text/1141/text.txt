The Portland Trail Blazers defeated the Denver Nuggets, 100 - 96, in Game 7 of the Western Conference semi - finals at Pepsi Center on Sunday. With the win, the Blazers have reached the Western Conference Finals and will face the Golden State Warriors. This game had everything one could've asked for out of a Game 7, including a huge comeback in the second half. Denver jumped to a quick 29 - 17 lead after the first quarter, but things went downhill for them after that lead. Portland won all of the remaining three quarters - 22 - 19, 32 - 24 and 29 - 24, respectively - and the Blazers made big after big shot down the stretch. The game wasn't decided until the very final seconds, though, but Portland held on and secured their return to the Western Conference Finals where they'll take on Steph Curry and the Warriors, who might have DeMarcus Cousins available for the upcoming round. There's no question C.J. McCollum was Portland's best player in this match, since he finished with 37 points to go along with nine rebounds in 45 minutes of action. Damian Lillard also played 45 minutes and while he wasn't very accurate from the floor (3/17 overall) he still finished with a productive stat line of 13 points, 10 rebounds and eight assists. Enes Kanter had 12 points with 13 rebounds. Evan Turner contributed 14 points off the bench. Nikola Jokic was Denver's most productive player since he ended with 29 points and 13 boards. Jamal Murray had 17 points, six rebounds and five assists, while Gary Harris added 15 points. Paul Millsap had 10 points before fouling out late in the fourth quarter. The bench struggled, however, as the whole second unit ended the contest with 17 points while going 6 - 16 from the field.
