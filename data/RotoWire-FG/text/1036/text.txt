The Los Angeles Lakers took down the Golden State Warriors, 127 - 101, on Christmas Day at Oracle Arena. It was a marquee matchup between two of the Western Conference's top teams on Christmas. Ultimately, however, it was not the Warriors' (23 - 12) night, as the team shot just 41 percent en route to a blowout loss. Struggles compounded for Golden State, as each of the team's four active All-Stars performed poorly in the loss. The team saw a dismal final quarter, culminating in Draymond Green fouling out with only four points scored on the night. It was a big win for the Lakers, who now sit at fourth in the Western Conference standings. All did not go as hoped for Los Angeles (20 - 14), though, as superstar LeBron James exited the game in the third quarter with a left groin strain. James did not return to action, though his team saw plenty of success in his absence. LA, without LeBron on the court, outscored Golden State 36 - 19 in the fourth quarter. LA saw a true team effort as it took down the defending champions. Seven players scored in double digits for the Lakers, and the team tallied 27 total assists. Kyle Kuzma led the team in scoring with 19 points, while veteran point guard Rajon Rondo delivered 15 points and 10 assists in just 23 minutes of action. Golden State, meanwhile, was led by a season - best 23 points from Andre Iguodala. Iduodala's vintage performance was not enough to help his team, though, as the Warriors shot just 41 percent from the field and 25 percent from three - point range. It was a particularly poor showing for Klay Thompson and Draymond Green, who combined to shoot just 4 - of - 14 while committing six turnovers on the night. Next on the agenda for the Lakers is a visit to Sacramento on Thursday. Golden State, meanwhile, will gear up to host the Portland Trail Blazers later this week.
