The New York Knicks defeated the Denver Nuggets, 116 - 110, at Madison Square Garden on Monday. Despite the fact that there were only four ties and two lead changes in this game, it was close throughout the second half. In fact, it was a single - digit margin for the final 18 minutes. Denver actually overcame a 22 - point halftime deficit by going on a 27 - 2 run in the third quarter to take their one and only lead. Rebounding was decisive for the Knicks, as they outrebounded the Nuggets 49 - 36. New York ended up shooting 50 percent from the field while holding Denver to just 41 percent shooting. The Nuggets (3 - 4) were led by Nikola Jokic, who accumulated 28 points, eight rebounds and two assists. Jamal Murray finished with 20 points, three rebounds and two assists. Gary Harris collected 18 points, three assists, three steals and two rebounds. The Knicks (3 - 3) got a career - high from Kristaps Porzingis, who led the team with 38 points, seven rebounds and three blocks. Tim Hardaway Jr. accrued 13 points, four rebounds, three assists and three steals. Kyle O'Quinn came off the bench to provide 15 points, 12 rebounds and five blocks. Both teams play again on Wednesday, with Denver hosting the Toronto Raptors and New York hosting the Houston Rockets.
