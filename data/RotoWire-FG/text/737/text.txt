The host Golden State Warriors took down the Minnesota Timberwolves on Tuesday, 121 - 107. Golden State (64 - 14) continues to roll, winning its 12th straight game against the Wolves. On Tuesday, it was shooting guard Klay Thompson who led the team to victory, scoring 41 points on the night while shooting 13 - of - 24 and pouring in seven three - pointers. As a team, the Warriors saw an impressive offensive night, shooting over 52 percent and hitting a superb 14 three - pointers. The Dubs also dished out a massive 33 assists, with nine coming from two - time reigning MVP Steph Curry. Minnesota (31 - 46) kept with the Warriors in the first half but fell victim to yet another dominant Warriors third quarter. It was a typically dominant night for the young up - and - comers of Minnesota, as Karl-Anthony Towns and Andrew Wiggins both scored over 20 points. Wiggins finished with 24 points, tying bench forward Shabazz Muhammad for the team lead. Muhammad also pulled down 11 rebounds on the night. It was a nice night for the whole team on the boards, as they out - rebounded Golden State 50 - 40 on the game. The Warriors will visit Phoenix on Wednesday, while the Timberwolves will visit Portland on Thursday.
