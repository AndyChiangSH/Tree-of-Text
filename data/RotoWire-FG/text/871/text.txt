The San Antonio Spurs defeated the Brooklyn Nets, 100 - 95, at Barclays Center on Wednesday evening. The Spurs (30 - 16) and the Nets (16 - 29) delivered a game that ended being far tighter than what anyone would have expected, but the Western Conference team ended coming out on top. It wasn't easy, however, as the Nets never gave up and in fact Brooklyn looked impressive in the fourth quarter, so much that they could have easily evened things up had they reacted sooner. San Antonio jumped on top quickly, as they won the first quarter 31 - 26 and maintained that five - point advantage heading into halftime. The Spurs extended that lead in the third, and entered the final 12 minutes with a 12 - points advantage. Brooklyn reacted and made it a close contest late in the fourth, but it was too late and San Antonio ended winning a tough game on the road. LaMarcus Aldridge was the star of the game for San Antonio, as the power forward once again carried a heavy load on the offense and finished with 34 points and eight rebounds. Patty Mills had one of his best games of the season coming off the bench, as the Australian point guard chipped in with 25 points on 7 - of - 10 from the field. Pau Gasol came close to a triple - double after notching 13 points, 12 rebounds and seven assists. Allen Crabbe was the top scorer for the Nets, as the talented scorer registered 20 points with eight rebounds and three assists. Spencer Dinwiddie struggled from the field since he finished with nine points on 3 - of - 13 from the field, but had 12 assists and two steals. Joe Harris (18 points) and Caris LeVert (13 points) made an impact off the bench. DeMarre Carroll had 11 points and 10 boards.
