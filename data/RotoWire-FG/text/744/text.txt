The Golden State Warriors defeated the New Orleans Pelicans, 123 - 101, at Oracle Arena on Saturday. This was a lopsided matchup on paper and that proved to be true on the court as well. The second half was particularly lopsided, with Golden State leading by at least 20 points for the final 16 minutes. Rebounding was the biggest factor for the Warriors, as they won that battle, 53 - 34. They were also very efficient, recording 36 assists on 51 percent shooting, while committing just nine turnovers. Free-throw shooting was a factor as well, with Golden State hitting 15 - of - 16 and New Orleans going just 12 - of - 19. The Pelicans (33 - 47) played this game without Anthony Davis and DeMarcus Cousins, as they had very little to compete with the best team in the league. Alexis Ajinca got the start at center and led the starters with 17 points and six rebounds. Jrue Holiday accrued 10 points, six rebounds and 10 assists. Jordan Crawford and Quinn Cook played well off the bench, combining for 43 points on 18 - of - 29 from the field. On deck for New Orleans is a road matchup against the Los Angeles Lakers on Tuesday. The Warriors (66 - 14) have secured the top - spot in the west and wins like this show just how motivated they are. Kevin Durant made his return from a knee injury to accrue 16 points, 10 rebounds and six assists. Klay Thompson led the team, as he scored 20 points to add to his three rebounds and two assists. Ian Clark and Andre Iguodala were huge off the bench, as Clark provided 17 points, four rebounds and three assists, with Iguodala tallying 15 points, five rebounds and five assists. The Warriors will host the Utah Jazz for their next game on Monday.
