The Indiana Pacers defeated the Chicago Bulls, 107 - 105, at United Center on Friday evening. The Pacers (6 - 3) came into the contest at full health, which is much more than the host Bulls (2 - 7) could say. Chicago came in missing a quartet of key players, but they didn't let that stop them from giving Indiana everything it could handle. The hosts jumped out to a 37 - 22 first - quarter lead, but the visitors flipped the game in the second quarter to head into halftime with a slim two - point edge. The two teams then traded the lead back and forth throughout the second half, and Chicago boasted a 99 - 92 advantage as late as the 5:55 mark of the fourth quarter. However, Indiana went on a 15 - 6 run to finish the game, with a Darren Collison 19 - foot jumper with 18 seconds remaining ultimately serving as the game - winning bucket. Ultimately, Myles Turner's block of an Antonio Blakeney jumper with less than a second remaining sealed the victory. Victor Oladipo led the Pacers with 25 points, which he supplemented with 14 rebounds, five assists, two steals and one block. Turner supplied 18 points, three rebounds, three assists and six blocks. Collison provided 14 points overall, while Tyreke Evans led the bench with 13. Blakeney paced the Bulls with 22 points and three rebounds off the bench. Zach LaVine registered 20 points, six rebounds, five assists, one steal and one block. Justin Holiday turned in 19 points, two assists and one rebound. Wendell Carter, Jr. and Jabari Parker provided matching 11 - point tallies, along with eight and seven rebounds, respectively. The Pacers head back home to take on the Boston Celtics on Saturday night, while the Bulls remain home and tangle with the Houston Rockets on Saturday evening as well.
