The visiting Indiana Pacers took down the Golden State Warriors, 91 - 82, at Oracle Arena on Tuesday night. With All-Stars Steph Curry, Klay Thompson, Kevin Durant, and Draymond Green all sidelined by various injuries, the Warriors (54 - 20) had trouble finding the bottom of the basket. This wasn't too problematic early in the game, as their stifling defense held Indiana (44 - 31) to just 39 first - half points. However, the Pacers heated up in the third quarter, thanks largely to All-Star guard Victor Oladipo. Oladipo had 24 points to lead the team as they steadily gained a lead through the second half. On top of Oladipo, forwards Bojan Bogdanovic and Thaddeus Young added a combined 34 points. Young also pulled down 10 rebounds, including five on the offensive end. While the team had a relatively strong night from inside the arc, hitting 49 percent of its shots, three - point shooting was a difficulty. The Pacers went just 6 - of - 21 from beyond the three - point line, including 1 - of - 6 off the bench. Golden State, though, struggled far more from every spot on the floor. The Warriors shot just 41 percent from the field, including 5 - of - 17 from three - point range. Veteran guard Nick Young was the team's scoring leader with only 12 points. The one saving grace for Golden State, though, was its strong offensive rebounding. The team grabbed 17 boards on the offensive end. On Thursday, Golden State will host the Milwaukee Bucks. Meanwhile, the Pacers will stay in California for a visit to Sacramento.
