The Chicago Bulls defeated the host Miami Heat, 105 - 89, at American Airlines Arena on Wednesday. While Miami came into this game as a sizable favorite, Chicago dominated them in this surprising upset. What's even more shocking is the fashion of the win, with the Bulls leading for nearly 40 minutes of this game. They did most of their damage in the first, third and fourth quarters, outscoring Miami, 86 - 62. The shooting disparity was key, with Chicago shooting 51 percent from the field while holding Miami to just 38 percent shooting. Much of that can be attributed to scoring down low, with the Bulls outscoring the Heat in the paint, 48 - 36. The Bulls (12 - 40) were without Zach LaVine (ankle) for this game but picked up just their second win in their last 16 games. Bobby Portis was their best player, as he provided 26 points and four rebounds on 10 - of - 17 shooting off the bench, Wayne Selden Jr. led the starters with 20 points, two rebounds and eight assists. Kris Dunn accrued 14 points, five rebounds, eight assists and two steals while Lauri Markkanen finished with 14 points, 13 rebounds and four assists. The Heat (24 - 25) were led by Tyler Johnson, as he provided 15 points, three rebounds and two assists off the bench. Justise Winslow led the starters with 14 points, six rebounds, five assists and two steals. On deck for Miami is a home matchup versus the Oklahoma City Thunder on Friday. As for Chicago, they return to action on Saturday when they play the Charlotte Hornets on the road.
