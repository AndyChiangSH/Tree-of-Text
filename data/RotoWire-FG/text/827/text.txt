Two of the most promising young teams in the league squared off on Tuesday, with the Philadelphia 76ers earning a 118 - 112 win over the Minnesota Timberwolves in a thrilling overtime period at Target Center. Philadelphia (14 - 13) snapped its four - game losing streak on Tuesday in exciting fashion. The Sixers kept it close against the host Timberwolves throughout, ultimately taking the game in overtime. Throughout the win, Philly's offensive approach was characterized by aggressively drawing contact. The team took 32 free - throws on the night, including 23 between starters Joel Embiid and J.J. Redick. The duo capitalized on those attempts, shooting a near - perfect 22 - of - 23 from the line. Embiid dominated throughout the game, finishing with a team - high 28 points and 12 rebounds, as well as eight assists. He made the most of the matchup with Minnesota star Karl-Anthony Towns, holding the opposing big man to just 6 - of - 16 shooting from the field. Redick also excelled throughout the game, scoring 26 points and knocking down three three - pointers. Minnesota (16 - 12), meanwhile, was not in the best position for an overtime period, as every starter played big minutes in regulation. Karl-Anthony Towns and All-Star Jimmy Butler played at least 45 minutes each, including 48 for Towns. Butler made the most of the minutes, though, scoring a game - high 38 points on 15 - of - 33 shooting. The Wolves were beneficiaries of an enormous number of Philly turnovers, as the Sixers coughed the ball up 24 times on the night. Thanks to the huge turnover disparity, as well as a solid offensive rebounding night, Minnesota attempted 28 more field goals than Philadelphia in the game. The gap in shot attempts made up for the Wolves' poor shooting, as the team shot only 41 percent from the field and hit just five three - pointers. Minnesota will host the Kings on Thursday, while Philadelphia will see the Thunder on Friday.
