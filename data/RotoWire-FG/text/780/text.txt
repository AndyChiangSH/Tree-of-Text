The New Orleans Pelicans defeated the host Chicago Bulls in overtime, 96 - 90, at United Center on Saturday. This was one of the tightest games of the season, as it took an extra session to decide a winner. In fact, there were 12 ties and 11 lead changes in total, as neither team ever achieved a double - digit lead. Defense was key for the Pelicans, as they held the Bulls to 35 percent from the field and 26 percent from three - point range. New Orleans also dominated down low, outscoring Chicago, 42 - 30, in the paint. The Pelicans (5 - 5) were led by Anthony Davis, who collected 27 points, 16 rebounds and three blocks. DeMarcus Cousins finished with 25 points, 11 rebounds and four assists. Jrue Holiday accrued 10 points, five rebounds and six assists. Jameer Nelson played well off the bench, providing 13 points, five rebounds, five assists and four steals. The Bulls (2 - 6) had five players score in double figures, but the other five players who played combined for just 15 points. Justin Holiday led the way with 18 points, nine rebounds and three steals. Robin Lopez amassed 14 points and nine rebounds but committed five turnovers. Lauri Markkanen totaled 14 points, seven rebounds and four assists. Jerian Grant fell just shy of a triple - double, collecting 13 points, eight rebounds and nine assists. Both teams return to action on Tuesday, with New Orleans traveling to face the Indiana Pacers and Chicago playing the Toronto Raptors on the road as well.
