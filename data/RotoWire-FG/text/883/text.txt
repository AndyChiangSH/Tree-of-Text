The Orlando Magic defeated the Los Angeles Lakers, 127 - 105, at Amway Center on Wednesday evening. The Magic (15 - 35) had an impressive performance in this game, and dominated the Lakers (19 - 31) from start to finish, to the point that they won each quarter separatedly on route to a massive win that also ended their three - game losing skid. Orlando grabbed a four - point advantage after one, and increased it to seven at halftime. The biggest differential came in the third, as Orlando pulled away thanks to a 43 - 30 huge lead in the third, and the Lakers didn't have enough response through the remainder of the game. It was a convincing win for Orlando, and one that should definitively boost their confidence going forward. The Magic had a balanced performance on offense, since seven players ended scoring in double digits. Marreese Speights led the way for Orlando with 21 points in just 23 minutes, while Elfrid Payton filled the stat sheet thanks to 15 points, seven rebounds, six assists, and four steals. Evan Fournier had 19 points and Jonathon Simmons added 14. Mario Hezonja (14 points), Shelvin Mack (13 points and six assists) and D.J. Augustin (12 points) contributed nicely off the bench as well. Jordan Clarkson and Julius Randle led the way for the Lakers, as each ended with 20 points although Randle added nine rebounds as well. Brandon Ingram had 13 points, six boards and five assists, while Kentavious Caldwell-Pope had 12. Kyle Kuzma finished with 11 points and three boards off the bench.
