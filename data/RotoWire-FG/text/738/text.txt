The Cleveland Cavaliers defeated the Boston Celtics, 114 - 91, Wednesday at TD Garden. The Cavaliers (51 - 27) came into Wednesday's contest with revenge on their minds, and following a low - scoring, tightly contested first quarter that saw the Celtics (50 - 28) take a one - point lead, the motivation kicked in; at least for LeBron James it did. James launched a 22 - 4 run to open up the second quarter and he and his teammates never looked back, as Boston was unable to get much closer than that throughout the remainder of Wednesday's game. James finished with a game - high 36 points, 10 rebounds and six assists in 39 minutes as his team avenged their last - second loss at Boston earlier in the season. Each of the other four Cleveland starters joined James in reaching double figures in the scoring column, but the story of the game was their defense. The Cavaliers' defensive effort was their best in a long time, as 91 points is the fewest they've given up since March 16 against the Jazz. The Celtics managed to shoot just 41 percent from the field, 21 percent from behind the arc, and to make matters even worse, 62 percent from the free throw line. It was the first time in nine games that Boston failed to score at least 100 points and their lowest point total since they scored 88 against the Warriors on Nov. 18. Isaiah Thomas still managed to lead the way with a team - high 26 points to go along with six assists and four rebounds, but no other Celtic scored more than 13 points in the loss. The win for Cleveland moves them one game ahead of the Celtics for first place in the Eastern Conference standings with just four games remaining for each team.
