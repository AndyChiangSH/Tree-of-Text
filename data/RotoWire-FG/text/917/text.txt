The Portland Trail Blazers defeated the visiting Oklahoma City Thunder, 108 - 100, at Moda Center on Saturday evening. The first quarter started off as a close battle with neither team taking more than a two - possession lead, before the Thunder emerged with a 28 - 25 advantage heading into the second. The Trail Blazers made a push in the second quarter and created some separation with a seven - point lead mid way through the quarter, but held on to just a three - point lead, 53 - 50, by halftime. The Trail Blazers came out strong in the third quarter and kept the advantage for most of the way, as they expanded their lead to 83 - 75 heading into the final frame. The Blazers kept their foot on the gas and pushed their lead to 10 with 5:44 left in the game. They stayed in control the rest of the way, playing the Thunder to a tie through the fourth quarter, en route to their sixth straight win. C.J. McCollum led the way for Portland with 28 points on 10 - of - 20 shooting, eight rebounds, six assists, two blocks and one steal, in 37 minutes. Damian Lillard followed up with 20 points on 5 - of - 18 shooting, seven assists, one rebound, one steal and one block, in 36 minutes. Evan Turner added 17 points, three rebounds, two assists and one block, in 32 minutes. The Trail Blazers shot 44 percent from the field, including 11 - of - 33 from long range. Meanwhile, Russell Westbrook led the Thunder with 30 points on 12 - of - 31 shooting, 11 rebounds, six assists and one block. Paul George followed up with 21 points, seven rebounds, four assists and one block, while Jerami Grant chipped in 13 points on 6 - of - 9 shooting off the bench. The Thunder shot 44 percent from the field, including 6 - of - 27 from long range. The Thunder (37 - 28) will look to bounce back as they play host to the Rockets on Tuesday. The Trail Blazers (37 - 26) take on the Lakers in Los Angeles on Monday.
