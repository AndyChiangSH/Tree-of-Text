The New York Knicks defeated the Charlotte Hornets, 124 - 101, at Madison Square Garden on Saturday. Despite the fact that Charlotte either led or tied for the entirety of the first quarter, that's the only time this game was competitive. In fact, a 35 - 26 second quarter set the tone for the Knicks, as a 42 - 17 third quarter was the dagger. Charlotte actually never got the game within 20 points in the fourth after that devastating third quarter. The shooting differential was key, with New York hitting 49 percent from the field and 46 percent from three - point range while holding Charlotte to 37 percent from the field and 27 percent from long range. Much of that can be attributed to ball movement, with the Knicks recording 24 assists and the Hornets handing out only 11 assists. The Hornets (30 - 40) had no player surpass 15 points scored, as Dwayne Bacon led the team with 15 points and three assists off the bench. Frank Kaminsky and Malik Monk both provided 12 points apiece off the bench as well. Dwight Howard led the starters with 14 points and 13 rebounds. Kemba Walker and Nicolas Batum combined for 12 points, four assists and no steals. The Knicks (25 - 45) had just one starter in double figures, as Tim Hardaway Jr. tallied 25 points, three rebounds and three assists. Enes Kanter amassed nine points and nine rebounds. Four bench players reached double figures, as Frank Ntilinka, Trey Burke, Troy Williams and Isaiah Hicks combined for 54 points. Both teams return to action on Monday, with New York playing host to the Chicago Bulls and Charlotte traveling to face the Philadelphia 76ers.
