The Detroit Pistons defeated the visiting Miami Heat, 112 - 103, at Little Caesars Arena on Sunday evening. The Pistons (10 - 3) led 31 - 29 after one quarter. The Heat (6 - 7) fired back in the second quarter and kept the momentum into the third, where they led 75 - 68 halfway through the quarter. The Pistons bounced back though, using a 21 - 8 run to close the third and take an 83 - 81 lead into the fourth. The Pistons continued to push and reached their biggest lead of the game with about four minutes left in the fourth, from where they cruised to their fifth straight win. Tobias Harris was the high - point man for Detroit, with 25 points on 8 - of - 15 shooting, including 5 - of - 8 from long range, in 36 minutes. Avery Bradley followed up with 24 points, six rebounds, two steals and one assist, in 30 minutes. Andre Drummond took care of business with 17 rebounds, while Luke Kennard had the best game of his young career, with 14 points, five rebounds and three assists, in 27 minutes off the bench. The Pistons shot 48 percent from the field, including 50 percent from long range. Meanwhile, five players scored in double digits for the Heat, led by Hassan Whiteside, who posted 20 points, 12 rebounds, two blocks and one steal, in 29 minutes. Goran Dragic followed up with 18 points, seven assists and four rebounds, in 34 minutes. Dion Waiters added 16 points, five assists, four rebounds and three steals, while Josh Richardson finished with 15 points, five rebounds and four assists. The Heat shot 43 percent from the field, including 35 percent from long range. This marked the sixth and final game of a road trip for the Heat, which they finished with a 3 - 3 record. They will return home to play host to the Wizards on Wednesday. The Pistons take on the Bucks in Milwaukee on Wednesday.
