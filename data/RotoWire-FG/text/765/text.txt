The Atlanta Hawks defeated the Dallas Mavericks, 117 - 111, at American Airlines Center on Wednesday. In a game that went down to the wire and wasn't decided until the very last second, the Hawks stunned the Mavs and came away with a surprising win on the road at Dallas. Atlanta led 58 - 47 at the break, but Dallas came out of nowhere to tie it 82 - 82 after three. The final minutes of the fourth quarter were quite sloppy, but Atlanta held on and secured the win thanks to a few clutch free throws in the final minute of the game. Dennis Schroder led the Hawks offense with 28 points and seven assists in 34 minutes. Marco Belinelli provided a scoring punch off the bench with points, and John Collins added 14 from the second unit as well. Second-year forward Taurean Prince fell one point shy of a double - double thanks to nine points and 10 rebounds in 25 minutes. Dennis Smith Jr. might be on the losing end of this one, but there is no question the first - year point guard did a good job in his NBA debut. The former NC State standout contributed with 16 points and 10 assists, but at the same time, he committed three turnovers. Nerlens Noel provided 16 points and 11 rebounds off the bench, while Wesley Matthews added 15 points, eight rebounds and five assists. The small - forward, however, also committed five turnovers.
