The Los Angeles Clippers defeated the Atlanta Hawks, 116 - 103, at Phillips Arena on Wednesday night. The two teams came into Wednesday night badly in need of a victory, as the Clippers (6 - 11) had lost a whopping nine straight while the Hawks (3 - 15) had dropped seven of their previous eight. The visitors turned out to want it just a bit more, as they rode a 9 - 2 run at the beginning of the fourth quarter to gain some distance and notch the double - digit road win. Los Angeles enjoyed the benefit of some blistering shooting, as they posted a 58 percent success rate from the field, including 50 percent from three - point range. Blake Griffin unsurprisingly led the way for the Clippers with 26 points, 10 rebounds, 10 assists and one steal. Wesley Johnson was right behind him with 24 points, a total he supplemented with one rebound, one assist, one steal and one block. Johnson went a red - hot 8 - for - 10 from the field, including 6 - for - 7 from three - point range. Lou Williams drew the start at two - guard and poured in 20 points, along with eight assists, one rebound and one steal. Austin Rivers contributed 18 points, five assists, two rebounds, two steals and one block. Marco Belinelli paced the Hawks with 20 points in 26 minutes off the bench. He also tallied three rebounds, two assists and two steals. Dennis Schroder turned in 19 points, seven assists, three rebounds and one steal. Dewayne Dedmon supplied 17 points, six rebounds, one assist and one block. John Collins was stellar while starting at power forward, providing a 14 - point, 10 - rebound double - double that also included three assists and four steals. Taurean Prince notably scored just two points in 21 minutes, going 1 - for - 7 from the field. The Clippers head back to California to take on the Sacramento Kings in a Saturday night road showdown, while the Hawks are home against the New York Knicks on Friday evening.
