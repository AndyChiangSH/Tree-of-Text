The Boston Celtics defeated the host Orlando Magic, 104 - 88, at Amway Center on Sunday. Boston have now won eight - straight games, as they now sit with the best record in the NBA. This was a decisive win too, considering Boston held a double - digit lead for the final 10 minutes. They really separated themselves in the second and fourth quarters, outscoring the Magic 54 - 37. Defense was key for the Celtics, as they held the Magic to 36 percent from the field and 21 percent from three - point range. Boston also won the rebounding battle, 55 - 45. The Celtics (8 - 2) had all five starters score in double figures, even though none of them played more than 28 minutes. Jaylen Brown led them with 18 points, seven rebounds and two steals. Al Horford recorded a double - double, amassing 14 points, 10 rebounds and four assists. Marcus Smart flirted with a triple - double off the bench, collecting 11 points, eight rebounds and eight assists. The Magic (6 - 4) were led by Aaron Gordon, who tallied 18 points and 12 rebounds. Nikola Vucevic accrued 13 points, 10 rebounds and seven assists. Evan Fournier and Terrence Ross combined to shoot just 4 - of - 26 from the field as the starting guards. Boston will be in action again on Monday, as they travel to play the Atlanta Hawks. As for Orlando, they host the New York Knicks on Wednesday for their next game.
