The Cleveland Cavaliers defeated the Atlanta Hawks, 136 - 114, at Quicken Loans Arena on Tuesday evening. The Cavaliers (1 - 6) finally won their first game of the 2018 - 19 season and they did it in emphatic fashion, as they didn't have problems to top the Hawks (2 - 5) in a game where they looked in absolute control right from tip - off. Cleveland quickly jumped ahead to a 35 - 31 lead after the first quarter, but Atlanta kept things close in the second as both teams went separated by one point (58 - 57) at the half - time break. The Cavs sealed the deal in the second half, though: they won the third quarter 41 - 30 and also won the final 12 minutes by a 37 - 27 score. Cleveland's second - half performance was, by far, the team's best performance across the entire season thus far. Seven players scored in double figures for the reigning Eastern Conference champions. Rodney Hood led the way with 26 points, while Cedi Osman chipped in with 20 points and five boards. George Hill added 17, Sam Dekker delivered 12 points with nine boards, and Tristan Thompson delivered a double - double of 11 points and 13 boards. Rookie Collin Sexton had 17 points and eight boards off the bench and Jordan Clarkson added 15 of his own. The Hawks only had four players scoring more than 10 points and the duo of Trae Young (24 points) and Alex Len (22 points, nine rebounds, two steals) led the way. Vince Carter did a good job of filling out the stat sheet since he contributed with 10 points, three rebounds and two assists in 24 minutes of action. Jeremy Lin came off the bench and chipped in with 12 points and eight assists in 24 minutes.
