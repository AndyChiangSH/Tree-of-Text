The Golden State Warriors defeated the Houston Rockets, 118 - 113, at Toyota Center on Friday night in Game 6 of their Western Conference semifinal - round playoff series. The Warriors have won the series, 4 - 2. Despite being down 3 - 2 in the series, the Rockets (2 - 4) came into the contest with a certain sense of confidence knowing they'd be facing a short - handed Warriors (4 - 2) squad on their home court. However, Golden State sent a message in the first half that they wouldn't waste any time hanging their heads, as they matched Houston bucket for bucket despite Stephen Curry failing to score a single point. Houston was able to carve out a five - point cushion heading into the fourth, but Curry remained hot throughout the second half. The sharpshooter then took over the contest with the defending champs trailing by a 97 - 95 margin and 6:43 remaining, scoring 18 of the Warriors' final 23 points to help seal the win and a trip to the conference finals. Curry's 33 points led the Warriors. He added five rebounds and four assists. Klay Thompson supplied 27 points, two rebounds, two assists, two blocks and one steal. Andre Iguodala furnished 17 points, three assists, two rebounds and five steals. Kevon Looney totaled 14 points, five rebounds, one assist, one steal and one block. Shaun Livingston poured in 11 points. Draymond Green mustered eight points, 10 rebounds, seven assists, one steal and one block. James Harden paced the Rockets with 35 points, complementing them with eight rebounds, five assists and four steals. Chris Paul generated a 27 - point, 11 - rebound double - double that also included six assists. P.J. Tucker collected 15 points, four rebounds, two assists and three steals. Clint Capela contributed a 10 - point, 10 - rebound double - double, while Eric Gordon and Austin Rivers had matching nine - point tallies. The Warriors will now await the winner of the Trail Blazers-Nuggets series, which will wrap up Sunday.
