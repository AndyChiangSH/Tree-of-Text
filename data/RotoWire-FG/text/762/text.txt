The Cleveland Cavaliers defeated the Boston Celtics, 112 - 99, in Game 4 of the Eastern Conference Finals at Quicken Loans Arena on Tuesday. Boston actually led for the majority of this game, as it took a late run in the second half for the Cavaliers to pull out the win. In fact, Cleveland outscored Boston, 65 - 42, in the second half to pull away in convincing fashion. The Cavaliers matched a franchise record by shooting 60 percent from the field. Rebounding was also key for Cleveland, as they won that battle, 37 - 29. The Cavaliers dominated the fastbreak points and points in the paint differentials, as they combined to win those categories, 74 - 38. The Celtics (1 - 3) have done a great job of reviving this series, but they're now going to need a three - game winning streak to get to the Finals. Avery Bradley led the Celtics, as he totaled 19 points, five rebounds, three assists and two steals. Jae Crowder amassed 18 points, eight rebounds and four assists. Al Horford accumulated 16 points, three rebounds and seven assists on 7 - of - 11 shooting. Kelly Olynyk started and collected 15 points, five rebounds, three assists and two steals. Marcus Smart did score eight point to add to his six assists, but he went just 1 - of - 9 from the field. The Cavaliers (3 - 1) had one of their biggest letdowns of the season in Game 3, but they now find themselves one win away from reaching the NBA Finals after this Game 4 victory. Kyrie Irving was the player of the game, as he tallied 42 points, three rebounds and four assists on 15 - of - 22 from the field. Irving scored 21 of those points in the third quarter to lead the comeback. LeBron James was equally as good, accruing 34 points, five rebounds and six assists. Kevin Love provided 17 points, 17 rebounds and five assists. No other player on the roster had more than seven points, seven rebounds or four assists. Game 5 will be in Boston on Thursday.
