The Indiana Pacers defeated the Atlanta Hawks 106 - 84 Wednesday at Bankers Life Fieldhouse. The Pacers (42 - 40) came into Wednesday's game with their playoff hopes cleanly laid out for them: they win and they're in. Fortunately for them, they were going against a Hawks (43 - 39) team that rested all of their key players after clinching the No. 5 seed with a win on Monday. The result was a beat down from Indiana, as they essentially led the game wire - to - wire on the back of their star, Paul George. George finished with a game - high 32 points and 11 rebounds in 40 minutes, as the potential All-NBA performer left it all on the floor when his team needed him to the most. Jeff Teague added 19 points and seven assists but left late in the game after coming down awkwardly on his ankle. His status will likely be updated over the course of the next couple of days. As for the Hawks, they had nothing to play for and will be well - rested before their first - round series against the Wizards starts. In the starter's absence, Ersan Ilyasova led the way with 15 points in just 18 minutes, as he was essentially rested prior to the postseason as well. Overall, it was an ugly game for the depleted Atlanta squad, as the 86 points was their lowest point total in five games. And it wasn't because they shot the ball poorly necessarily, but rather, they failed to take care of the basketball, as they committed 18 turnovers to just 19 team assists. Nevertheless, while the loss ended a four - game winning streak, the Hawks will still enter the playoffs with plenty of momentum.
