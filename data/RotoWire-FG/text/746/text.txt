The host Los Angeles Clippers blew out the Houston Rockets on Monday, 125 - 96. LA (50 - 31) remains in a close battle with Utah to earn home - court advantage in their first round series. Monday's game saw an easy win for the Clippers, as they held a double - digit lead for almost the entirety of the second half. Seven Clippers players scored in double digits, as the game proved to be a well - rounded team victory. Superstar point guard Chris Paul led the team with 19 points and dished out nine assists on the night. The team finally appeared to be healthy and firing on all cylinders, as the Clippers ended with a 49 - 41 rebounding advantage and shot over 50 percent from the field on the night. It was also a historic night for veteran forward Paul Pierce, who scored 10 points to pass John Havlicek for 15th on the all - time scoring list. Houston (54 - 27) had a rough night on Monday, starting with superstar James Harden. The MVP contender shot just 2 - of - 9 from the field and 9 - of - 14 at the free - throw line while committing five fouls and five turnovers in only 24 minutes of play. That was roughly how the night went for most of the Rockets' lineup, as the team finished shooting only 39 percent from the field. Rebounding also played a role in the blowout, as Houston was at an eight - rebound disadvantage by the night's end. The Clippers will host the Kings on Wednesday, while Houston will host the Timberwolves.
