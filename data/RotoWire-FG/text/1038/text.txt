The Atlanta Hawks defeated the host Minnesota Timberwolves in overtime, 123 - 120, at Target Center on Friday. While there were only three ties in the first three quarters, it took an extra session to determine a winner in this tightly - contested game. In fact, there were four ties and seven lead changes in the fourth quarter and overtime alone, as it came down to the final shot. Atlanta ended the game strong though, finishing on an 8 - 3 run to take control. Defensive rebounding was critical for the Hawks, as they won that battle, 41 - 31. Atlanta's three - point shooting was also huge, as they connected on 16 - of - 35 while Minnesota hit just 11 - of - 32. Free-throw shooting was the Timberwolves downfall, as they hit just 21 - of - 38. The Hawks (10 - 24) were led by Kent Bazemore, as he tallied 23 points, five rebounds, four assists and two steals before fouling out. John Collins also fouled out, as he finished with 21 points, 10 rebounds and four assists. DeWayne Dedmon amassed 19 points and 13 boards while Trae Young accumulated 16 points, four rebounds and 11 assists. The Timberwolves (16 - 19) were led by Karl-Anthony Towns, as he tallied 31 points, 19 rebounds, five assists and three blocks on 10 - of - 17 shooting. Robert Covington accrued 28 points, seven rebounds, four assists and three blocks. Derrick Rose collected 25 points, three rebounds and nine assists. Andrew Wiggins amassed 16 points, six rebounds, two assists and two blocks. Atlanta has a quick turnaround, as they play host to the Cleveland Cavaliers on Saturday. As for Minnesota, they travel to South Beach to play the Miami Heat on Sunday for their next outing.
