The Philadelphia 76ers defeated the Miami Heat, 106 - 102, at AmericanAirlines Arena in Game 4 of their Eastern Conference first - round playoff series. The Sixers now lead the series, 3 - 1. The Sixers (3 - 1) and the Heat (1 - 3) played a back - and - forth game that included fights, pushovers, a triple - double and several individual performances worth highlighting. The first quarter was quite tight, however, and it ended with a 26 - 26 draw, but Miami grabbed a five - point advantage heading into the break. Philadelphia reduced the margin to four points after three, but they pulled away for good in the fourth quarter. Their 27 - 19 edge during the final 12 minutes was enough to give them a huge win and a commanding 3 - 1 lead in the series. Ben Simmons was the star of the game for the Sixers, as he finished with the first triple - double of his playoff career thanks to 17 points, 13 rebounds and 10 assists. Joel Embiid had 14 points, 12 rebounds and five blocks, but also committed eight turnovers. J.J. Redick was the team's top scorer with 24 points, and Dario Saric added 11 points with eight rebounds. Marco Belinelli and Ersan Ilyasova each added 10 points off the bench. Dwyane Wade was the best player for the Heat since he finished with 25 points coming off the bench. Goran Dragic added 20 points with four steals and three assists. Hassan Whiteside registered a double - double thanks to 13 points and 13 rebounds. James Johnson filled the stat sheet thanks to 15 points, eight rebounds, and five assists.
