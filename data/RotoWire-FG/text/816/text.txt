The Oklahoma City Thunder defeated the visiting Utah Jazz, 100 - 94, at Chesapeake Energy Arena on Tuesday evening. The Thunder (11 - 12) came from behind to pull a huge win against the Jazz (13 - 12) that seemingly came out of nowhere. Utah was leading through most of the game and they entered the final 12 minutes up 80 - 68. But the Thunder used a massive comeback in the fourth quarter to pull away, including a 30 - 12 scoring run in the final 10:30 minutes of the game. Utah tried to recover, it was too late for them and their own mistakes - most notably on the defensive end of the court - doomed them. Russell Westbrook was the star of the game for the Thunder, as the All-Star point guard once again delivered a triple - double thanks to his 34 points, 13 rebounds, and 13 assists. He wasn't alone in the comeback effort, though. Carmelo Anthony and Paul George both came through in the clutch for OKC, and finished with 14 and 21 points, respectively. Steven Adams added 20 points with nine rebounds. Donovan Mitchell was the star of the game for the Jazz, as the talented rookie finished with 31 points, four assists and four steals, although he also committed five turnovers. Joe Ingles added 16 points, while Thabo Sefolosha and Alec Burks both had big games off the bench. Sefolosha finished with 11 points, seven boards and four steals, while Burks added 11 points with five assists.
