The Houston Rockets defeated the host Indiana Pacers, 118 - 95, at Bankers Life Fieldhouse on Sunday, This was a dominating victory for Houston, as they led from start to finish. In fact, the Rockets held a double - digit lead for the final 20 minutes en route to their wire - to - wire win. A 35 - 18 first quarter was key, as that was too much of a hill for the Pacers to climb. Three-point shooting was a huge factor for Houston, as they hit 17 - of - 47, while holding the Pacers to 8 - of - 22. Free-throw shooting really hurt Indiana too, as they hit just 62 percent from the charity stripe. The Rockets (11 - 3) were led by James Harden, as he collected 26 points, five rebounds, 15 assists and two steals. Clint Capela recorded a monster double - double, totaling 20 points and 17 rebounds. Eric Gordon finished with 21 points, four assists and two steals. The Pacers (6 - 8) got another gem out of Victor Oladipo, as he tallied 28 points, four rebounds and two assists, Myles Turner amassed 13 points, eight rebounds, two assists and three blocks. Domantas Sabonis was the only other Pacer in double figures, as he provided 17 points, five rebounds and two assists off the bench. Houston host the Toronto Raptors for their next game on Tuesday. As for Indiana, they travel to face the Memphis Grizzlies for their next fixture on Wednesday,
