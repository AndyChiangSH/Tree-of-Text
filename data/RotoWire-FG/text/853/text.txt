The Toronto Raptors defeated the Milwaukee Bucks, 129 - 110, at BMO Harris Bradley Center on Friday evening. The Raptors (27 - 10) and the Bucks (20 - 17) played a very even game during the first half, and all signs indicated this one was headed for another close finish just like it happened when these two Eastern Conference rivals met earlier this week. However, the Raptors pulled away for good in the second half, as their impressive performance during the third quarter (43 - 19) pulled them away for good. Milwaukee won the fourth quarter, but it wasn't enough and Toronto secured the win rather comfortably. Surprisingly, Serge Ibaka led the Raptors offensively since he finished with 21 points and five rebounds. DeMar DeRozan added 20 points with five rebounds and four assists, while Jonas Valanciunas chipped in with 20 points and 13 rebounds. Kyle Lowry added 15 points, six boards, four dimes and two steals. The bench did a good job as well, as three players (Delon Wright (12), C.J. Miles (11) and Fred VanVleet (10)) scored in double digits. Giannis Antetokounmpo led the way for the Bucks, as the Greek forward finished with 24 points, five rebounds, five assists and two steals. Eric Bledsoe chipped in with 17 points, and Khris Middleton had 11 points. Malcolm Brodgon thrived coming off the bench, as the second - year guard scored 19 points.
