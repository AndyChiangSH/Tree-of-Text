The Miami Heat defeated the Memphis Grizzlies 107 - 82 on Monday at FedExForum. The Heat made light work of the home team Monday, outscoring them by 17 points in the final quarter and 24 points across the entire second half. And it was a complete team effort from Miami, with seven different players managing to score in double figures. Point guard Goran Dragic led the way with 19 points, five assists and five rebounds in just 25 minutes, with Josh Richardson not far behind with 17 points and five rebounds in 30 minutes. As a team, Miami shot a very impressive 56 percent from the field and a scorching 52 percent from behind the arc. Tyler Johnson and Wayne Ellington were a big part of that, as they both came off the bench to shoot a combined 8 - of - 13 from three. The Grizzlies, on the other hand, failed to find the same success from the floor, shooting just 30 percent from behind the arc and 39 percent overall. The resulting 82 points was their lowest total in seven games, and they have now lost four games in a row and 15 of their last 16. Marc Gasol led the way in defeat with 19 points and six rebounds, but he shot just 5 - of - 14 from the field. Andrew Harrison was close behind with 16 points of his own on 5 - of - 6 shooting, while Tyreke Evans was the only other Grizzlies player to hit double figures with 11 points. Up next, the Heat will head home Wednesday to take on the Trail Blazers, while the Grizzlies will travel to Washington on Wednesday to play the Wizards.
