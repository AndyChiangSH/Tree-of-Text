The Toronto Raptors defeated the Memphis Grizzlies, 101 - 86, at Air Canada Center on Sunday. A 15 - point final margin is unfair to the Grizzlies, as there were six ties and 12 lead changes before a lopsided fourth quarter. In fact, Toronto outscored Memphis, 25 - 12, in the fourth quarter and 31 - 20 in the second quarter. Defense was key for the Raptors, as they recorded 13 steals and seven blocks while winning the turnover differential, 19 - 11. They also held Memphis to just 36 percent from the field while shooting 46 percent on their end. Bench production was also critical, with the Raptors bench outscoring the Grizzlies bench, 52 - 19. The Grizzlies (18 - 34) were led by Marc Gasol, as he tallied 20 points, six rebounds, five assists and two blocks. Andrew Harrison accrued 16 points, four rebounds and three assists. No other player on the roster had more than 12 points or four assists. The Raptors (36 - 16) got a gem out of Fred VanVleet off the bench, as he collected 13 points, seven rebounds, eight assists and two steals. Delon Wright actually led the team in scoring off the bench as well, as he totaled 15 points, four rebounds, two assists and four steals. DeMar DeRozan led the starters with 13 points, two rebounds, two assists and two steals. Jons Valanciunas totaled 12 points, nine rebounds and three assists. Both teams return to action on Tuesday, with Memphis traveling to play the Atlanta Hawks and Toronto playing host to the Boston Celtics.
