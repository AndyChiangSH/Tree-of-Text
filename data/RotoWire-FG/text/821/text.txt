The Oklahoma City Thunder defeated the host Memphis Grizzlies, 102 - 101, at FedEx Forum on Saturday. This was a competitive matchup throughout, as it took a extra - session to decide a winner. In fact, there were nine ties and 12 lead changes en route to Oklahoma City's win, as neither team led by more then five points for the final 25 minutes of action. Memphis did everything they could to lose, as they went 2 - of - 6 from the free - throw line in the final 10 seconds of regulation and overtime. Transition offense was the only true advantage for the Thunder in the stat sheet, as they won the fastbreak differential, 25 - 16. The Thunder (12 - 13) got a triple - double out of Russell Westbrook, as he collected 20 points, 11 rebounds and 14 assists. Carmelo Anthony amassed 21 points, nine rebounds, two steals and two blocks. Those two combined to go just 14 - of - 49 from the field and 3 - of - 18 from three - point range though. Steven Adams finished with 21 points and nine rebounds, while Alexis Abrines accrued 20 points, four rebounds and three steals. The Grizzlies (8 - 18) were led by Tyreke Evans, as he tallied 29 points, 13 rebounds and five assists. Marc Gasol accumulated 22 points, six rebounds and six assists. Dillon Brooks provided 13 points, four rebounds and three assists. Ben McLemore led the bench with 17 points and four rebounds. Both teams return home on Monday, with Oklahoma City hosting the Charlotte Hornets and Memphis facing the Miami Heat.
