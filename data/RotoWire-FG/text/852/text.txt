The visiting New Orleans Pelicans took down the struggling Utah Jazz, 108 - 98, at Vivint Smart Home Arena on Wednesday. New Orleans (19 - 18) snapped a quick two - game losing streak with Wednesday's win. The team has had issues with depth all season, but was able to rely on its starters to take down Utah. The Pelicans' starting five combined for 90 of the team's 108 points and had 37 total rebounds. Superstar big man Anthony Davis led the way with 29 points and 15 rebounds. Davis played a team - high 40 minutes on Wednesday, helping make up for star center DeMarcus Cousins' mildly limited minutes due to foul trouble. Cousins had five fouls on the night, but managed to record a 19 - point, 11 - rebound double - double nonetheless. Utah (16 - 22), meanwhile, has lost 11 of its last 14 games. The team is still without star center Rudy Gobert (knee). On Wednesday, Utah struggled to find reliable sources of offense. The team shot just 21 percent from three - point range and reached the free - throw line a meager 17 times. The top scorer for the Jazz, rookie Donovan Mitchell, had 24 points but shot just 6 - of - 17 on the night. Two of Mitchell's buckets came in garbage time, when the rookie refused to let up his intense offensive push. Veteran Joe Johnson added another 20 points off the bench. The Jazz will visit Denver on Friday, while the Pelicans will travel to Minnesota on Saturday.
