The Charlotte Hornets defeated the Orlando Magic, 137 - 100, at Amway Center on Friday. The Hornets (35 - 45) had one of their best scoring performances of the season and even though they are solely playing for their pride, it is clear that Charlotte has some nice pieces going forward. This time, they outplayed the Magic (24 - 55) right from the start of the game, and jumped to an 18 - point lead after the first quarter. Charlotte never looked back and in fact they were so dominant that they managed to win all four quarters separatedly: 43 - 25, 29 - 24, 31 - 26 and 34 - 25. The Magic looked outmatched on both ends of the court, and Charlotte cruised their way towards an easy win. Dwight Howard has been a beast on the paint of late, and once again delivered a great performance of 16 points, 17 rebounds and four blocks, so he made his presence felt on both ends of the court, Kemba Walker had 13 points and four assists, while Marvin Williams added 18 points. The team's top scorers came from the bench, however, as Malik Monk had 26 points and eight dimes, while Frank Kaminsky had 21 points. Orlando rest some of their best players for this game, but they still had some notable performances worth highlighting. D.J. Augustin was the top scorer with 19 points in just 18 minutes. Rodney Purvis, Khem Birch and Jamel Artis had 12 points, while Mario Hezonja added 11. Wesley Iwundu finished with 10 points, and Marreese Speights added 14 off the bench.
