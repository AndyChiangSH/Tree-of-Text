The Brooklyn Nets defeated the visiting Cleveland Cavaliers, 113 - 107, at Barclay's Center on Wednesday. Brooklyn came into this matchup as a sizable favorite but Cleveland made it close late. A 31 - 20 first quarter really set the tone for the Nets, as they capped off their victory with a 33 - 22 fourth quarter. With that said, there were six ties and eight lead changes in total, as Brooklyn overcame an eight - point third quarter deficit to win. The shooting disparity was key, with the Nets shooting 47 percent from the field and the Cavaliers connecting on just 40 percent of their shots. Brooklyn did most of their damage down low, outscoring Cleveland in the paint, 58 - 38. The Cavaliers (16 - 49) were led by Kevin Love, as he tallied 24 points, 16 rebounds and four assists. Larry Nance Jr. finished with 17 points, 10 rebounds, three assists and four steals. Collin Sexton amassed 12 points, five assists and two blocks while David Nwaba led the bench with 22 points. The Nets (34 - 33) were led by D'Angelo Russell, as he accrued 25 points, four rebounds, five assists and three steals. Jarrett Allen amassed 15 points, 11 rebounds and three assists. Spencer Dinwiddie was huge off the bench, providing 28 points, four rebounds and five assists. Ed Davis led the team with 12 rebounds off the bench as well. On deck for Cleveland is a road matchup versus the Miami Heat on Friday. As for Brooklyn, they return to action on Saturday when they travel to face the Atlanta Hawks.
