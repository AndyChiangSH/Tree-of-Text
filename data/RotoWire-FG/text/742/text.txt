The host Phoenix Suns stunned the Oklahoma City Thunder on Friday, winning, 120 - 99. The Suns (23 - 57) did themselves no favors in terms of their lottery odds, as the win moved them back into a tie for second - worst record with the Los Angeles Lakers. The resounding win did produce plenty of positives for the struggling team, though, as they saw 37 points from burgeoning star Devin Booker. As a whole, Phoenix played a stellar game on the offensive end, shooting almost 52 percent from the field. The Suns reached the free - throw line 31 times on the night, making 26 of their attempts. OKC (45 - 34) saw a shocker, as the team had been rolling heading into Friday's game. Superstar point guard Russell Westbrook was seen immediately heading to a practice court to put up shots after the loss, and given his performance, it may be hard to blame him. The potential MVP had one of his worst outings of the season, shooting a dismal 6 - of - 25 from the field and turning the ball over eight times. Though Westbrook finally officially secured a triple - double average for the season in the game, he failed to record a record - setting 42nd triple - double. His poor shooting was also a large piece of OKC's offensive struggles on the night, as the team shot just 36 percent from the field. OKC will visit Denver on Sunday, while Phoenix will host Dallas.
