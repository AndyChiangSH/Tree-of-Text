The Portland Trail Blazers defeated the Los Angeles Lakers, 104 - 101, at Staples Center on Tuesday. Drama surrounding the Lakers' organization overshadowed the team's final game of the season on Tuesday, as Lakers (37 - 45) president Magic Johnson unexpectedly stepped down from his role this evening. Johnson held a press conference that lingered past the start time of the team's game to address his decision to move forward. On the court, though, the Lakers put up a tough fight against Portland (52 - 29), but the playoff - bound Blazers ultimately got the last laugh. Maurice Harkless came up in the clutch for Portland, scoring the team's last 10 points, including a game - winning three - pointer with just one second remaining. Harkless led the Trail Blazers with 26 points. He was also active on the defensive end, blocking four shots. Enes Kanter contributed a double - double for Portland in the win, finishing with 22 points and 16 rebounds. With the win, Portland sits at fourth in the Western Conference. However, a win on Wednesday coupled with a loss by Denver could move Portland into third place. The Lakers, meanwhile, saw 32 points from Kentavious Caldwell-Pope. It was the second straight game in which Caldwell-Pope tallied 32 points. Jemerrio Jones grabbed 15 rebounds, and all five Lakers starters scored in double figures in the narrow loss. Portland will visit Sacramento for its final game of the season on Wednesday.
