The Detroit Pistons defeated the host Memphis Grizzlies, 101 - 94, at FedEx Forum on Wednesday evening. The contest got out to a competitive start, with the visitors emerging with a two - point lead after one quarter. The Grizzlies took the upper hand through the second, as they outscored the Pistons by six in the quarter, to take a 55 - 51 lead by halftime. However, the Pistons flipped the switch in the third quarter, where they held the Grizzlies to Just 12 points, and pulled ahead to take a 72 - 67 lead into the fourth. The Pistons maintained the upper hand through the fourth and held on to end a three - game slide. Blake Griffin led the Pistons with 26 points, eight rebounds, seven assists and two steals, in 38 minutes. Reggie Jackson followed up with 16 points, seven rebounds, five assists, two blocks and one steal, while Luke Kennard and Reggie Bullock added 13 points each. The Pistons shot 43 percent from the field, including 16 - of - 40 from long range. Meanwhile, Jaren Jackson Jr. led the Grizzlies with 26 points, 10 rebounds and two assists, in 35 minutes. Kyle Anderson followed up with 15 points, five rebounds, one assist and one block, while Marc Gasol added 11 points, seven rebounds, three assists and two blocks. The Grizzlies shot 47 percent from the field, including 4 - of - 18 from deep. The Grizzlies (18 - 19) will look to avoid a fourth straight loss as they play host to the Nets on Friday. The Pistons (17 - 19) play host to the Jazz on Saturday.
