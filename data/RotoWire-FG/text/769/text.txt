The Detroit Pistons defeated the visiting Minnesota Timberwolves, 122 - 101, at Little Ceasars Arena on Wednesday. This was expected to be quite a battle between two 2 - 2 teams, and it showed right from tip - off. The game was extremely close in the first quarter and the Timberwolves were actually in front, 26 - 23, after the first 12 minutes. But Detroit (3 - 2) responded with a 40 - 18 win during the second quarter, and they never looked back. Despite the fact Minnesota (2 - 3) kept things close in the second half losing 59 - 57 over that span, Detroit did all the damage in the second quarter and moved over.500 with a convincing win in front of their home crowd. The Pistons had huge contributions all across the board in this game. Tobias Harris was the undisputed star of the game for Detroit, as his 34 - point performance matched a career - high for the small forward. However, he wasn't alone in Detroit's impressive offensive output. Avery Bradley added 20 points, Andre Drummond contributed with a double - double of 15 points and 14 rebounds, and Reggie Jackson chipped in with 10 points. A total of six Detroit players scored in double figures, including all five members of the starting lineup. On the other hand, the Timberwolves were missing Jimmy Butler (illness) and could not mask the absence of their star forward. Karl-Anthony Towns had 23 points with 10 rebounds and Andrew Wiggins added 21 points of his own, but it was not enough for a Minnesota squad that lost their second consecutive game. They only shot 44 percent from the field, and were out - rebounded, 47 - 37. That's usually not a recipe for success.
