# Jonatan Christie vs Kidambi Srikanth gets underway

Indonesia’s Jonatan Christie, the third seed, is up against the fifth seed Kidambi Srikanth from India in the first men’s singles semi-finals.

Jonatan had beaten Kidambi in three games at the Swiss Open last month. Will we have the same result today? Only time will tell.

The players are making their way to the court now.

# Srikanth leads

Both Jonatan and Kidambi exchange points at the start before the latter sends a smash down the line and another cross court shot to open the gap.

Now Jonatan pockets a series of points aided by his brilliant court presence. But a few unforced errors from him help Kidambi soar to an 11-8 lead at the first break.

# Anybody's game

Jonatan wins three points to cancel Kidambi’s lead post the break. But the unforced errors are not helping Jonatan’s cause.

The world silver medallist from India is winning points aplenty but Jonatan’s defence is making things difficult for Kidambi.

The Indonesian is now level again at 17-17.

This can go either way.

# Jonatan pulls through

A delightful cross net drop shot from Kidambi, but he fumbles with a wide smash in the next point and we are equal again.

Jonatan wins two points to set up his game point, which Kidambi saves. But the Indonesian takes the next to win the opener.

He did not lead for much of the match but came back strongly towards the end.

Score: Jonatan Christie 21-19 Kidambi Srikanth

# Jonatan Christie inching towards victory

Jonatan ups the pace in the second game and goes up 3-0 before netting one and hitting the next one out.

Now Kidambi is the one giving away points through unforced errors, helping Jonatan go up 7-3.

But the former world No. 1 from India produces a sublime drive and a smash to make it 7-6.

That is some power in Jonatan’s smashes. Two points on the bounce which Kidambi can’t return.

Jonatan goes up 11-8 in Game 2.

# Jonatan Christie beats Kidambi Srikanth

Kidambi Srikanth has shifted gears after the break, reducing the lead and even going ahead at 14-13.

Jonatan, however, is not done yet. He is constructing those points nicely in the long rallies and is now leading again 17-14.

The Indonesian has swung the game in his favour, winning five points in a row. Just two points away from victory.

Another intense rally ensues but Jonatan gives a lifeline to Kidambi by smashing it outside.

That lifeline didn’t last long though as Jonatan is through to the final.

Score: Jonatan Christie 21-19, 21-16 Kidambi Srikanth
