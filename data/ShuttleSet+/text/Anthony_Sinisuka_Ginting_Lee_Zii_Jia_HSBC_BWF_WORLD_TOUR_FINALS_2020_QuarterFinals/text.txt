In the quarterfinals of the HSBC BWF World Tour Finals 2020, Indonesian badminton player Anthony Sinisuka Ginting faced Malaysian player Lee Zii Jia in a highly anticipated match. This event took place during a challenging year for sports due to the COVID-19 pandemic, with the tournament being held in Bangkok, Thailand, from January 27 to February 1, 2021, after being rescheduled from its original dates.

First Game: Ginting started strong, showcasing his trademark speed and precision. He quickly established a lead with aggressive play, forcing Lee into defensive positions. The first game concluded with Ginting winning decisively.

Second Game: Lee Zii Jia adjusted his strategy, focusing on maintaining longer rallies and utilizing his height advantage to execute effective net play. This shift allowed him to take control of the game and ultimately secure a win in the second game.

Deciding Game: The final game was intense, with both players displaying their skills and resilience. Ginting's experience shone through as he fought back from a deficit. However, Lee's consistency and tactical changes proved crucial as he edged out Ginting in a nail-biting finish.

Lee Zii Jia emerged victorious with a scoreline of 18-21, 21-16, 21-19, advancing to the semifinals of the tournament. This victory not only highlighted Lee's potential as an emerging star in badminton but also marked a significant moment in his career against a top competitor like Ginting.
