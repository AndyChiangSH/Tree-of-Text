On January 15, 2021, at the YONEX Thailand Open, Hong Kong's Ng Ka Long Angus faced compatriot Lee Cheuk Yiu in the men's singles quarterfinals. Ng secured a straight-sets victory, 21-17, 21-12, advancing to the semifinals.

In the first game, Ng trailed 14-17 but rallied to win seven consecutive points, taking the game 21-17. He maintained momentum in the second game, leading to a 21-12 finish. 

Post-match, Ng expressed satisfaction with his performance, noting improved adaptation to the venue's conditions. He highlighted the first game's comeback as pivotal and credited earlier matches for enhancing his form. 

This win set up a semifinal clash with Taiwan's Chou Tien-chen, a familiar adversary for Ng.
