KUALA LUMPUR: After years of struggle to make it to the badminton summit of men's singles, Indonesia's Chico Aura Dwi Wardayo stands on the brink of a career breakthrough.

The 24-year-old reached the Malaysia Masters final after prevailing 20-22, 23-21, 21-19 against China's Lu Guang Zu in a thrilling one hour and 28 minutes battle at Axiata Arena today.

Chico is just one step away from a maiden Super 500 title. The world No 45 will battle Hong Kong's Angus Ng in the final tomorrow. Angus defeated India's H.S. Prannoy 17-21, 21-9, 21-17 in the semi-final.

"Having made it this far, I definitely want to go all the way to win the title," said Chico, who will appear in only his second final on the World Tour after finishing second at last year's Spain Masters Super 300.
