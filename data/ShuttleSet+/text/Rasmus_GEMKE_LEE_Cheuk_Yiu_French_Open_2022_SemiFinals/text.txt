Rasmus Gemke has played his way to the final at the French Open, and that after a great 21-18, 21-14 victory over Lee Cheuk Yiu. – It was a really good performance, says national coach Kenneth Jonassen.

The 25-year-old Rasmus Gemke from Aarhus is ready for his first Super 750 final since the Denmark Open in 2020. This is clear after a solid effort and victory over Lee Cheuk Yiu from Hong Kong.

– It was a really good performance. It was full of focus and clarity on how to solve his opponent. The fact that he wins as surely as 21-18, 21-14 I think characterizes that he is in a really good place and heading in the right direction. He is disciplined, believes in himself, and he has a constructive mindset, and then he can be in the physical discomfort. Both were well worn, but he solves that really, really nicely today. So I’m happy and proud, of course, says Kenneth Jonassen.

Gemke got off to a terrific start in the first set, leading 8-0. Lee Cheuk Yiu came close to stealing the set at the end of the set. But Rasmus Gemke ended up taking the set 21-18 after four set points.

One of the reasons why it was close in the first set must also be seen in the light of the fact that Rasmus Gemke’s shoe split, which meant that it had to be taped. But he also handled that situation well, according to Kenneth Jonassen.

– He has an accident with his shoe, which affects him a bit, so he is not at ease at the end of the first set and large parts of the second set, but he handles it well. It affects him, of course, but he maintains his focus on tactics. He can be really, really proud of this victory, says Kenneth Jonassen.

From the stay in the second set, Lee Cheuk Yiu began to lean over his racket as if it were a cane when the duels were over. It was obvious that he was physically marked.

Gemke took advantage of that to drive home the victory in the end, and it probably hurts him according to Jonassen.

– Rasmus (Gemke, ed.) is right now a product of his own professionalism over the past many years, but especially the past five to six weeks, when he has been playing badminton in just the right way. He has been detailed and quality conscious in his daily training. He will probably also mention himself that the body is the most important thing to him, so he can be very proud of that part too, says Kenneth Jonassen.

In his most recent Super 750 final, Rasmus Gemke met and lost to Anders Antonsen. Now he gets the chance again, and it may be against another Dane, namely Viktor Axelsen, who will play his semi-final later today against Kodai Naraoka from Japan.

The head coach has no doubts about how the semi-final outcome will end.

– I hope for a Danish final, so that we get a title for Denmark, says Jonassen.

The men’s singles final at the French Open can be seen tomorrow on TV 2 SPORT. The program for the final day has not yet been determined.
