SEATTLE--Mitch Haniger understands his time batting in the cleanup spot of Seattle's batting order is likely ending soon.
No better way to finish up a stint filling in for Nelson Cruz than with a game-deciding home run.
Haniger hit a three - run homer and Daniel Vogelbach added a two - run shot--both with two outs in the seventh inning--rallying the Mariners to a 7 - 4 win over the Oakland Athletics on Friday night.
Seattle took advantage of a breakdown by Oakland's bullpen for five runs in the seventh. Yusmeiro Petit retired the first two batters before Jean Segura reached on a single with two outs. Lefty specialist Danny Coulombe (0 - 1) walked Robinson Cano, and Chris Hatcher was summoned to face Haniger.
After evening the count at 2 - 2, Hatcher and catcher Jonathan Lucroy briefly met at the mound to discuss the next pitch to Haniger. Their decision was the wrong one as Haniger clubbed his third homer of the season.
Haniger has been the No. 4 hitter in Seattle's lineup since Cruz went on the disabled list with a sprained ankle the first weekend of the season. Cruz is expected back as early as Saturday, meaning Haniger can drop deeper into Seattle's lineup.
Seattle's big inning wasn't done as Kyle Seager followed with a single and Vogelbach turned on the first pitch from Hatcher for his first career homer.
It was the second time in the young season the Mariners put up five runs in an inning.
Khris Davis hit two home runs for Oakland, a two - run shot in the first and a solo homer in the eighth inning. Matt Chapman added a solo homer in the sixth inning, his fifth of the season, off Seattle starter Mike Leake to snap a 2-all tie.
